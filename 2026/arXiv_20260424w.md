## Executive view

This week is strong in exactly the areas the profile cares about: high-redshift gas, overdense environments, early black holes, and the physical interpretation of JWST/ALMA tracers. The single highest-priority result is [2604.21218](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21218) Yongda Zhu, which uses JWST/NIRSpec absorption to show that metal-enriched, kinematically disturbed gas already surrounded luminous galaxies at `z=7.2-9.3`. The best environment paper is [2604.21007](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21007) Takumi Kakimoto, which spectroscopically confirms massive quiescent galaxies in `z=3.71` and `z=4.01` overdensities and connects synchronized quenching to large-scale structure and AGN feedback.

The week also gives a coherent chain for early-galaxy interpretation. [2604.21516](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21516) Zuyi Chen constrains a `z=9.3` super-luminous weak-line galaxy with ultra-deep JWST spectroscopy and favors a recent star-formation downturn plus a small ionized bubble. [2604.21493](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21493) Rui Marques-Chaves points to very massive stars in `z~8.7` galaxies. [2604.19075](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19075) Junehyoung Jeon frames the He II source Hebe as a Pop III versus SMBH test. [2604.20966](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20966) Pratika Dayal connects the broader JWST over-massive-BH problem to light seeds, heavy seeds, and primordial black holes.

On the CGM/IGM and gas-cycle side, [2604.16751](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16751) Marie Wisz and [2604.16294](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16294) Nicolas Ledos are the cleanest complements to Zhu: one gives a `z~5` DLA metallicity break, and the other models how quasar radiation changes cold CGM cloud survival and Lyalpha output. [2604.18522](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18522) Yang Sun adds a JWST/NIRSpec census of Na D neutral outflows at `0.6<z<4`.

The tracer-physics cluster is unusually useful. [2604.18730](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18730) Benedetta Casavecchia and [2604.18731](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18731) Aniket Bhagwat show that `[O III]` and `[C II]` emission in first galaxies are feedback-dependent rather than simple luminosity or gas-mass tracers. [2604.19928](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19928) Ryusei R. Kano turns the `z~8` dust-budget problem into an ISM-structure problem involving dense molecular clumps. [2604.18503](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18503) Jean-Baptiste Jolly and [2604.18504](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18504) Jianhang Chen make the cosmic-noon gas-transport connection with NOEMA3D.

## Reionization-era gas, metals, and first galaxies

- [2604.21218](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21218) Yongda Zhu et al. `Early metal-enriched baryon cycling before the midpoint of cosmic reionization`
  - **Question:** was metal-enriched gas already cycling through galaxy-associated outflows before reionization was halfway complete?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec: rest-frame UV spectra of three galaxies at `z=7.2-9.3`
    - Absorption tracers: O I, Si II, C II, Si IV, and C IV
    - Systemic reference: nebular emission-line redshifts for velocity-offset measurements
  - **Method:**
    - Detect rest-UV metal absorption in individual high-redshift galaxies
    - Compare absorption velocities with systemic redshifts from nebular lines
    - Use ionization-state coexistence and equivalent-width ratios to infer multiphase gas structure
    - Interpret the shared velocity structure as outflowing or kinematically disturbed galaxy-associated gas
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Metal absorption is detected in all three galaxies, showing that enriched gas already surrounded luminous galaxies at `z=7.2-9.3`.
    - The detected transitions span neutral, low-ionization, and high-ionization phases, so the gas is multiphase rather than a single narrow component.
    - Velocity offsets are typically `|Delta v| ~ 50-250 km/s` and are mostly blueshifted relative to systemic redshift, consistent with outflowing or disturbed baryon-cycle gas.
    - The result requires rapid early metal production and redistribution within the first several hundred Myr.
  - **Position:** the week's strongest direct bridge between first galaxies and CGM/IGM physics

- [2604.21516](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21516) Zuyi Chen et al. `SPURS: Bursty Star Formation in an Extremely Luminous Weak Emission Line Galaxy at z=9.3`
  - **Question:** why does a super-luminous `z=9.3` galaxy have extremely weak emission lines despite its large stellar mass and UV luminosity?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/SPURS: ultra-deep `29 hr` rest-frame UV spectroscopy
    - Target galaxy: `z=9.3`, `M_UV=-21.66`, stellar mass `1.6 x 10^9 Msun`
    - Spectral diagnostics: Hbeta, stellar wind features, rest-optical line ratios, interstellar absorption, and Lyalpha damping-wing shape
  - **Method:**
    - Measure weak nebular emission and stellar-wind features in deep grating spectroscopy
    - Infer metallicity from stellar and nebular diagnostics
    - Use interstellar absorption to test neutral-gas covering fraction and outflow velocity
    - Model recent star-formation history and the IGM damping wing
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The galaxy has very weak Hbeta, with `EW ~ 25 Angstrom`, but is already significantly enriched at `0.4-0.7 Zsun`.
    - Interstellar absorption shows outflows at about `v ~ -161 km/s` and a large neutral-gas covering fraction, disfavoring a pure high-escape-fraction explanation.
    - A `10-20 Myr` burst followed by a downturn within the last `10 Myr` explains the Balmer break, weak lines, and wind signatures.
    - The smooth Lyalpha break implies a small ionized bubble of `0.29^{+0.11}_{-0.09} pMpc` and a high neutral fraction of `x_HI = 0.81^{+0.14}_{-0.21}`.
  - **Position:** one of the best weekly papers for connecting bursty SFHs, gas covering fraction, and reionization topology in a single `z>9` object

- [2604.21493](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21493) Rui Marques-Chaves et al. `Signatures of Very Massive Stars in the Epoch of Reionization`
  - **Question:** are very massive stars required to explain the UV spectra of bright `z~8.7` galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec: ultra-deep `20-30 hr` rest-frame UV spectra
    - Targets: CEERS-1019 and CEERS-1025 at `z~8.7`
    - Spectral diagnostics: N V `1240`, C IV `1550`, and broad He II `1640`
  - **Method:**
    - Compare the observed UV wind profiles with synthetic stellar population models
    - Fit models with and without very massive stars at `Z_star ~ 0.1 Zsun`
    - Use information criteria to compare model quality
    - Infer stellar ages and ionizing photon production efficiencies
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Models including very massive stars fit the UV wind features far better than models without them, with `Delta AIC` and `Delta BIC > 70`.
    - The spectra show strong P-Cygni N V and C IV plus broad He II with `EW ~ 2-4 Angstrom`.
    - Best-fit ages are about `1.5-2.0 Myr`.
    - The inferred ionizing photon production efficiency is high, with `log xi_ion >= 25.8`.
  - **Position:** a high-z stellar-population result that matters for both reionization photon budgets and early chemical enrichment

- [2604.17963](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17963) Kimi C. Kreilgaard et al. `BEACON: JWST NIRCam Pure-parallel Imaging Survey. III. Constraints on the UV LF and the Clustering of z~7-14 Galaxies`
  - **Question:** what do many independent JWST pure-parallel sightlines imply for UV luminosity functions and bright-galaxy clustering over `z~7-14`?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRCam BEACON DR2: `~400 arcmin^2` across `36` independent sightlines
    - Dropout sample: `164` candidates over `7<z<12`
    - Spectroscopic overlap: `11` pointings with public JWST spectroscopy
  - **Method:**
    - Select F090W, F115W, and F150W dropout candidates
    - Build UV luminosity functions at `z~7.5`, `z~10`, and higher-redshift limits
    - Use spectroscopic-overlap fields to assess sample purity
    - Measure overdensities around bright galaxies and compare with semi-numerical simulations
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The candidate set contains `150` F090W dropouts, `14` F115W dropouts, and no robust F150W dropouts.
    - The spectroscopy-overlap subset gives `100%` purity for matched candidates.
    - At `z~10`, the BEACON LF is lower than many photometric JWST estimates but agrees better with spectroscopic constraints.
    - Bright galaxies with `M_UV<-20.5` at `7<z<10` are significantly clustered, and fields containing them are about `~3x` more likely to be overdense than the survey average.
  - **Position:** the week's cleanest survey-scale bridge between reionization-era luminosity functions and large-scale structure

## Protoclusters, quenching, and environmental growth

- [2604.21007](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21007) Takumi Kakimoto et al. `DeepDive: Simultaneous Formation of Massive Quiescent Galaxies in High-Redshift Galaxy Proto-clusters`
  - **Question:** do massive quiescent galaxies in high-redshift overdensities form and quench in a synchronized way?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec DeepDive plus archival Dawn JWST Archive spectra
    - Confirmed systems: three quiescent galaxies around Jekyll and Hyde at `z=3.71` and two around SXDS-27434 at `z=4.01`
    - Photometric-redshift environment maps: COSMOS and SXDS fields
    - Simulation comparison: Illustris TNG300 overdensity analogue at `z=3.71`
  - **Method:**
    - Spectroscopically confirm quiescent galaxies near massive high-z systems
    - Measure local galaxy number-density excess with photometric redshifts
    - Fit star-formation histories and quenching epochs
    - Use emission-line diagnostics to identify AGN-driven line emission
    - Compare the observed overdensity to TNG300 structures
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Both target fields are overdense, with galaxy number densities about `3x` the field average.
    - The quiescent galaxies have broadly consistent formation and quenching epochs, supporting synchronized evolution.
    - JWST spectra reveal broad Halpha from SXDS-27434 and faint AGN-like emission lines from three other quiescent galaxies.
    - A TNG300 overdensity at `z=3.71` reproduces similar member quiescent galaxies and similar quenching epochs.
  - **Position:** the most important high-z environment paper this week because it connects overdensity, massive QG assembly, and AGN-linked quenching

- [2604.19651](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19651) Mary C. Knowlton et al. `A Possible Protocluster of Galaxies Serendipitously Discovered in the Field of an Intermediate-Redshift Post-starburst Galaxy`
  - **Question:** do ALMA continuum detections near a `z~0.7` post-starburst field reveal a background protocluster candidate?
  - **Data:**
    - ALMA `870 um` and `2 mm` observations of the SDSSJ0909-0108 field
    - Source sample: `6` submillimeter galaxies within a `35 arcsec` region
    - Ancillary redshift status: no source at the post-starburst redshift and `4` sources without spectroscopic redshifts
  - **Method:**
    - Identify compact dust-emitting sources in ALMA maps
    - Use flux ratios to assess whether emission is dust dominated
    - Compare the source surface density with expectations for random `870 um` detections
    - Flag the field for optical and ALMA redshift follow-up
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The field contains a compact overdensity of `6` dust-emitting galaxies.
    - The sources are unlikely to be associated with the foreground `z~0.7` post-starburst galaxy.
    - The configuration makes a background protocluster plausible but still unconfirmed.
    - Spectroscopic or line-scan redshifts are required before treating the system as a physical structure.
  - **Position:** lower confidence than Kakimoto, but still useful as a dusty-overdensity follow-up alert

- [2604.20411](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20411) Kaimin He et al. `The Evolution of the SFR-M_* relation at 0.1<z<4: Environmental and Morphological Dependencies`
  - **Question:** how do environment and morphology separately affect the star-forming main sequence from `z=0.1` to `4`?
  - **Data:**
    - COSMOS2020 mass-complete sample: approximately `290,000` galaxies
    - Redshift range: `0.1 < z < 4`
    - Structural split: early-type, spiral, and irregular morphologies
  - **Method:**
    - Measure the SFR-`M_*` relation across redshift
    - Compare high- and low-density environments
    - Separate full-sample trends from star-forming-only trends
    - Contrast environmental effects with morphology-dependent offsets
  - **Conclusion:**
    - High-density environments suppress SFR at `z<1`, especially at the high-mass end, in the full population.
    - Within star-forming galaxies alone, no strong environmental effect is seen across the full redshift range.
    - Morphology has a stronger effect than environment at `z<2`.
    - The high-mass downturn of the SFR-`M_*` relation is interpreted as morphology-linked quenching rather than a pure environment trend.
  - **Position:** broad demographic context for the more direct high-z protocluster papers

## CGM, IGM, and neutral-gas cycling

- [2604.16751](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16751) Marie Wisz et al. `The Qz5 Survey (II): Metallicity Evolution of Damped Lyalpha Systems Out to z~5`
  - **Question:** does the neutral gas metallicity traced by DLAs decline smoothly to `z~5`, or is there a sharper transition near the end of reionization?
  - **Data:**
    - VLT/X-shooter and Keck/HIRES: spectroscopy for newly detected `z>4.7` DLAs
    - Literature DLA compilation: metallicity-unbiased systems over `0<z<5.5`
  - **Method:**
    - Add `5` newly detected high-redshift DLAs without metallicity pre-selection
    - Correct Fe abundances for dust depletion and alpha enhancement
    - Fit alpha-element metallicity evolution as a function of redshift
    - Compare the highest-redshift bin with the trend below `z<4.7`
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Neutral gas metallicity follows a slope of about `-0.22 +/- 0.05 dex` per unit redshift below the highest-redshift bin.
    - The highest-redshift bin has `N_HI`-weighted mean metallicity `<Z> = -2.00`, offset from the lower-redshift trend by `4.4 sigma`.
    - The high-z DLA metallicity distribution differs from the lower-redshift comparison at about `2.4 sigma`.
    - A sharp decline near `z~5` may trace delayed CGM enrichment or the end of reionization.
  - **Position:** pairs naturally with [2604.21218](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21218) Yongda Zhu: DLAs trace neutral reservoirs, while JWST absorption traces galaxy-associated enriched gas

- [2604.16294](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16294) Nicolas Ledos et al. `The hydrodynamical response of cold circumgalactic clouds to quasar radiation`
  - **Question:** how does quasar ionizing radiation change the structure, survival, and Lyalpha output of cold `10^4 K` circumgalactic clouds?
  - **Data:**
    - Analytical cloud-response framework: optically thin, radiation-shielded, and rocket-effect regimes
    - Radiation-hydrodynamic simulations: static cold CGM clouds exposed to quasar EUV radiation fields
    - Lyalpha-emission calculations: recombination and fluorescent components
  - **Method:**
    - Solve for the ionization and dynamical response of cold clouds under different quasar fluxes
    - Classify behavior into optically thin, shielded, and rocket-effect regimes
    - Track how expansion and acceleration modify emitted Lyalpha luminosity
    - Compare bright and faint quasar cases through Lyman-limit specific luminosity
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Hydrodynamics changes the observable Lyalpha output once radiation is strong enough to drive a rocket effect.
    - In the rocket-effect regime, Lyalpha can become up to `~10x` brighter than in the optically thin case.
    - Unless a cloud remains strongly shielded, about `~50-60%` of Lyalpha luminosity comes from recombination.
    - Bright quasars with `L_nu,LL ~ 10^31.6 erg s^-1 Hz^-1` likely ionize most cold CGM clouds completely.
  - **Position:** a theory-side complement to quasar-environment and extended-Lyalpha observations

- [2604.18522](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18522) Yang Sun et al. `A Census of Na D-traced neutral ISM and outflows at 0.6<z<4`
  - **Question:** how common are neutral Na D absorption and Na D-traced outflows in galaxies across `0.6<z<4`, and what drives those outflows?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec medium-resolution grating spectroscopy: `309` galaxies over `0.6<z<4`
    - Surveys: SMILES, JADES, Blue Jay, and Aurora
    - Spectral tracer: Na D `5890, 5896 Angstrom` absorption after stellar-continuum subtraction
  - **Method:**
    - Subtract stellar continua from NIRSpec spectra
    - Fit the Na D doublet to identify neutral ISM absorption
    - Separate systemic neutral gas from outflowing components
    - Compare outflow incidence with mass, star-formation state, age indicators, and AGN fraction
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Neutral ISM absorption is detected in `76/309` galaxies.
    - Massive galaxies dominate detections, with `85%` above `log(M_*/Msun)>10`.
    - Na D outflows are identified in `26` galaxies.
    - Star-forming galaxies show SFR-linked outflows, while quiescent galaxies often require an AGN energy source.
  - **Position:** the week's best direct JWST census of neutral outflows near cosmic noon

## LRDs, early black holes, and compact-source interpretation

- [2604.19988](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19988) Devesh Nandal et al. `Pulsational mass loss from supermassive stars creates the compact shells of Little Red Dots`
  - **Question:** can late pulsational mass loss from supermassive stars create the dense cocoon-like shells inferred around little red dots?
  - **Data:**
    - GENEC stellar-evolution models: five accreting supermassive-star models with masses of order `10^5 Msun`
    - Metallicity range: Pop III to `10^-2 Zsun`
    - Pulsation and GR-instability calculations: post-accretion late evolutionary stages
  - **Method:**
    - Evolve accreting supermassive stars through post-accretion phases
    - Calculate strange-mode pulsation ejection episodes
    - Track shell masses, durations, and radii
    - Compare ejecta properties with compact shells implied by LRD spectra
  - **Conclusion:**
    - In the clearest LRD analogue at `Z = 10^-2 Zsun`, four late ejection episodes last `41-282 yr` and eject `10-348 Msun` each.
    - The total late mass loss is `(4.8-10) x 10^2 Msun`, with the final episode contributing about `~73%`.
    - The final episode can leave an optically thick shell extending to about `0.4 pc`.
    - The final ejecta are nitrogen rich, with `log(N/O) ~ 0.13` and `log(C/O) ~ -0.23`.
  - **Position:** a direct LRD-physics paper that links compact shells to supermassive-star evolution and heavy black-hole seeds

- [2604.20966](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20966) Pratika Dayal `Light, heavy, primordial: exploring the diversity of black hole seeding and growth mechanisms in the JWST era`
  - **Question:** which early black-hole seeding channels can reproduce JWST-era over-massive black holes, host stellar masses, and low metallicities?
  - **Data:**
    - DELPHI semi-analytic model: light and heavy astrophysical seed populations
    - PHANES analytic model: primordial black-hole seed populations
    - JWST comparison space: `z~5-10` black-hole masses, stellar masses, metallicities, luminosities, and host halo masses
  - **Method:**
    - Compare light seeds of about `100 Msun`, heavy seeds of `10^3-10^5 Msun`, and PBHs over `10^0.5-10^6 Msun`
    - Model Eddington-limited and super-Eddington growth where relevant
    - Compare predicted BH-host scaling relations with observed high-z candidates
    - Identify halo and clustering signatures that distinguish seed families
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Eddington-limited accretion onto light seeds is the only tested model ruled out by the current comparison.
    - High `M_BH/M_*` ratios of `0.3-1` can be reproduced by PBHs and heavy seeds with super-Eddington growth.
    - PBHs and Eddington-limited heavy seeds can reproduce high black-hole masses, host stellar masses, and very low metallicities of `Z <= 0.01 Zsun`.
    - `z~7` systems with `M_BH/M_* > 0.1`, `L_bol ~ 10^44-10^46 erg/s`, and halo masses `10^9-10^11 Msun` could discriminate PBH seeding through clustering.
  - **Position:** a useful framework for connecting LRD/obese-BH observations to survey-scale clustering tests

- [2604.19075](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19075) Junehyoung Jeon et al. `What is Powering the Enigmatic He II Emitter Hebe: The First Stars or Black Holes?`
  - **Question:** is the strong He II emitter Hebe near GN-z11 powered by Pop III stars or an accreting SMBH?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST spectroscopy: strong He II `1640` emission in a clump near GN-z11 with metallicity upper limits
    - Cosmological simulations: Pop III star-cluster formation constraints
    - SMBH continuum modeling: ionizing rates and recombination-line luminosities
  - **Method:**
    - Compare inferred Pop III stellar masses with simulated Pop III cluster formation
    - Model SMBH ionizing continua
    - Compare H I and He II recombination-line luminosities with observations
    - Evaluate which power source is more plausible within standard first-star formation
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Pop III stellar masses of a few `10^5 Msun` can plausibly form at Hebe's location in the simulations.
    - SMBH models can produce broadly compatible ionizing rates and recombination luminosities.
    - A massive Pop III star cluster remains the preferred interpretation, but it sits near the upper limit allowed by standard first-star formation.
  - **Position:** a targeted comparison case for separating pristine stars from black-hole-powered hard ionization in JWST sources

## Tracer physics, gas transport, and feedback

- [2604.18730](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18730) Benedetta Casavecchia et al. `New constraints on stellar feedback through [O III] emission: interpreting ALMA and JWST observations with SPICE simulations`
  - **Question:** how does stellar feedback change `[O III]` emission from high-redshift star-forming galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - SPICE radiation-hydrodynamical simulations: galaxies relevant to `z>6` ALMA and JWST line detections
    - Emission modeling: sub-grid `[O III]` line-emission post-processing
    - Feedback variants: bursty supernova, smooth supernova, and hypernova prescriptions
  - **Method:**
    - Post-process simulated galaxies with `[O III]` emission modeling
    - Compare metal enrichment, neutral gas fraction, ionized-halo morphology, and `[O III]` output
    - Measure mass-metallicity and neutral-gas-fraction trends against `[O III]` luminosity
    - Compare feedback prescriptions through predicted luminosity functions and kinematics
  - **Conclusion:**
    - `[O III]` emission mainly traces gas that is both shock-heated and radiatively ionized.
    - Bursty feedback ionizes gas efficiently but enriches galaxies less effectively by `z=5`.
    - Bursty and hypernova models both suppress the `[O III]` luminosity function relative to smoother feedback.
    - Faint `[O III]` observations are especially diagnostic because feedback differences are strongest below the bright end.
  - **Position:** a practical warning that `[O III]` luminosity and morphology are feedback diagnostics, not simple SFR tracers

- [2604.18731](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18731) Aniket Bhagwat et al. `Stellar feedback SPICEs up [C II] emission in the first galaxies`
  - **Question:** how reliably does `[C II]` trace star formation, outflows, and disk settling in `z>=5` galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - SPICE simulations: first-galaxy populations under bursty and continuous feedback
    - Synthetic `[C II] 158 um` emission: spatial and spectral line diagnostics
    - Comparison context: ALMA observations of `z>5` galaxies
  - **Method:**
    - Model `[C II]` luminosity, morphology, and line profiles under different feedback prescriptions
    - Compare `[C II]` sizes with rest-frame UV sizes
    - Track outflowing and inflowing gas components
    - Use gas kinematics and `V/sigma` to diagnose disk settling
  - **Conclusion:**
    - `[C II]` luminosity correlates with SFR, but bursty feedback lowers `[C II]` at fixed SFR and increases scatter.
    - `[C II]` emission is typically `~2-4x` more extended than the rest-frame UV continuum.
    - Outflows can reach `~10 Msun/yr`, but net mass flux in `[C II]`-bright galaxies remains inflow dominated.
    - `[C II]` line profiles can overestimate true cold-gas outflow velocities by factors of `2-3`.
  - **Position:** one of the most useful weekly papers for interpreting ALMA line profiles in first galaxies

- [2604.19928](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19928) Ryusei R. Kano et al. `Resolving the Dust Budget Crisis at z ~ 8 with Optically Thick, High-Density Molecular Clumps: MACS0416_Y1`
  - **Question:** can extremely dense cold neutral clumps solve the dust-budget crisis in the `z=8.312` galaxy MACS0416_Y1?
  - **Data:**
    - Target galaxy: MACS0416_Y1 at `z=8.312`
    - SED constraints: observed UV-to-FIR emission, including ALMA Band 9 continuum
    - Grain-size-resolved dust model: multi-temperature dust-population predictions
  - **Method:**
    - Treat cold neutral medium density as a free parameter in the dust-evolution model
    - Fit the observed UV-to-FIR SED
    - Track UV trapping, dust growth, and supernova dust destruction in dense clumps
    - Resolve the infrared output by grain-size population
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Reproducing the SED requires `n_H,CNM ~ 7.5 x 10^3 cm^-3`.
    - Dense clumps increase UV trapping, accelerate dust processing, and reduce supernova dust destruction.
    - Intermediate-size grains with `a = 0.01-0.1 um` contribute about `89%` of luminosity near the SED peak and in ALMA Band 9.
    - The dominant warm dust has characteristic temperatures of about `~70 K`.
  - **Position:** turns early dust abundance into an ISM-structure and radiative-transfer problem

- [2604.18503](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18503) Jean-Baptiste Jolly et al. `NOEMA3D: Resolving radial gas flows in disk galaxies at z~1.1-1.6 with high-resolution CO observations`

    - IRAM-NOEMA: deep CO observations, with `>=20 hr` on source per target
    - Galaxy sample: `10` massive main-sequence galaxies at `z~1.1-1.6`
    - JWST imaging: stellar and morphological context
  - **Method:**
    - Resolve molecular-gas kinematics and morphology on kiloparsec scales
    - Model axisymmetric disk rotation with DysmalPy
    - Measure residual velocity fields after subtracting circular rotation
    - Interpret residuals as in-plane radial flows linked to bars and spiral structure
  - **Conclusion:**
    - All galaxies show ordered rotation with median intrinsic turbulence `sigma_0~32 +/- 10 km/s`.
    - The median dynamical ratio is `V_c/sigma_0~8.6 +/- 2.9`.
    - Non-circular motions reach `~50-100 km/s`.
    - Molecular inflow rates are of order the SFR, with typical net inflow around `~50 Msun/yr`.
  - **Position:** the week's clearest cosmic-noon gas-transport paper
