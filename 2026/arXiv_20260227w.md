## Executive view

This is one of the better ecosystem and survey-design weeks in the current set. The clearest direct match to your core program is [2602.22004](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22004) Jiang-Tao Li, which lays out a concrete Euclid/CSST/JPCam/JUST strategy for linking galaxy structure, CGM emission, and foreground-IGM mapping over `~20 deg^2` at cosmic noon.

The gas-exchange and halo-structure side is also strong. [2602.20241](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20241) Wuji Wang compares ISM and CGM abundances at `4<z<6` with JWST plus ALMA, while [2603.00336](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00336) Dylan L. Jow shows that FRB scattering statistics should be sensitive to whether cool halo gas is volumetric, intermittent, filamentary, or sheet-like.

On the compact high-z side, the week is useful mainly because the papers separate different physical interpretations. [2603.00232](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00232) Robert G. Pascalau resolves metal-poor inflowing gas in a `z=10.17` system, while [2602.20247](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20247) Pablo G. Pérez-González and [2602.22386](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22386) Piero Madau offer two different LRD frameworks: heterogeneous dense-gas plus starburst spectra versus an orientation-driven super-Eddington unification picture.

## Large-scale structure and gas-mapping infrastructure

- [2602.22004](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22004) Jiang-Tao Li `Probing Large-scale Structure and the Multi-Phase IGM at the Cosmic Noon -- Insights from a Joint Survey with Euclid, CSST, JPCam, and JUST`
  - **Question:** can a coordinated Euclid Deep Field North program recover both galaxy large-scale structure and multi-phase CGM/IGM information at cosmic noon?
  - **Data:**
    - Mock Euclid, CSST, JPCam, and JUST observations over `~20 deg^2` in EDF-N
    - Simulated galaxy catalog plus mock `Lyalpha` and `Mg II` absorption spectra for background sources
  - **Method:**
    - Forecast photo-z performance with broad-band-only and broad-plus-narrow-band combinations
    - Test stacked narrow-band detectability of extended `[O II]` CGM halos
    - Reconstruct the `3D` `H I` field from simulated CSST `Lyalpha`-forest sightlines
    - Check whether diffuse IGM `Mg II` is observable with JUST through stacking or two-point statistics
  - **Conclusion:**
    - JPCam-like narrow bands substantially improve photo-z accuracy relative to Euclid plus CSST broad bands alone, especially for star-forming galaxies at `z~1.0-1.4`.
    - That photo-z gain is enough to identify galaxy groups and protoclusters directly from imaging-based redshifts.
    - Stacked narrow-band imaging should detect extended `[O II]`-emitting CGM halos, and CSST `Lyalpha`-forest tomography should recover the large-scale `H I` structure reliably.
    - Diffuse IGM `Mg II` absorption is not feasible with JUST in the tested configurations, so real metallicity mapping will need deeper and higher-resolution spectroscopy such as future `39 m`-class facilities.
  - **Position:** one of the most directly relevant future-survey design papers here, because it links photo-z structure finding, CGM emission, and IGM absorption in the same cosmic-noon framework

- [2602.24162](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.24162) Pierluigi Rinaldi `Filling the Gap in Cluster Evolution: JWST's Glimpse into a Young, Star-Forming Cluster at Cosmic Noon`
  - **Question:** what does a still star-forming cluster in a massive halo look like at `z~1.84` before environmental quenching is fully established?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRISS spectroscopy of HUDF46
    - Deep HST/ACS, JWST/NIRCam, and JWST/MIRI imaging from rest-frame UV to mid-infrared
    - Deep `Chandra` data for X-ray comparison
  - **Method:**
    - Expand the confirmed member list from spectroscopy
    - Characterize stellar populations and structures across a `3 dex` stellar-mass baseline
    - Estimate velocity dispersion and halo mass under the virialization assumption
    - Compare the member population with coeval field galaxies
  - **Conclusion:**
    - JWST/NIRISS expands the confirmed membership from `18` to `37` galaxies, reaching masses of `M_* ~ 10^7.5-10.5 Msun`.
    - The overdensity has `sigma ~ 670 +- 91 km s^-1` and `M_200 ~ (1.2 +- 0.2) x 10^14 Msun`, consistent with the X-ray-based mass scale.
    - Despite that large halo mass, the population is overwhelmingly star-forming, with no established red sequence and no extended X-ray-bright intracluster medium.
    - Member stellar and structural properties remain close to those of field galaxies, so HUDF46 is still in the pre-quenching transition stage.
  - **Position:** a useful benchmark for the onset of cluster environmental processing, because it isolates a massive halo before a mature passive population has formed

- [2602.20733](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20733) Suman Sarkar `Galaxy evolution in the cosmic web: the relative impact of nodes and filaments in the EAGLE simulation`
  - **Question:** do nodes and filaments imprint galaxy evolution differently once their effects are separated in the same cosmic-web analysis?
  - **Data:**
    - EAGLE cosmological hydrodynamical simulation
    - DisPerSE-identified filament spines and cluster-scale nodes
  - **Method:**
    - Measure distances to both filament spines and nodes for the galaxy sample
    - Classify galaxies into red, green, and blue populations with an entropic-thresholding method
    - Quantify environmental influence with normalized mutual information across the `d_f - d_n` plane
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Red galaxies preferentially occupy filament cores and nodes, while blue galaxies dominate the outskirts.
    - The transition from blue-dominated to red-dominated populations appears at two different environmental scales: `~2.5 Mpc` for node proximity and `~0.75 Mpc` for filament proximity.
    - At fixed filament distance, the mass-colour coupling weakens toward nodes, which implies that the cluster-scale environment and the filamentary environment are not interchangeable.
    - Low-mass galaxies show a stronger environmental response than high-mass galaxies.
  - **Position:** a useful theory-side paper for interpreting node versus filament effects separately in observed overdensity and web analyses

## High-z gas exchange and baryon budgets

- [2602.20241](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20241) Wuji Wang `The ALPINE-CRISTAL-JWST Survey: Chemical Abundance Comparison Between the ISM and CGM of Main-Sequence Galaxies at z=4-6`
  - **Question:** how similar are ISM and CGM abundances in normal massive galaxies at `4<z<6`, and what does any offset imply for early metal transport?
  - **Data:**
    - ALPINE-CRISTAL-JWST sample of main-sequence galaxies with `M_* > 10^9.5 Msun` at `4<z<6`
    - JWST/NIRSpec IFU measurements of optical emission-line ratios in the ISM and CGM
    - ALMA `[C II] 158 um` constraints for the same systems
  - **Method:**
    - Compare line ratios from the ISM at `<=3 kpc` and the CGM at `~5-10 kpc`
    - Translate the line-ratio differences into abundance and ionization constraints
    - Use `Cloudy` models to separate abundance effects from ionization effects
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The ISM and CGM show broadly similar chemical abundances, so the CGM in these typical `4<z<6` galaxies is already enriched nearly to the ISM level.
    - Some oxygen-line ratios still differ marginally between the two regions, but the dominant driver is higher ionization in the ISM rather than a large metallicity gap.
    - The CGM shows a possible nitrogen deficit relative to oxygen, which is consistent with delayed redistribution of secondary nitrogen.
    - The CGM also has elevated `F_[CII]158um / F_Halpha`, which points to feedback or mergers as important metal-mixing channels.
  - **Position:** a high-value early-universe CGM paper because it compares enrichment, ionization, and metal transport between `<=3 kpc` and `~10 kpc` scales in normal `z=4-6` galaxies

- [2603.00336](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00336) Dylan L. Jow `FRB scattering statistics through the CGM are sensitive to morphology and intermittency`
  - **Question:** can FRB scattering statistics distinguish different small-scale morphologies and intermittency patterns in the cool CGM?
  - **Data:**
    - Forward models of FRB propagation through individual foreground halos
    - Small-scale CGM models spanning volumetric and intermittent scattering regimes
  - **Method:**
    - Define the scattering-time distribution function (`TDF`) for a single halo
    - Compare turbulent volume-filling scattering with scattering from discrete structures
    - Test spherical, filamentary, and sheet-like cool-gas morphologies
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The `TDF` is sensitive to both gas intermittency and morphology, so FRBs can probe small-scale CGM structure that hydrodynamical simulations still fail to resolve directly.
    - The statistics distinguish volumetric scattering from intermittent scattering and also separate spherical, `1D` filamentary, and `2D` sheet-like cool-gas geometries.
    - The `TDF` is directly analogous to covering-factor measurements in absorber work, which makes it straightforward to compare with more traditional CGM observables.
    - Nearby-halo samples with hundreds of FRB sightlines should be able to test these predictions with facilities such as MeerKAT, Parkes, FAST, and DSA-2000.
  - **Position:** a methodologically useful bridge between absorption-based CGM work and radio transient probes of unresolved halo structure

- [2603.00230](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00230) Umberto Maio `The Baryon Budget of Galaxies across the First Billion Years`
  - **Question:** where do baryons reside across the first billion years of galaxy growth, and how do the phase budgets scale with mass, SFR, and metallicity?
  - **Data:**
    - ColdSIM non-equilibrium chemistry simulations of early galaxies
    - Explicit cold `H I` and `H_2` tracking across galaxies, CGM, and diffuse IGM
  - **Method:**
    - Build a full baryon census across cold, warm, hot, and stellar phases
    - Measure depletion times, gas-to-star fractions, and stellar return fractions as functions of redshift and stellar age
    - Derive fitting functions for phase masses, main-sequence scaling, and depletion times
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Before reionization, cosmic gas is mostly cold, but the warm phase becomes dominant later as star formation and UV reionization intensify.
    - Stellar return fractions are only `~0.15-0.20`, about a factor of two below the values often assumed in simple models.
    - Cold, warm, and hot gas masses all increase with host mass and SFR, while depletion times fall to `0.01-0.1 Gyr` with only weak metallicity dependence.
    - Star-formation efficiency stays at only a few per cent, and gas-to-star fractions decline with mass under the combined effect of feedback and environment.
  - **Position:** a useful framework paper for early baryon cycling, especially when linking stellar growth to explicit `H I` and `H_2` budgets

## LRDs and compact high-z systems

- [2603.00232](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00232) Robert G. Pascalau `GA-NIFS: Dissecting The Alchemised: NIRSpec/IFU reveals turbulent gas inflows in a complex system at z=10.17`
  - **Question:** what powers and feeds the complex MACS0647-JD system at `z=10.17`, and can resolved metallicity structure identify fresh inflow?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec IFU spectroscopy of MACS0647-JD
    - JWST/NIRCam photometry for the same source-plane system
    - Spatially resolved emission-line, metallicity, and kinematic information
  - **Method:**
    - Compare the two stellar components in mass and metallicity
    - Measure the offset between the `Hgamma` emission centroid and the stellar continuum
    - Relate metallicity gradients, velocity dispersion, and bursty star-formation structure to gas inflow
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The main component is more massive and more enriched than its companion, with `log(M_*/Msun) = 7.77 +- 0.09` and `12 + log(O/H) = 7.89 +- 0.11` versus `7.42 +- 0.07` and `7.47 +- 0.14`.
    - The `Hgamma` emission centroid is offset by `0.1 arcsec`, or `150 pc` in the source plane, from the stellar continuum peak, which supports a merger-driven burst interpretation.
    - Metal-poor, dynamically unstable gas is inferred outside the stellar components from the combined metallicity, velocity-dispersion, and burstiness maps.
    - That metal-poor gas most likely feeds the recent star-formation enhancement in the north-east region of the system.
  - **Position:** a useful early-universe inflow paper because it ties resolved metallicity structure, bursty growth, and merger geometry together at `z=10.17`

- [2602.20247](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.20247) Pablo G. Pérez-González `Little Red Dots: One Photometric Tag Concealing Diverse Spectroscopic Flavors of Massive Star Formation and Black Hole Activity`
  - **Question:** do LRDs form a single spectroscopic population, or does the photometric label hide several distinct dense-gas and starburst states?
  - **Data:**
    - `249` LRDs with JWST/NIRSpec prism spectra at `2.3<z<9.3`
    - MIRI photometry extending the stacked SED to rest-frame `4 um`
    - Median stack plus `4` additional subsample stacks split by optical-to-UV luminosity ratio
  - **Method:**
    - Build rest-frame `0.09-1.2 um` stacked spectra and compare them across the subsamples
    - Infer black-hole masses, stellar masses, and accretion rates under a dense-gas BH-plus-stars interpretation
    - Identify UV, optical, and NIR line diagnostics plus continuum-shape differences across the stacks
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The stacked LRD population is heterogeneous, not uniform, with continuum slopes and line ratios that vary strongly with optical-to-UV luminosity ratio.
    - Under the BH-plus-stars interpretation, the typical masses are `M_BH ~ 10^6.0-6.5 Msun` and `M_* ~ 10^8.3 Msun`, giving BH-to-stellar mass ratios of `1-2%`.
    - The stacks show ubiquitous UV and optical `Fe II` emission and a sub-Eddington but high accretion rate of `lambda_Edd = 0.6 +- 0.2`.
    - The bluest systems can have stellar far-UV contributions up to `~80%`, while possible Wolf-Rayet features imply compact starbursts with ages of `~3-7 Myr`.
    - Strong Balmer breaks, unusual Balmer and Paschen line ratios, and warm dense gas of `4000-7000 K` all support radiative-transfer effects in high-density nuclear regions.
  - **Position:** one of the more useful population-level LRD papers because it argues directly against a single spectral template and quantifies how BH activity and compact starbursts mix

- [2602.22386](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.22386) Piero Madau `Little Red Dots as Obscured Little Blue Dots: A Super-Eddington Unification Model`
  - **Question:** can LRDs be unified with compact blue broad-line AGN as obscured, high-inclination views of the same super-Eddington accretion state?
  - **Data:**
    - Inclination-dependent SED model for super-Eddington compact AGN
    - `Cloudy` calculations tied to JWST LRD continuum and line constraints
  - **Method:**
    - Model a geometrically thick radiation-pressure-supported accretion flow plus equatorial BLR and dust
    - Compute line and continuum observables as a function of inclination
    - Compare predicted `Halpha` EW, Balmer decrement, Balmer break, and IR emission with LRD and LBD phenomenology
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Extreme broad `Halpha` EWs are reproduced with a global BLR covering factor of only `15%`, so they do not require a fully enclosing cocoon.
    - A gray attenuation law with `A_V = 2.8` along LRD sightlines reproduces the V-shaped UV-optical continua and large Balmer decrements.
    - Strong Balmer breaks appear only in the more obscured orientations, while weak high-ionization lines follow from orientation-dependent suppression of the XUV and soft-X-ray continuum.
    - The dust component reradiates only a small fraction of `L_bol`, implying a modest hot-dust bump and small dust masses compatible with current far-IR and sub-mm limits.
  - **Position:** a concrete AGN-unification model for LRDs, useful mainly as a compact orientation-based alternative to dense-cocoon or purely stellar explanations
