## Executive view

The 2026-04-24 batch is strong enough that it should have triggered both a daily report and the weekly wrap. The most important paper for the core profile is [2604.21218](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21218) Yongda Zhu, because it reports metal absorption in three galaxies at `z=7.2-9.3` and directly shows that multiphase, metal-enriched baryon cycling was already operating before the midpoint of reionization. The strongest environment paper is [2604.21007](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21007) Takumi Kakimoto, which spectroscopically confirms massive quiescent galaxies in two high-redshift overdense regions and links synchronized quenching to large-scale structure and AGN feedback.

The early-galaxy interpretation side is also good. [2604.21516](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21516) Zuyi Chen gives a detailed JWST/NIRSpec case for a super-luminous `z=9.3` weak-line galaxy whose weak emission lines are better explained by a recent star-formation downturn than by a large escape fraction. [2604.21493](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21493) Rui Marques-Chaves argues that very massive stars are needed to explain strong UV wind features in two `z~8.7` galaxies. [2604.20966](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20966) Pratika Dayal compares light seeds, heavy seeds, and primordial black holes in the JWST era and proposes clustering as a discriminant for high-z over-massive black holes.

For AGN feedback and large-scale environment, [2604.21405](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21405) E. Amenta and [2604.20953](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20953) G. Mountrichas are the useful reads. Amenta connects X-ray ultra-fast outflows to extended radio emission in SUBWAYS AGN. Mountrichas tests whether broad-line X-ray AGN occupy different dark-matter halos from matched galaxies and finds only mild evidence for a halo-mass excess at the highest black-hole masses.

## Reionization-era gas and first-galaxy physics

- [2604.21218](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21218) Yongda Zhu et al. `Early metal-enriched baryon cycling before the midpoint of cosmic reionization`
  - **Question:** was metal-enriched gas already cycling through galaxy-associated outflows before reionization was halfway complete?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec: rest-frame UV spectra of three galaxies at `z=7.2-9.3`
    - Absorption tracers: O I, Si II, C II, Si IV, and C IV
    - Systemic reference: nebular emission-line redshifts for velocity-offset measurements
  - **Method:**
    - Detect rest-UV metal absorption in individual high-redshift galaxies
    - Compare absorption velocities with systemic redshifts from nebular lines
    - Use ionization-state coexistence and equivalent-width ratios to infer multiphase gas structure
    - Interpret the shared velocity structure as outflowing or kinematically disturbed galaxy-associated gas
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Metal absorption is detected in all three galaxies, showing that enriched gas already surrounded luminous galaxies at `z=7.2-9.3`.
    - The detected transitions span neutral, low-ionization, and high-ionization phases, so the gas is multiphase rather than a single narrow component.
    - Velocity offsets are typically `|Delta v| ~ 50-250 km/s` and are mostly blueshifted relative to systemic redshift, consistent with outflowing or disturbed baryon-cycle gas.
    - The result requires rapid early metal production and redistribution within the first several hundred Myr.
  - **Position:** a core IGM/CGM result for the week because it moves baryon-cycle evidence into the pre-midpoint reionization epoch with direct JWST absorption measurements

- [2604.21516](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21516) Zuyi Chen et al. `SPURS: Bursty Star Formation in an Extremely Luminous Weak Emission Line Galaxy at z=9.3`
  - **Question:** why does a super-luminous `z=9.3` galaxy have extremely weak emission lines despite its large stellar mass and UV luminosity?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/SPURS: ultra-deep `29 hr` rest-frame UV spectroscopy
    - Target galaxy: `z=9.3`, `M_UV=-21.66`, stellar mass `1.6 x 10^9 Msun`
    - Spectral diagnostics: Hbeta, stellar wind features, rest-optical line ratios, interstellar absorption, and Lyalpha damping-wing shape
  - **Method:**
    - Use deep grating spectroscopy to measure weak nebular emission and stellar-wind features
    - Infer metallicity from stellar and nebular diagnostics
    - Use interstellar absorption to test neutral-gas covering fraction and outflow velocity
    - Model recent star-formation history and the IGM damping wing
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The galaxy has very weak Hbeta, with `EW ~ 25 Angstrom`, but is already significantly enriched at `0.4-0.7 Zsun`.
    - Interstellar absorption shows outflows at about `v ~ -161 km/s` and a large neutral-gas covering fraction, so weak lines are not best explained by a high escape fraction.
    - A `10-20 Myr` burst followed by a downturn within the last `10 Myr` explains the Balmer break, weak lines, and wind signatures.
    - The smooth Lyalpha break implies a small ionized bubble of `0.29^{+0.11}_{-0.09} pMpc` and a high neutral fraction of `x_HI = 0.81^{+0.14}_{-0.21}`.
  - **Position:** a high-priority first-galaxy paper because it links weak-line spectra to bursty SFHs and gives a clean damping-wing constraint for a massive `z>9` galaxy

- [2604.21493](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21493) Rui Marques-Chaves et al. `Signatures of Very Massive Stars in the Epoch of Reionization`
  - **Question:** are very massive stars required to explain the UV spectra of bright `z~8.7` galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec: ultra-deep `20-30 hr` rest-frame UV spectra
    - Targets: CEERS-1019 and CEERS-1025 at `z~8.7`
    - Spectral diagnostics: N V `1240`, C IV `1550`, and broad He II `1640`
  - **Method:**
    - Compare the observed UV wind profiles with synthetic stellar population models
    - Fit models with and without very massive stars at `Z_star ~ 0.1 Zsun`
    - Use information criteria to compare model quality
    - Infer stellar ages and ionizing photon production efficiencies
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Models including very massive stars fit the UV wind features far better than models without them, with `Delta AIC` and `Delta BIC > 70`.
    - The spectra show strong P-Cygni N V and C IV plus broad He II with `EW ~ 2-4 Angstrom`.
    - Best-fit ages are extremely young, about `1.5-2.0 Myr`.
    - The inferred ionizing photon production efficiency is high, with `log xi_ion >= 25.8`, about `0.1-0.2 dex` above non-VMS models.
  - **Position:** a strong reionization-era stellar-population paper because it connects UV spectral features, ionizing output, and early chemical enrichment to an IMF extending beyond `100 Msun`

## High-z environments, quenching, and early black holes

- [2604.21007](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21007) Takumi Kakimoto et al. `DeepDive: Simultaneous Formation of Massive Quiescent Galaxies in High-Redshift Galaxy Proto-clusters`
  - **Question:** do massive quiescent galaxies in high-redshift overdensities form and quench in a synchronized way?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec DeepDive plus archival Dawn JWST Archive spectra
    - Confirmed systems: three quiescent galaxies around Jekyll and Hyde at `z=3.71` and two around SXDS-27434 at `z=4.01`
    - Photometric-redshift environment maps: COSMOS and SXDS fields
    - Simulation comparison: Illustris TNG300 overdensity analogue at `z=3.71`
  - **Method:**
    - Spectroscopically confirm quiescent galaxies near massive high-z systems
    - Measure local galaxy number-density excess with photometric redshifts
    - Fit star-formation histories and quenching epochs
    - Use emission-line diagnostics to identify AGN-driven line emission
    - Compare the observed overdensity to TNG300 structures
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Both target fields are overdense, with galaxy number densities about `3x` the field average.
    - The quiescent galaxies have broadly consistent formation and quenching epochs, supporting synchronized evolution.
    - JWST spectra reveal broad Halpha from SXDS-27434 and faint AGN-like emission lines from three other quiescent galaxies.
    - A TNG300 overdensity at `z=3.71` reproduces similar member quiescent galaxies and similar quenching epochs.
    - Large-scale structure likely enhances merger activity or gas accretion, triggering AGN feedback and coordinated quenching.
  - **Position:** a core protocluster paper because it directly connects high-z overdensity, massive quiescent-galaxy formation, and AGN-linked quenching

- [2604.20966](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20966) Pratika Dayal `Light, heavy, primordial: exploring the diversity of black hole seeding and growth mechanisms in the JWST era`
  - **Question:** which early black-hole seeding channels can reproduce JWST-era over-massive black holes, host stellar masses, and low metallicities?
  - **Data:**
    - DELPHI semi-analytic model: light and heavy astrophysical seed populations
    - PHANES analytic model: primordial black-hole seed populations
    - JWST comparison space: `z~5-10` black-hole masses, stellar masses, metallicities, luminosities, and host halo masses
  - **Method:**
    - Compare light seeds of about `100 Msun`, heavy seeds of `10^3-10^5 Msun`, and PBHs over `10^0.5-10^6 Msun`
    - Model Eddington-limited and super-Eddington accretion for astrophysical seeds
    - Compare predicted BH-host scaling relations with observed high-z candidates
    - Identify clustering or halo-mass signatures that distinguish seed families
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Eddington-limited accretion onto light seeds is the only tested model ruled out by the current comparison.
    - High `M_BH/M_*` ratios of `0.3-1` can be reproduced by PBHs and by heavy seeds with super-Eddington growth.
    - PBHs and Eddington-limited heavy seeds can reproduce high black-hole masses, host stellar masses, and very low metallicities of `Z <= 0.01 Zsun`.
    - Systems at `z~7` with `M_BH/M_* > 0.1`, `L_bol ~ 10^44-10^46 erg/s`, and halo masses `10^9-10^11 Msun` could discriminate PBH seeding through clustering.
  - **Position:** an important theory frame for LRD and over-massive-BH work because it turns early seed models into observable halo and clustering tests

- [2604.20953](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20953) G. Mountrichas et al. `Black hole mass, host galaxy mass, and dark matter halos: Testing the environmental connection`
  - **Question:** do broad-line X-ray AGN with different black-hole masses occupy different large-scale environments from matched non-AGN galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - AGN sample: XMM-XXL and Stripe 82X broad-line X-ray AGN
    - Galaxy controls: VIPERS and SDSS/Stripe 82 galaxies matched in host properties
    - Black-hole mass bins: `8.0 <= log(M_BH/Msun) < 8.5` and `8.5 <= log(M_BH/Msun) < 9.0`
  - **Method:**
    - Measure the empirical `M_BH-M_*` relation for the AGN sample
    - Match AGN to control galaxies in `M_*`, SFR, and sSFR
    - Use AGN-galaxy cross-correlation functions to infer characteristic halo masses
    - Compare halo masses across black-hole mass bins
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The observed `M_BH-M_*` relation is shallow and scattered, likely because of flux limits and virial mass uncertainties.
    - Lower-mass black-hole AGN occupy halos statistically similar to matched controls.
    - Higher-mass black-hole AGN show only mild evidence for more massive halos, with an offset of about `0.4 dex`.
    - Halo-scale processes may matter most at the highest black-hole masses, but the current evidence is not yet decisive.
  - **Position:** useful for AGN-environment work because it tests the same halo-connection question that high-z quasar environment studies often assume

## AGN feedback and radio diagnostics

- [2604.21405](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21405) E. Amenta et al. `Supermassive Black Hole Winds in X-rays: SUBWAYS IV. Tracing Radio Emission and Unveiling the Role of Winds`
  - **Question:** are X-ray ultra-fast outflows connected to larger-scale radio emission in radio-quiet AGN?
  - **Data:**
    - SUBWAYS sample: `21` X-ray-selected AGN at `z=0.1-0.5`
    - Bolometric luminosities: `log L_bol = 44.9-46.3 erg/s`
    - Radio data: JVLA `1.5` and `6 GHz`, LoTSS, and public `150-1400 MHz` surveys
    - UFO status: ultra-fast outflows detected in `30%` of targets
  - **Method:**
    - Build radio SEDs for the SUBWAYS AGN
    - Combine radio spectral indices, luminosities, and morphologies with SFR, X-ray, Eddington-ratio, and UFO kinetic-luminosity information
    - Separate star formation, weak jets, winds, and coronal emission as possible radio sources
    - Compare UFO hosts with wind-driven shock model predictions
  - **Conclusion:**
    - All targets are radio detected and mostly consistent with radio-quiet AGN.
    - About `80%` show evidence for an outflow, either wind-like or weak-jet-like.
    - UFO hosts tend to have larger radio extents and steep spectra, consistent with outflows.
    - The six UFO hosts have radio emission consistent with wind-driven shock predictions, suggesting a possible direct connection between X-ray and radio outflow phases.
  - **Position:** a useful AGN-feedback bridge because it links nuclear UFOs to extended radio structures in a controlled X-ray-selected sample

- [2604.20950](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20950) Maria Werhahn et al. `Revisiting radio synchrotron diagnostics in star-forming galaxies`
  - **Question:** how reliable are common radio synchrotron assumptions for interpreting cosmic-ray transport in star-forming galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - MHD simulation: isolated Milky Way-mass galaxy
    - Cosmic-ray electron modeling: CREST evolution along Lagrangian tracer particles
    - Physics included: injection at supernova remnants, gas advection, and spatially varying radiative losses
  - **Method:**
    - Compare time-dependent cosmic-ray electron evolution with common steady-state models
    - Test whether advection-only transport explains vertical radio profiles
    - Compare galaxy-integrated CR electron spectra with the resulting synchrotron spectra
    - Include bremsstrahlung and Coulomb losses in radio-emitting electrons
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Self-consistently driven advection-only winds cannot reproduce observed extended vertical radio intensity profiles, even when they reproduce spectral-index steepening.
    - Matching radio halos with advection alone requires unrealistically high midplane wind speeds.
    - Galaxy-integrated CR electron spectra can look similar while the observable synchrotron spectra differ because radio emission is biased toward young electrons in dense, magnetized regions.
    - Bremsstrahlung and Coulomb losses materially shape radio spectra and should not be ignored.
  - **Position:** mainly a diagnostics paper, but useful for feedback studies because it warns against over-interpreting radio continuum as a simple wind or SFR tracer

- [2604.21224](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21224) Troy A. Porter et al. `Geometry, Not Calorimetry, Drives the Radio/Infrared/Gamma-Ray Correlation`
  - **Question:** is the radio-infrared-gamma-ray correlation in star-forming galaxies caused by local cosmic-ray calorimetry or by projection through galactic structure?
  - **Data:**
    - GALPROP models: 3D Milky Way models with varied cosmic-ray, gas, radiation-field, and magnetic-field distributions
    - Synthetic observations: external viewing geometries and inclinations
    - Local calibration: model normalization to local cosmic-ray data
  - **Method:**
    - Generate multiwavelength synthetic maps from the model grid
    - Compare radio, infrared, and gamma-ray correlations across viewing angles
    - Test whether correlations persist without local calorimetry
    - Identify geometric regimes where the relation breaks down
  - **Conclusion:**
    - A tight quasi-linear correlation emerges naturally from line-of-sight integration through radially structured disks.
    - The correlation can persist even without local cosmic-ray calorimetry.
    - Edge-on views break the relation more strongly because galactic components become stratified.
    - Scatter in the correlation is likely a diagnostic of galaxy structure and viewing angle rather than random noise.
  - **Position:** a useful companion to [2604.20950](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20950) Maria Werhahn because both caution that radio diagnostics encode geometry and transport, not just star formation
