## Executive view

The 2026-04-23 batch is stronger than the average daily listing for this profile. The highest-priority paper is [2604.19928](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19928) Ryusei R. Kano, because it tackles the `z~8` dust-budget problem directly and argues that extremely dense cold neutral clumps can reconcile the UV-to-FIR SED of MACS0416_Y1. The most provocative interpretation paper is [2604.19988](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19988) Devesh Nandal, which proposes late pulsational mass loss from supermassive stars as the physical origin of the compact dense shells inferred around little red dots.

On the gas and baryon-cycle side, [2604.19896](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19896) P. P. Deka, [2604.20060](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20060) Razieh Emami, and [2604.20080](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20080) R. Slater are the key reads. They span HI absorption in radio AGN, multi-element chemical evolution in extreme emission-line galaxies, and molecular-gas survival in a low-metallicity dusty starburst. These are mostly low-redshift systems, but all three are useful method or physics anchors for interpreting how gas accretion, shielding, outflows, and enrichment interact in more distant galaxies.

The environment and demographics side is led by [2604.20411](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20411) Kaimin He and [2604.20056](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20056) Toby Brown. One maps how environment and morphology separately affect the SFR-`M_*` relation out to `z=4`, and the other shows that Virgo cluster disks have broader ionized-gas kinematics and more diffuse-ionized-gas-dominated line ratios than field disks. [2604.19865](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19865) J. C. S. Pierce adds a broad radio-feedback population result out to `z=2.5`.

## High-redshift dust, LRDs, and early-source interpretation

- [2604.19928](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19928) Ryusei R. Kano et al. `Resolving the Dust Budget Crisis at z ~ 8 with Optically Thick, High-Density Molecular Clumps: MACS0416_Y1`
  - **Question:** can extremely dense cold neutral clumps solve the dust-budget crisis in the `z=8.312` galaxy MACS0416_Y1?
  - **Data:**
    - Target galaxy: MACS0416_Y1 at `z=8.312`
    - SED constraints: observed UV-to-FIR emission, including ALMA Band 9 continuum
    - Grain-size-resolved dust model: multi-temperature dust-population predictions
  - **Method:**
    - Modify the standard dust-evolution framework by treating the cold neutral medium density as a free parameter
    - Fit the observed UV-to-FIR SED
    - Track UV trapping, dust growth, and supernova dust destruction in dense clumps
    - Resolve the infrared output by grain-size population
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Reproducing the observed SED requires extreme dense-gas conditions of `n_H,CNM ~ 7.5 x 10^3 cm^-3`.
    - Dense clumps increase UV trapping, accelerate dust processing, and reduce supernova dust destruction.
    - Intermediate-size grains with `a = 0.01-0.1 um` contribute about `89%` of the luminosity near the SED peak and in ALMA Band 9.
    - The dominant warm dust has characteristic temperatures of about `~70 K`.
  - **Position:** a high-priority first-galaxy dust paper because it turns the dust-budget crisis into a concrete ISM-structure problem rather than a generic failure of dust production alone

- [2604.19988](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19988) Devesh Nandal et al. `Pulsational mass loss from supermassive stars creates the compact shells of Little Red Dots`
  - **Question:** can late pulsational mass loss from supermassive stars create the dense cocoon-like shells inferred around little red dots?
  - **Data:**
    - Stellar-evolution models: five accreting GENEC supermassive-star models with characteristic masses of order `10^5 Msun`
    - Metallicity range: Pop III to `10^-2 Zsun`
    - Pulsation and GR-instability calculations: post-accretion late evolutionary stages
  - **Method:**
    - Evolve accreting supermassive stars through post-accretion phases
    - Calculate strange-mode pulsation ejection episodes
    - Track shell masses, durations, and radii
    - Compare the ejecta properties with the compact dense shells implied by LRD spectra
  - **Conclusion:**
    - In the clearest LRD analogue at `Z = 10^-2 Zsun`, four late ejection episodes last `41-282 yr` and eject `10-348 Msun` each.
    - The total late mass loss is `(4.8-10) x 10^2 Msun`, with the final episode contributing about `~73%` of the budget.
    - The final episode can leave an optically thick shell extending to about `0.4 pc`, matching the required compact cocoon scale.
    - The final ejecta are nitrogen rich, with `log(N/O) ~ 0.13` and `log(C/O) ~ -0.23`.
    - The star reaches GR instability at an age of about `~1 Myr` and retains about `~99%` of its mass at collapse.
  - **Position:** a direct LRD interpretation paper that links compact shells, supermassive-star evolution, and heavy black-hole seeds in a single physical channel

## Gas, absorption, and the baryon cycle

- [2604.19896](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19896) P. P. Deka et al. `HI 21-cm absorption in low- and high-excitation radio-loud AGNs at z<0.5 from MALS`
  - **Question:** how common is associated HI 21-cm absorption in radio-loud AGN at `z<0.5`, and does it differ between LERGs and HERGs?
  - **Data:**
    - MeerKAT Absorption Line Survey plus SDSS DR18 cross-match: `1908` radio sources at `z<0.5`
    - AGN sample: `613` BPT- and radio-selected AGN, including `327` LERGs and `99` HERGs in the excitation-classified subset
    - Radio-bright search sample: `79` LERGs and `20` HERGs with `S_1.4GHz > 4 mJy`
  - **Method:**
    - Cross-match MALS radio sources with SDSS spectroscopy
    - Classify sources into LERGs and HERGs
    - Search the radio-bright subset for associated HI 21-cm absorption
    - Compare detection rates, colors, and absorption-profile kinematics
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The survey reports `5` new detections at `0.29 < z < 0.47`.
    - The overall detection rate is `3^{+3}_{-2}%`, consistent with sensitivity-matched low-redshift samples and showing no clear evolution to `z~0.5`.
    - LERGs are significantly redder in `k`-corrected `g-r`, consistent with older and more quenched host populations.
    - Several HI profiles show velocity offsets exceeding `350 km s^-1`, indicating disturbed cold-gas kinematics likely driven by jet or lobe activity.
  - **Position:** the best direct absorption result in the batch, and a useful low-redshift anchor for how cold gas responds to AGN radio-mode feedback

- [2604.20060](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20060) Razieh Emami et al. `Unraveling Chemical Enrichment in Extreme Emission-Line Galaxies: A Multi-Element Bayesian View of Bursty Star Formation and Galaxy Evolution in DESI`
  - **Question:** what do multi-element abundances reveal about star-formation efficiency, outflows, and inflow metallicity in extreme emission-line galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - DESI DR1 sample: `23` nearby EELGs
    - Abundance set: `19` ionic species with `S/N >= 4`
    - Galaxy selection: `M_* >= 10^7 Msun` and `EW(Halpha), EW([O III]5007) >= 500 Angstrom`
  - **Method:**
    - Infer non-parametric star-formation histories
    - Fit a Bayesian single-zone chemical-evolution model to O, N, Ne, S, and Ar
    - Allow time-dependent star-formation efficiency, outflow mass loading, and inflow metallicity
    - Use abundance ratios to isolate which baryon-cycle terms drive the chemical tracks
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The galaxies require short depletion times and large mass-loading factors, implying rapid gas cycling in a non-equilibrium burst regime.
    - Outflows and star-formation efficiency are well constrained, while inflow metallicity remains more degenerate.
    - `N/O` is especially sensitive to burst timing and gas flows.
    - `Ne/O` remains nearly invariant, while `S/O` and `Ar/O` have intermediate sensitivity.
  - **Position:** a strong methods paper for connecting abundance patterns to baryon-cycle physics, with clear reuse value for JWST-era low-mass starburst interpretation

- [2604.20080](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20080) R. Slater et al. `Cold molecular gas distribution and kinematics in the low-metallicity dusty starburst of Mrk 996 resolved with ALMA`
  - **Question:** how is molecular gas distributed and kinematically organized in the low-metallicity dusty starburst Mrk 996?
  - **Data:**
    - ALMA CO(1-0) and CO(2-1): resolved molecular-gas morphology and kinematics
    - Target galaxy: Mrk 996, a low-mass Wolf-Rayet galaxy with metallicity of about `~1/5` solar
    - Comparison data: optical and UV tracers of ionized gas and young stars
  - **Method:**
    - Map CO morphology and velocity structure
    - Compare molecular-gas locations to the super star cluster and highly ionized regions
    - Measure the global CO excitation ratio
    - Infer molecular-gas mass with a metallicity-dependent CO-to-H2 conversion factor
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Compact CO clouds are detected within `800 pc` of the starburst but are offset from the nuclear SSC and the most ionized gas.
    - The global CO(2-1)/CO(1-0) ratio is low, with `R21 ~ 0.3`, consistent with subthermal excitation.
    - ALMA recovers only about `~50%` of the single-dish flux, implying extended low-surface-brightness molecular gas.
    - The inferred molecular-gas mass is a few `10^7 Msun`.
    - Molecular gas is only weakly coupled to the strong stellar feedback seen in the ionized phase, favoring a multiphase medium with substantial CO-dark H2.
  - **Position:** a useful low-metallicity gas-survival paper for interpreting shielded clumps, CO-dark gas, and feedback decoupling in dusty starbursts

## Environment, quenching, and galaxy demographics

- [2604.20411](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20411) Kaimin He et al. `The Evolution of the SFR-M_* relation at 0.1<z<4: Environmental and Morphological Dependencies`
  - **Question:** how do environment and morphology separately affect the star-forming main sequence from `z=0.1` to `4`?
  - **Data:**
    - COSMOS2020 mass-complete sample: approximately `290,000` galaxies
    - Redshift range: `0.1 < z < 4`
    - Structural split: early-type, spiral, and irregular morphologies
  - **Method:**
    - Measure the SFR-`M_*` relation across redshift
    - Compare high- and low-density environments
    - Separate the trends for the full sample and for star-forming galaxies only
    - Contrast environmental effects with morphology-dependent offsets
  - **Conclusion:**
    - High-density environments suppress SFR at `z<1`, especially at the high-mass end, for the full galaxy sample.
    - Within the star-forming population alone, no strong environmental effect is seen across the full redshift range.
    - Morphology has a stronger effect than environment at `z<2`, with early-type galaxies lying below spirals and irregulars at fixed mass.
    - The high-mass downturn of the SFR-`M_*` relation is interpreted as a morphology-linked quenching effect rather than a pure environment trend.
  - **Position:** a useful broad demographic paper for separating structural quenching from fast environmental quenching in massive galaxies

- [2604.20056](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20056) Toby Brown et al. `MAUVE-MUSE: Ionization and Kinematic Signatures of Environmental Effects on Virgo Cluster Disks`
  - **Question:** how does the Virgo cluster environment change ionization conditions and gas kinematics in disk galaxies on `~100 pc` scales?
  - **Data:**
    - MAUVE-MUSE early sample: `12` Virgo galaxies
    - Comparison sample: PHANGS-MUSE field disks
    - Spatially resolved line-ratio and line-width measurements
  - **Method:**
    - Compare line-ratio medians between Virgo and field disks
    - Use BPT diagnostics to classify ionization mechanisms spaxel by spaxel
    - Measure `Halpha` line-of-sight velocity dispersions
    - Separate diffuse-ionized-gas-dominated emission from shock-like tails
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Virgo disks show elevated median `[N II]/Halpha`, `[S II]/Halpha`, and `[O III]/Hbeta` compared with field disks.
    - About `74%` of MAUVE-MUSE spaxels are ionized by non-H II-region sources, versus `61%` in the field comparison.
    - About `44%` of Virgo spaxels exceed `sigma_LOS = 40 km s^-1`, compared with `26%` in field disks.
    - A `5%` tail of spaxels with `sigma_LOS > 100 km s^-1` points to shocks or turbulent mixing layers from intracluster interactions.
    - The dominant environmental signature is suppression of star formation that reveals diffuse ionized gas, with shocks as a secondary effect.
  - **Position:** a strong local environment calibration paper for linking cluster processing, DIG-dominated emission, and broadened gas kinematics

- [2604.19865](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.19865) J. C. S. Pierce et al. `A population-based approach to understanding radio AGN feedback with LOFAR: The LoTSS Deep Fields`
  - **Question:** how does the kinetic power output of radio AGN evolve across cosmic time when source size and environment are modeled explicitly?
  - **Data:**
    - Initial LoTSS plus ILT sample: `619` radio AGN at `z < 2.5`
    - Expanded LoTSS Deep Field sample: `5,187` objects in Lockman Hole, ELAIS-N1, and Boötes
    - Derived products: jet kinetic-luminosity functions and integrated kinetic-luminosity densities
  - **Method:**
    - Infer jet powers with a physically motivated semi-analytic model
    - Weight the population toward shorter source lifetimes
    - Extend the inference from the pilot sample to the larger deep-field population
    - Integrate the kinetic output over redshift
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The population is dominated by short-lived, lower-power radio sources.
    - The total kinetic power per comoving volume is about `~10^32-10^33 W Mpc^-3` over the full redshift range.
    - The results suggest moderate positive evolution from `z=0-1` and little further evolution from `z=1-2`.
    - The inferred power budget is broadly compatible with the requirements for radio-mode feedback in cosmological models.
  - **Position:** a useful population-level radio-feedback benchmark for comparing AGN heating budgets across epochs rather than object by object
