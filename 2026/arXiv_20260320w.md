## Executive view

This is one of the strongest weeks in the current set for first-galaxy and Pop III-adjacent reading. [2603.18775](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18775) Joris Witstok pushes Lyalpha-based ionizing-source arguments to `z=13.1`, while [2603.20360](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20360) Hannah Übler and [2603.20362](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20362) Roberto Maiolino reinforce the case for chemically pristine gas and a metal-free `He II` emitter near GN-z11.

The environment side is narrower but still useful. [2603.16991](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16991) Jake S. Bennett is the cleanest protocluster-gas paper of the week, following the inside-out transition from filament-fed CGM to a hot enriched intracluster medium in a `z~3` cluster progenitor.

The rest of the shortlist is mostly interpretation work around extreme early systems. [2603.15841](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15841) Giulia Rodighiero confirms a red dusty galaxy at `z=11.45`, [2603.15761](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15761) James A. A. Trussler expands the empirical search space for extremely metal-poor galaxies, and [2603.15736](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15736) plus [2603.17667](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17667) give two different LRD-origin diagnostics: clustering and gas-envelope column density.

## Cosmic dawn, pristine gas, and Pop III candidates

- [2603.18775](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.18775) Joris Witstok `An OASIS of Lyman-α within a neutral intergalactic desert: reaffirmed line and blue continuum reveal efficient ionising agents at z = 13`
  - **Question:** what do a confirmed Lyalpha line and extremely blue UV continuum at `z=13.1` imply about local ionization conditions inside a largely neutral IGM?
  - **Data:**
    - Combined `56 h` JWST/NIRSpec PRISM spectroscopy from OASIS and JADES
    - Rest-frame UV continuum and Lyalpha measurements for GS-z13-1-LA
  - **Method:**
    - Re-measure the Lyalpha equivalent width and UV slope in the deeper combined spectrum
    - Use the line and continuum constraints to infer the required ionizing production and bubble size
    - Search for a surrounding overdensity that could enlarge the ionized region
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The combined spectrum confirms both the Lyalpha line and the blue continuum at `z=13.1`, with rest-frame equivalent width `66^{+10}_{-9} Angstrom` and `beta_UV ~ -3`.
    - The source needs a very efficient ionizing engine and allows only a modest nebular-continuum contribution.
    - A small ionized bubble of `R_ion ~ 0.2 pMpc` is sufficient only if the galaxy reaches `xi_ion ~ 10^26.4 Hz erg^-1`; a larger surrounding bubble relaxes that to `~10^25.9 Hz erg^-1`.
    - The larger-bubble solution requires an overdensity of nearby ionizing systems, for which the paper finds tentative support in `16` nearby photometric candidates and one confirmed neighbor, JADES-GS-z13-0.
  - **Position:** a high-value reionization paper because it ties one extreme `z=13.1` Lyalpha detection directly to bubble-growth requirements and local overdensity arguments

- [2603.20360](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20360) Hannah Übler `GA-NIFS & JADES: Confirmation of pristine gas near GN-z11`
  - **Question:** is the previously suggested pristine gas near GN-z11 real, and how chemically unevolved is it?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec IFS `G395H` data from GA-NIFS
    - JADES plus NIRCam imaging context around GN-z11
    - Emission region `~3 pkpc` north-east of GN-z11
  - **Method:**
    - Confirm the emission-line redshift with `Hgamma`
    - Search for additional Balmer lines and metal-line non-detections across `0.25-0.45 um` rest-frame
    - Compare the line constraints with photoionization models for Pop III and Pop II mixtures
  - **Conclusion:**
    - `Hgamma` is detected at `S/N = 5.9` and confirms the source at `z = 10.5862 +- 0.0003`; `Hdelta` is marginally seen at `S/N ~ 2`.
    - No metal lines are detected across the observed spectral range, giving `12 + log(O/H) < 6.96` and `Z_gas < 0.019 Zsun` at `3 sigma`.
    - Comparison with imaging implies `EW_0(Hgamma) > 350 Angstrom`.
    - The emission is consistent with photoionization by Pop III stars, possibly mixed with next-generation Pop II stars, and the dynamical mass is constrained to `M_dyn <= 3 x 10^8 Msun`.
  - **Position:** a directly relevant pristine-gas paper because it moves the GN-z11 adjacent source from intriguing hint to a quantitatively constrained near-metal-free region

- [2603.20362](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20362) Roberto Maiolino `The search for Population III: Confirmation of a HeII emitter with no metal lines at z=10.6`
  - **Question:** does the He II emitter near GN-z11 remain robust under high-resolution NIRSpec/IFU follow-up, and are metal-line alternatives still viable?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec IFU high-resolution spectroscopy
    - Independent `Hgamma` confirmation from the adjacent Übler et al. source analysis
  - **Method:**
    - Reconfirm the `He II lambda1640` line at the GN-z11-adjacent position
    - Check the line profile for internal velocity structure
    - Search for any accompanying metal lines
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The `He II lambda1640` emitter at `z=10.6` is confirmed by the higher-resolution IFU data.
    - The line is spectrally resolved into two components separated by `120 km s^-1`.
    - The rest-frame equivalent width is extremely high, `>20 Angstrom`, and no metal lines are detected.
    - The paper argues that Pop III stars remain the most plausible explanation because other source classes fail to reproduce the combination of strong `He II` and metal-line absence.
  - **Position:** one of the clearest current Pop III-candidate papers, because the line survives higher-resolution scrutiny without introducing metal-line contamination

## Protocluster gas and structure formation

- [2603.16991](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16991) Jake S. Bennett `The birth of the intracluster medium: the evolution of multiphase gas and Lyman-α haloes in a simulated z~3 protocluster`
  - **Question:** when does a protocluster stop looking like a network of cold-fed galaxy halos and start behaving like a true hot intracluster medium?
  - **Data:**
    - Cosmological zoom-in simulation of a massive cluster progenitor evolved to `z=2.7`
    - Enhanced CGM resolution plus post-processed `Mg II`, `OVII`, `Lyalpha`, and `Halpha` diagnostics
  - **Method:**
    - Follow the protocluster from `z~4.4` to `z=2.7`
    - Track the response of density, temperature, metallicity, and multiphase absorbers to mergers and AGN feedback
    - Compare the morphology of `Lyalpha` and `Halpha` halos during the transition
  - **Conclusion:**
    - A major merger plus AGN feedback drives an inside-out transformation between `z~4.4` and `2.7`, redistributing gas to larger radii and flattening the density, temperature, and metallicity profiles.
    - Intermediate-column `Mg II` absorbers are rapidly destroyed, while the inner halo becomes ionized beyond `OVII` as it enters the X-ray regime.
    - The cold gas becomes clumpier and more satellite-associated as the hot ICM forms.
    - The extended `Lyalpha` halo remains detectable even without AGN photoionization and evolves from filamentary to more spherical, while `Halpha` halos stay smaller and dimmer.
  - **Position:** a strong theory-side bridge between filament-fed protocluster gas and the emergence of a hot enriched ICM at `z~3`

- [2603.15841](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15841) Giulia Rodighiero `EGS-z11-R0: a red, dust-rich galaxy at Cosmic Dawn`
  - **Question:** are genuinely dust-rich red galaxies already present at `z>10`, rather than the early universe being dominated only by very blue systems?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec PRISM and `G395M` spectroscopy
    - HST, NIRCam, and MIRI photometry
    - EGS-z11-R0 at `z = 11.452 +- 0.021`
  - **Method:**
    - Confirm the redshift from `C IV` and `C III]`
    - Use emission-line diagnostics to constrain the ionizing field
    - Fit the combined photometry and spectroscopy with `CIGALE`, including stellar, nebular, dust, and AGN components
  - **Conclusion:**
    - EGS-z11-R0 is spectroscopically confirmed at `z=11.45`, making it the most distant confirmed red galaxy to date.
    - The UV continuum is much redder than the canonical high-z relation, with `beta_UV ~ -1.0`.
    - The best-fit SED implies `log(M_*/Msun) ~ 9.2-9.6`, `SFR ~ 10-40 Msun yr^-1`, and substantial attenuation `A_V ~ 1.2`.
    - The ionizing field is consistent with extreme star formation and is also compatible with a composite stellar plus AGN scenario.
  - **Position:** a useful counterpoint to the dominant blue cosmic-dawn narrative, because it confirms that chemically evolved dusty systems already exist by `z~11.5`

- [2603.15761](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15761) James A. A. Trussler `Out of oxygen: Extremely metal-poor galaxy candidates identified at 2.5 < z < 6.5 with deep JADES medium-band imaging`
  - **Question:** can deep JADES medium bands isolate extremely metal-poor galaxy candidates at `2.5<z<6.5` more efficiently than waiting for serendipitous spectroscopy?
  - **Data:**
    - Deep JADES medium-band imaging
    - Candidate sample of `22` EMPG candidates at `2.5<z<6.5`
  - **Method:**
    - Select sources with strong `Halpha` photometric boosts but weak `[O III] + Hbeta` boosts
    - Require strong Balmer jumps to favor young starbursts over broad-line AGN or LRDs
    - Fit the SEDs with near-pristine `~0.1-1% Zsun` models
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The candidates are faint low-mass dwarfs with median `M_* ~ 10^6.7 Msun` and `M_UV ~ -16.6`.
    - Their observed ionizing efficiency is very high, `log(xi_ion,obs / Hz erg^-1) ~ 26.0`.
    - They likely represent `~0.04-0.6%` of galaxies in the range `-19 < M_UV < -16` at `2.5<z<6.5`.
    - The paper lays out two practical follow-up paths: deep `~28 h` NIRCam slitless spectroscopy for bright EMPGs or highly multiplexed NIRSpec for the faintest candidates out to `z=10`.
  - **Position:** a useful search-strategy paper for finding near-pristine galaxies systematically rather than through rare spectroscopic luck

## LRDs and their physical origin

- [2603.15736](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.15736) Zihao Wang `Halo assembly bias in the early Universe: a clustering probe of the origin of the Little Red Dots`
  - **Question:** can halo assembly bias and clustering distinguish competing physical origin scenarios for LRDs?
  - **Data:**
    - Shin-Uchuu large-volume `N`-body simulation
    - Halo assembly-bias measurements as functions of age, concentration, and spin
  - **Method:**
    - Characterize how halo clustering depends on secondary properties at high redshift
    - Map different LRD origin scenarios onto different host-halo populations
    - Predict the resulting large-scale bias and pair fractions
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The sign and amplitude of assembly bias depend strongly on halo mass for both concentration and spin.
    - High-concentration halos are more strongly clustered below `nu ~ 1.5`, and low-spin halos below `nu ~ 0.75`, while the trends weaken or reverse at higher mass.
    - In the LRD applications, the direct-collapse black hole scenario predicts the strongest large-scale bias and enhanced pair fractions, the SIDM core-collapse and low-spin compact-galaxy scenarios predict weaker clustering, and the primordial-black-hole scenario is close to unbiased.
  - **Position:** a useful LRD-origin paper because it turns the debate into a clustering test with concrete predictions for JWST and future deep surveys

- [2603.17667](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17667) Jorryt Matthee `The Engine and its Flows: Little Red Dot spectra are shaped by the column densities of their gas envelopes`
  - **Question:** what controls the diversity of Little Red Dot Balmer-line spectra: the engine mass, or the gas-envelope properties around it?
  - **Data:**
    - `18` broad-`Halpha` selected LRD-like sources at `z~3-7` with high-quality JWST spectra
  - **Method:**
    - Compare `Halpha` profile shape against Balmer-break strength
    - Track how absorption, wing strength, and absorber velocity change with UV-optical color
    - Interpret the trends with a clumpy partially ionized gas-envelope model
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Blue LRDs show narrow-core `Halpha` profiles, intermediate systems show P Cygni-like blue-shifted absorption, and the reddest systems have absorption-dominated cores.
    - All systems have symmetric exponential wings, but the wings are slightly broader and more dominant in the reddest sources.
    - Balmer absorption appears in `~60%` of the sample, with stronger `Hbeta` absorption than `Halpha`.
    - The absorber velocity correlates with Balmer-break strength: denser absorbers tend to be inflowing, while bluer lower-column systems show faster outflows.
    - The spectral diversity is therefore better explained by column density, ionization state, and optical depth than by simple virial broadening around a single engine class.
  - **Position:** a strong empirical LRD paper because it reframes the Balmer-line problem in terms of gas-envelope physics rather than black-hole mass alone
