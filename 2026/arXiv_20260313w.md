## Executive view

The strongest thread this week is the combination of new high-z tracers with explicit structure mapping. [2603.08830](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08830) L. N. Martínez-Ramírez adds `16` bright quasars at `z=5.94-6.45`, [2603.08066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08066) Xiaoyang Wei identifies a compact pre-virialized group at `z~7.04`, and [2603.09739](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09739) Ashley Ortiz reconstructs `6` massive ODIN protoclusters in `3D` across `~14 deg^2`.

The survey-planning side is also strong. [2603.09828](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09828) Micaela B. Bagley is a practical Roman deep-field trade study rather than a generic forecast, and [2603.13195](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.13195) the Euclid Collaboration paper gives environment-split FIR expectations for cluster, protocluster, and field samples.

On the gas and primordial side, [2603.11411](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11411) Tamal Mukherjee is the most interesting observational inflow claim this week, while [2603.13471](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.13471) Henriette Reumert and [2603.13493](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.13493) Lilian L. Lee provide two complementary JWST views of very early chemical states: a possible Pop III interaction and resolved abundance gradients in `4<z<6` main-sequence galaxies.

## Quasars and structure at the end of reionization

- [2603.08830](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08830) L. N. Martínez-Ramírez `16 new quasars at the end of the reionization unveiled by self-supervised learning`
  - **Question:** how effectively can self-supervised learning recover new luminous quasars at the end of reionization?
  - **Data:**
    - DESI Legacy Survey DR10 multiband imaging
    - Spectroscopic follow-up of `1139` high-priority candidates
  - **Method:**
    - Apply contrastive-learning selection directly to the imaging rather than to pre-defined color cuts
    - Prioritize the machine-selected sample with SED fitting
    - Spectroscopically confirm the highest-priority candidates
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The pipeline confirms `16` new quasars at `z=5.94-6.45` with a `45%` success rate.
    - All `16` are bright systems with `M_1450 < -25.5`, which makes them immediately useful as reionization and environment tracers.
    - Several have unusual properties, including narrow `Lyalpha` with `FWHM < 2600 km s^-1`, very strong `Lyalpha+N V` with equivalent width `>100 Angstrom`, and mildly red NIR continua.
    - `3` of the confirmed quasars would have been missed by traditional color-color selection.
  - **Position:** a directly useful high-z quasar-discovery paper because it expands the bright background-source pool and shows a scalable way to find objects beyond standard color cuts

- [2603.08066](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08066) Xiaoyang Wei `Pre-Virialized Assembly at Cosmic Dawn: The Dynamics and Extreme Ionization of Compact Group CGG-z7 at z~7.04`
  - **Question:** what are the dynamics and ionization conditions of the compact group CGG-z7 at `z~7.04`, and is it already virialized?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST POPPIES observations north of GOODS-N
    - Compact group with at least `6` members in `7.8 x 5.7 kpc^2`
    - `4` spectroscopic confirmations from `[O III]` and `Hbeta`
  - **Method:**
    - Measure the group velocity dispersion and compare it with the stellar mass content
    - Use line ratios to assess metallicity and ionization conditions across the group
    - Interpret the system dynamically as either virialized or caught in a pre-virialized merger state
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The line-of-sight velocity dispersion is only `~93.7 km s^-1` despite a total stellar mass of `~10^9.8 Msun`, giving `M_*/M_vir ~ 0.15`.
    - That stellar-to-dynamical mass ratio is about three times higher than typical virialized halos, so the structure is unlikely to be in dynamical equilibrium.
    - The system is better interpreted as a pre-virialized major merger near apocenter, likely during the rapid assembly of a future compact massive galaxy.
    - The group is extremely ionized and metal-poor, with the central galaxy reaching `[O III]/Hbeta ~ 18`, which likely signals an obscured AGN.
  - **Position:** a useful bridge between early group assembly, obscured AGN activity, and the formation of compact massive descendants at high redshift

- [2603.09805](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09805) Qing-Bo Ma `POLAR-II: modeling star formation history of galaxies on the 21-cm signal from Epoch of Reionization`
  - **Question:** does realistic bursty star-formation history materially change the topology and timing of the `21 cm` signal during reionization?
  - **Data:**
    - Jiutian-300 dark-matter simulation
    - L-Galaxies 2020 semi-analytic galaxy catalog
    - Grizzly radiative-transfer post-processing
  - **Method:**
    - Keep the total ionizing-photon budget fixed while changing the assumed galaxy star-formation histories
    - Compare constant-SFR and burstier SFH realizations
    - Track the resulting changes in IGM ionization, heating, and the global plus power-spectrum `21 cm` signal
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Galaxies with SFHs drawn from L-Galaxies 2020 produce ionized regions that are slightly larger and warmer than constant-SFR models with the same total ionizing budget.
    - At fixed stellar mass, galaxies with older mass-weighted stellar ages generate smaller ionized regions.
    - The SFH dependence changes both the topology and timing of ionization and heating in the IGM.
    - Those differences propagate into both the global `21 cm` signal and the `21 cm` power spectrum.
  - **Position:** a useful reionization-modeling paper because it isolates SFH burstiness as a real uncertainty in `21 cm` inference, not just a nuisance detail

## Protoclusters and survey planning

- [2603.09739](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09739) Ashley Ortiz `ODIN: Confirmation and 3D Reconstruction of Six Massive Protoclusters at Cosmic Noon`
  - **Question:** can ODIN move beyond projected overdensity identification and reconstruct full `3D` protocluster structure at cosmic noon?
  - **Data:**
    - Wide-field ODIN `Lyalpha` imaging plus DESI and ancillary spectroscopy
    - Extended COSMOS and XMM-LSS coverage over `~14 deg^2`
    - `6` confirmed systems at `z~2.4` and `z~3.1`
  - **Method:**
    - Confirm the candidate structures spectroscopically
    - Reconstruct their `3D` geometry and estimate descendant halo masses
    - Use overlapping narrowband filters for one structure to test redshift tomography
    - Compare galaxy overdensities with `Lyalpha`-forest `H I` overdensities where LATIS coverage exists
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The survey confirms `6` massive protoclusters and reconstructs their `3D` structures rather than only identifying projected peaks.
    - For one structure at `z~3.12`, the overlapping `NB497` and `N501` filters provide accurate redshift tomography for emission-line galaxies.
    - The `z~2.45` system overlapping a LATIS field enables direct comparison between galaxy overdensity and `H I` overdensity traced by `Lyalpha`-forest absorption.
    - Another system at `z~3.12` already hosts a massive quiescent galaxy with `M_* ~ 1.2 x 10^11 Msun`, which implies early quenching in a dense environment.
    - Protocluster galaxies have higher median `Lyalpha` line fluxes and fewer faint emitters than field galaxies, especially in the densest cores.
  - **Position:** one of the strongest structure papers in this batch, because it directly combines protocluster confirmation, `3D` mapping, and gas-overdensity comparison

- [2603.09828](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09828) Micaela B. Bagley `Going Wide and Deep with Roman: The z~6-9 UV luminosity function in a Roman Deep Field`
  - **Question:** what Roman ultra-deep survey design best constrains the `z~6-9` UV luminosity function while controlling contamination and cosmic variance?
  - **Data:**
    - Mock `2 deg^2` lightcone with `>7.6 million` galaxies at `0<z<10`
    - `16` Roman survey configurations, each `500` hours, spanning `0.28-2 deg^2` and four filter combinations
  - **Method:**
    - Compare photometric-redshift recovery and contamination across depth-area-filter choices
    - Measure the recovered UV luminosity function and UV luminosity density in four bins at `z~6-9`
    - Test the specific role of the `R062` and `F184` filters
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Even a single Roman pointing strongly reduces cosmic variance relative to HST-like fields and recovers the true UVLF much more faithfully.
    - The `R062` filter is essential at `z~5-6`, dropping contamination from nearly `100%` to negligible levels while recovering the bright end correctly.
    - The `F184` filter is critical above `z>9` and also important for stellar-contamination removal at all redshifts.
    - The recommended configuration is at least two Roman pointings, `0.56 deg^2`, with all six filters, which reduces uncertainties on the UV luminosity density by factors of `2-4` relative to the deepest current JWST programs.
  - **Position:** a practical Roman strategy paper with direct value for wide-plus-deep early-universe survey design

- [2603.13195](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.13195) Euclid Collaboration `Euclid preparation. Far-infrared predictions for Euclid galaxy catalogues: cluster, protocluster, and field`
  - **Question:** what far-infrared properties should Euclid-detectable galaxies have in clusters, protoclusters, and the field, and how deep must follow-up surveys be?
  - **Data:**
    - MAMBO mock galaxy catalog built on the Millennium Simulation
    - FIR flux and physical-property predictions for Euclid-detectable galaxies in different environments
  - **Method:**
    - Predict FIR flux distributions, number counts, and IR luminosity functions for Euclid Wide and Deep samples
    - Use stacking to recover mean dust properties by stellar mass and redshift
    - Compare environmental trends in the IRLF between cluster, protocluster, and field galaxies
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Only the brightest Euclid galaxies are individually detectable in current FIR surveys, so stacking is required for most of the sample.
    - Dust temperatures and infrared luminosities rise with redshift across all mass bins, while dust masses stay roughly constant.
    - The Euclid Deep Survey recovers the IRLF to fainter luminosities and higher redshift than the Wide Survey, but its detectability still falls below `80%` at `z>4`, while the Wide Survey becomes strongly incomplete beyond `z~2`.
    - The faint-end Schechter slope flattens with redshift for cluster and protocluster galaxies but stays roughly constant in the field.
    - Survey areas at least `30x` larger than the MAMBO area are required for statistically robust `>3 sigma` constraints on luminous `z~4` sources.
  - **Position:** a useful Euclid follow-up paper because it translates structure catalogs into realistic FIR depth, area, and stacking requirements

## Gas inflow, metallicity, and primordial systems

- [2603.11411](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11411) Tamal Mukherjee `BLUEPRINT: Blue-dominant Lyman-alpha (Lyα) emission as an evidence of gas inflow in ultra-low-mass galaxies at z = 3`
  - **Question:** can blue-dominant `Lyalpha` profiles provide direct evidence of gas inflow into ultra-low-mass galaxies at `z=3`?
  - **Data:**
    - MUSE eXtremely Deep Field observations of a system at `z=3.066`
    - Deep JWST/NIRCam imaging revealing two compact star-forming regions
    - Cosmic-web filament environment from the MXDF field context
  - **Method:**
    - Model the blue-dominated `Lyalpha` profile with a clumpy multiphase radiative-transfer framework
    - Measure the stellar masses, ages, and sSFRs of the embedded compact systems
    - Interpret the system geometry relative to the surrounding cosmic-web filament
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The observed clumpy `Lyalpha` emission is blue-dominated and is best fit by radial inflow of gas clumps at `~100 km s^-1`.
    - The two compact ultra-low-mass components have `log(M_*/Msun) = 6.3-6.9` and formed most of their stellar mass in less than `7 Myr`.
    - Their high specific SFRs and elevated ionization parameters are consistent with very recent bursty star formation.
    - The system provides direct observational evidence that gas accretion, likely from the cosmic web, can trigger starbursts in ultra-low-mass galaxies.
  - **Position:** a rare observational inflow paper with unusually clean cosmic-web context, making it relevant to both Lyalpha radiative transfer and gas-accretion studies

- [2603.13471](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.13471) Henriette Reumert `A hot, nebular-dominated galaxy interacting with a pristine PopIII system uncovered by JWST`
  - **Question:** is JWST seeing an interaction between a hot nebular-continuum galaxy and a pristine Pop III-like companion?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/CAPERS spectroscopy and imaging of a galaxy at `z=5.124`
    - NIRSpec Prism spectrum from rest-frame UV to optical
    - A blue companion candidate offset by `3 kpc`
  - **Method:**
    - Fit the full spectrum with a nebular-continuum-dominated model
    - Test and reject a strong damped-`Lyalpha` absorption explanation for the UV turnover
    - Search the companion for hydrogen, helium, and metal-line signatures
  - **Conclusion:**
    - More than `95%` of the observed continuum is reproduced by nebular emission, not by a stellar continuum plus damped-`Lyalpha` absorption.
    - The main nebular region is extremely hot and dense, with `T = (5.3 +- 0.2) x 10^4 K` and `n_e = (5.4 +- 0.8) x 10^3 cm^-3`.
    - The companion shows hints of hydrogen and helium lines but no metal lines, which is why the paper argues for a possible Pop III-remnant-dominated source.
    - If the interpretation is correct, the companion may help power the excess nebular continuum in the main galaxy and implies a substantial role for very massive or Pop III stars at cosmic dawn.
  - **Position:** one of the more interesting Pop III-adjacent JWST papers because it is built from a full spectral fit rather than from broad-band colors alone

- [2603.13493](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.13493) Lilian L. Lee `The ALPINE-CRISTAL-JWST Survey: Gas-phase abundance gradients of main sequence star-forming galaxies and their kinematics at 4 < z < 6`
  - **Question:** what do gas-phase metallicity gradients look like in `4<z<6` main-sequence galaxies once JWST IFU maps and `[C II]` kinematics are combined?
  - **Data:**
    - `20` main-sequence galaxies at `4<z<6`
    - JWST/NIRSpec IFU radial metallicity maps
    - ALMA `[C II] 158 um` kinematics from the ALPINE-CRISTAL-JWST program
  - **Method:**
    - Derive radial oxygen-abundance profiles from rest-frame optical strong lines
    - Fit linear gradients to annular-binned profiles
    - Compare the gradients with intrinsic gas velocity dispersion and `V_rot / sigma_0`
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The sample has slightly positive metallicity gradients on average, with median `+0.039 +- 0.010 dex kpc^-1`.
    - Only `3` galaxies show gradients above `0.05 dex kpc^-1` at `1 sigma`, and none show a significant negative gradient.
    - The gradients correlate only weakly with gas velocity dispersion, but there is a shallow negative correlation with `V_rot / sigma_0`.
    - As `V_rot / sigma_0` rises toward later times, the result supports disk maturity as an important driver of the cosmic evolution of metallicity gradients.
  - **Position:** a useful resolved-chemistry paper because it connects early abundance gradients to disk maturity rather than treating them as standalone metallicity measurements
