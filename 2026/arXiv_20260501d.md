## Executive view

The 2026-05-01 batch is strongest for protocluster gas and AGN feedback. The best profile match is [2604.27071](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27071) Jaclyn B. Champagne, which uses ALMA CO(3-2) to study five dusty star-forming galaxies in the Spiderweb protocluster at `z=2.16`. The galaxies have disturbed molecular-gas kinematics, high gas masses, and declining gas fractions with proximity to the Spiderweb Galaxy, making this the day's most direct high-z environment paper.

The AGN-feedback cluster is unusually coherent. [2604.27159](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27159) Ming-Yi Lin resolves a compact molecular outflow in the Compton-thick AGN NGC 3079 and favors a jet-powered, energy-driven scenario. [2604.27332](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27332) Minhang Guo and [2604.27301](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27301) Minhang Guo use MACER simulations to show that jet-wind coupling suppresses star formation more efficiently than either channel alone. [2604.27061](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27061) Pierpaolo Condo reanalyzes IRAS 13224-3809 and confirms a rapidly variable ultra-fast outflow with `v>0.2c`.

The methods and environment context papers are also useful. [2604.27056](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27056) Noah S. J. Rogers gives a first empirical JWST/MIRI calibration of the mid-infrared abundance diagnostic `Ne_23`. [2604.27845](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27845) Zhuoming Zhang connects satellite anisotropy to central galaxies, halos, and large-scale filaments. [2604.27888](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27888) L. Pecoraro releases a deep multiwavelength Abell S1063 outskirts catalog for cluster-environment studies.

## High-z environments and gas diagnostics

- [2604.27071](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27071) Jaclyn B. Champagne et al. `Chaotic Molecular Gas in Five Dusty Star-forming Galaxies in the Spiderweb Protocluster at z = 2.16`
  - **Question:** how do molecular-gas content and kinematics behave in the dusty star-forming members of a mature `z=2.16` protocluster?
  - **Data:**
    - ALMA: CO(3-2) observations of five dusty star-forming galaxies
    - Environment: Spiderweb protocluster at `z=2.16`
    - Spatial context: galaxies within about `0.5-4 cMpc` of the Spiderweb core
    - Ancillary information: Halpha-detected companions and previous X-ray AGN identifications
  - **Method:**
    - Measure molecular-gas masses and CO kinematics from ALMA data
    - Fit classical disk models and inspect line-profile residuals
    - Compare gas fractions and depletion times with cosmic-noon field relations
    - Test trends with distance from the Spiderweb Galaxy
  - **Conclusion:**
    - All five galaxies show disturbed molecular-gas kinematics through non-Gaussian CO profiles, irregular morphology, and strong disk-model residuals.
    - The disturbed kinematics are consistent with an elevated merger rate in the protocluster outskirts.
    - Gas fractions and depletion times are similar to cosmic-noon field relations, so high SFRs are matched by high gas masses.
    - The most massive galaxies and all X-ray AGN in the sample have gas fractions `<30%`, below the sample average of `49%`.
    - Gas fractions and sSFR decline with distance from the Spiderweb Galaxy, supporting a reversed SFR-density-radius relation in this high-z protocluster.
  - **Position:** the day's highest-priority environment result because it connects cold gas, mergers, AGN, and radial trends inside a benchmark `z>2` protocluster

- [2604.27056](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27056) Noah S. J. Rogers et al. `The First Empirical Calibration of the MIR Abundance Diagnostic Ne_23 with JWST`
  - **Question:** can a mid-infrared neon diagnostic provide a lower-systematics gas-phase abundance measurement for dusty or metal-rich galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/MIRI MRS: ten H II regions from the CHAOS project
    - JWST/MIRI archival sample: eight low-metallicity galaxies
    - Calibration baseline: optical direct-method `T_e` and O/H measurements
    - Lines: `[Ne II] 12.81 um` and `[Ne III] 15.56 um`
  - **Method:**
    - Extract matched-aperture one-dimensional MIR spectra
    - Measure the `Ne_23` diagnostic from fine-structure neon lines
    - Calibrate `Ne_23` against direct-method oxygen abundance
    - Quantify scatter in O/H at fixed `Ne_23`
  - **Conclusion:**
    - `Ne_23` is strongly correlated with O/H across `1.5 dex` in `12+log(O/H)`.
    - The empirical relation has only `0.06 dex` scatter in O/H at fixed `Ne_23`.
    - The diagnostic is relatively insensitive to electron temperature compared with optical strong-line methods.
    - The calibration enables JWST/MIRI abundance surveys of ionized nebulae out to `z~0.8`.
  - **Position:** a useful calibration paper for future dusty and metal-rich galaxy work, even though the empirical sample is low-redshift

- [2604.27888](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27888) L. Pecoraro et al. `A New Perspective on Galactic Evolution: Studying the Outskirts of the Abell S1063 Galaxy Cluster`
  - **Question:** can deep wide-field photometry map galaxy properties and overdense structures in the outskirts of the intermediate-redshift cluster Abell S1063?
  - **Data:**
    - Target: Abell S1063 at `z=0.346`
    - VST-GAME and VISTA data: optical `u,g,r,i` plus near-infrared `Y,J,K_s`
    - Field: `1 x 1 deg^2`
    - Catalog: `64394` sources with multiwavelength photometry and photometric redshifts
  - **Method:**
    - Build a cleaned multiwavelength photometric catalog
    - Estimate photometric redshifts and identify cluster members
    - Reconstruct the density field across the wide field
    - Compare galaxy properties, colors, and masses across overdense regions
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The data reach `r<24.65 mag` over a wide cluster-outskirts field.
    - A dense structure is detected near the cluster center.
    - A second dense region is found to the northwest, opposite the main cluster elongation.
    - Filaments connecting the overdense regions are visible in the reconstructed field.
  - **Position:** a useful cluster-environment dataset for testing preprocessing and filament-linked galaxy evolution at intermediate redshift

## AGN feedback and outflows

- [2604.27159](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27159) Ming-Yi Lin et al. `Molecular Outflows in the Nucleus of the Nearby Compton-thick AGN NGC 3079`
  - **Question:** what drives the compact nuclear molecular outflow in the Compton-thick Seyfert 2 galaxy NGC 3079?
  - **Data:**
    - NOEMA: CO(2-1) observations of NGC 3079
    - Angular resolution: `0.5 arcsec`, about `40 pc`
    - Modeling tools: 3D-Barolo and DysmalPy disk models
    - Visibility data: high-velocity nuclear molecular component
  - **Method:**
    - Model the rotating molecular disk with mock 3D data cubes
    - Identify unresolved high-dispersion nuclear residuals
    - Measure the spatial offset and velocity range of the blueshifted component
    - Estimate molecular outflow rate, kinetic power, and momentum rate
  - **Conclusion:**
    - A blueshifted high-velocity component is offset by `0.17 arcsec`, about `14 pc`, from the continuum peak.
    - The component has `v-v_sys=-350` to `-450 km/s`.
    - The inferred molecular outflow rate is `8.82 Msun/yr`.
    - The outflow kinetic power is `3.8 x 10^41 erg/s`, and the momentum rate is `2.05 x 10^34 dyne`.
    - The momentum rate is about `15x` the AGN radiation momentum rate, favoring an energy-driven and likely jet-powered outflow.
  - **Position:** a compact low-z benchmark for molecular AGN feedback because it links parsec-scale radio activity to tens-of-parsec molecular gas removal

- [2604.27332](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27332) Minhang Guo et al. `Active Galactic Nucleus Feedback in an Elliptical Galaxy. IV. The Importance of the Jet Wind Coupling`
  - **Question:** how does adding jet feedback change AGN regulation in an elliptical-galaxy MACER simulation that already includes radiation and winds?
  - **Data:**
    - MACER framework: isolated elliptical galaxy feedback simulations
    - Feedback modes: `FullFeedback`, `JetOnly`, and `WindOnly`
    - Jet parameters: informed by small-scale GRMHD simulations
  - **Method:**
    - Run controlled simulations with jets, winds, and combined feedback
    - Compare time-averaged star-formation rates
    - Measure kinetic-energy dissipation through turbulence and shocks
    - Identify instabilities at the jet-wind interface
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Time-averaged SFRs are `10^-1`, `10^-2`, and `10^-3 Msun/yr` in `JetOnly`, `WindOnly`, and `FullFeedback`.
    - Jet feedback alone is less efficient than wind feedback because of small opening angle and low momentum flux.
    - Combined jet-wind feedback suppresses star formation more strongly than a linear sum of the single-channel effects.
    - AGN energy dissipation efficiencies are `0.64`, `0.48`, and `0.26` in `FullFeedback`, `WindOnly`, and `JetOnly`.
    - Kelvin-Helmholtz instability at the wind-jet shear layer drives turbulence and converts kinetic energy into heat.
  - **Position:** a useful AGN-feedback theory paper because it argues that feedback coupling between modes is more important than either mode in isolation

- [2604.27061](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27061) Pierpaolo Condo et al. `Unveiling the dynamics of the ultra-fast outflow in IRAS 13224-3809 with X-ray spectroscopy`
  - **Question:** what drives the fast and variable ultra-fast outflow in the narrow-line Seyfert 1 galaxy IRAS 13224-3809?
  - **Data:**
    - XMM-Newton: full 2016 dataset, `1.5 Ms`
    - NuSTAR: `500 ks`
    - Spectral selections: time- and flux-resolved intervals with equal-count binning
  - **Method:**
    - Reanalyze all intervals with uniform background treatment
    - Compare photoionized absorption, broad emission, and relativistic reflection models
    - Track UFO velocity, line width, and flux dependence
    - Test acceleration response to X-ray flares
  - **Conclusion:**
    - A strong, variable outflow with velocities `>0.2c` is confirmed.
    - Absorption models reveal a velocity-luminosity correlation and persistently large line widths.
    - Significant UFO detections remain in most intervals even when emission or reflection components reduce line significance.
    - The wind rapidly accelerates in response to X-ray flares.
    - The response favors magnetic driving, analogous to coronal mass ejections, over pure radiative acceleration.
  - **Position:** a high-value UFO paper because it connects wind dynamics, variability, and possible magnetic launching in a heavily monitored AGN

## Structure, satellites, and AGN-host measurements

- [2604.27845](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27845) Zhuoming Zhang et al. `Anisotropy of Satellite Galaxies-I: Contrasting Correlations with Central Galaxy, Host Halo, and Large-Scale Filament Structures`
  - **Question:** which structures control satellite-galaxy anisotropy on galaxy, halo, and filament scales?
  - **Data:**
    - Simulations: SIMBA, EAGLE, and IllustrisTNG-100
    - Tracers: satellite positions, central galaxy morphology, host halo triaxiality, and cosmic filaments
    - Redshift reach: alignments persist up to `z=1.5`
  - **Method:**
    - Measure satellite-distribution anisotropy relative to central galaxies and host halos
    - Compare alignment signals across radius and halo mass
    - Trace satellite trajectories in SIMBA to identify the dynamical origin of alignment transitions
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Satellite anisotropy is robustly aligned with the host-halo and central-galaxy major axis.
    - The alignment extends to filamentary structures outside the halo over several virial radii.
    - A `3 sigma` scale-dependent transition appears: central-galaxy morphology dominates at `<0.3 R_200c`, host halos at `0.3-2 R_200c`, and filaments beyond `2 R_200c`.
    - The mass dependence persists down to `M_200c~10^11 Msun`.
    - Satellite orbits erase primordial filament signals after accretion inside `<2 R_200c`.
  - **Position:** a useful LSS/environment paper because it links observed satellite anisotropy to a physical transition from local morphology to halo shape to cosmic-web structure

- [2604.27783](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27783) C. Aydar et al. `Robust AGN and host-galaxy decomposition in optical spectral fitting`
  - **Question:** can repeated optical spectra be decomposed into AGN and host components with stable host and black-hole measurements across changing activity states?
  - **Data:**
    - SDSS repeated spectra: `32` changing-look AGN plus `15` quasars and `15` galaxies with multiple spectra
    - Additional comparisons: eFEDS AGN photometry, Gemini spectra, and VLT spectra
    - Redshift range: `z<0.55`
  - **Method:**
    - Combine pPXF and PyQSOFit for optical stellar-plus-AGN spectral decomposition
    - Compare stellar mass, velocity dispersion, and black-hole mass across epochs
    - Test reliability as a function of AGN-to-total continuum fraction
    - Apply broad-line flux and FWHM quality cuts for single-epoch mass estimates
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Host-derived properties remain consistent when `f_AGN<0.8`.
    - Broad-line flux and FWHM detections at `3 sigma` are effective quality cuts for reliable single-epoch masses.
    - Measurements across epochs agree within uncertainties after quality cuts.
    - Many changing-look AGN show broad-line-region breathing, while some deviate in ways consistent with non-virialized transitions.
  - **Position:** a practical AGN-host measurement paper because robust decomposition is a prerequisite for interpreting black-hole growth and host demographics
