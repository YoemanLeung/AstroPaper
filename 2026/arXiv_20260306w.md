## Executive view

This week is centered on ionizing escape, LAE structure, and reionization diagnostics. The core thread for your interests is the three-paper LyC chain formed by [2603.01487](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01487) Shuairu Zhu, [2603.04517](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04517) Ilias Goovaerts, and [2603.05907](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05907) Shengzhe Wang: a new `z=4.444` candidate, a second `z=4.442` system with explicit Lyalpha-morphology tests, and a JWST/IFU confirmation-plus-refutation exercise at `z~3`.

The LAE and environment side is also unusually good. [2603.03570](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03570) Ronaldo Laishram gives a `z~4.9` LAE protocluster with a wavelength-dependent size signal, while [2603.06409](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06409) R. Tripodi uses stacked JWST spectroscopy of `287` LAEs to connect Lyalpha escape, metallicity, nitrogen enhancement, and bursty feedback.

The lower-redshift and theory bridges are still worth keeping. [2603.05584](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05584) Scott Lucchini is a good high-resolution CGM infrastructure paper, [2603.01882](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01882) J. A. Hernández-Guajardo shows arc-tomography absorption mapping of a `z~1` outflow to `~50 kpc`, and [2603.01668](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01668) Yingjie Peng gives a geometric LRD interpretation that is simple enough to test.

## LyC escape and reionization diagnostics

- [2603.01487](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01487) Shuairu Zhu `LCEz4-M1: A Lyman Continuum Emitter Candidate at z = 4.444 in the MUSE Hubble Ultra Deep Field`
  - **Question:** is LCEz4-M1 a genuine high-redshift Lyman-continuum emitter at `z=4.444`?
  - **Data:**
    - VLT/MUSE spectroscopy in the HUDF
    - HST/ACS `F435W` imaging
    - JWST imaging for the rest-frame optical morphology
  - **Method:**
    - Confirm the redshift from the `Lyalpha` emission line in the MUSE spectrum
    - Check for independent LyC detections in the MUSE spectrum and the `F435W` image
    - Use the combined HST, MUSE, and JWST data to infer `f_esc`, compactness, and environment
  - **Conclusion:**
    - LyC is detected independently at `~3.7 sigma` in `F435W` and `~2.8-3.0 sigma` in the MUSE spectrum, making this one of the few credible `z>4` candidates.
    - The LyC centroid is aligned with the JWST-traced rest-frame optical continuum to within `0.06 arcsec`, or `0.4 kpc`.
    - The inferred escape fractions are high, `f_esc(F435W) = 0.38^{+0.25}_{-0.15}` and `f_esc(MUSE) = 0.33^{+0.22}_{-0.13}`.
    - The galaxy is compact and starbursting, with `Sigma_SFR ~ 7 Msun yr^-1 kpc^-2`, and it sits in an overdense environment with a faint companion at `~0.5 arcsec`, which is consistent with feedback- or interaction-opened escape channels.
  - **Position:** a directly relevant LyC-leaker candidate, useful because it pushes credible LyC evidence to `z=4.444` with both photometric and spectroscopic support

- [2603.04517](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04517) Ilias Goovaerts `MXDFz4.4: A LyC emitter 250Myr after the epoch of reionization and a first test of Ly-alpha morphology as a tracer of LyC escape at high redshift`
  - **Question:** can Lyalpha morphology trace LyC escape in a galaxy only `~250 Myr` after the end of reionization?
  - **Data:**
    - MUSE eXtremely Deep Field observations of MXDFz4.4 at `z=4.442`
    - `F435W` imaging for direct LyC flux constraints
    - SED-fitting inputs plus UV structural diagnostics
  - **Method:**
    - Confirm the redshift from `Lyalpha`
    - Measure the LyC flux and correct it for intrinsic production and IGM opacity
    - Test low-z LyC tracers and Lyalpha morphological tracers, including the Lyalpha halo fraction
    - Use SED fitting and UV diagnostics to interpret how bursty star formation affects both LyC production and escape
  - **Conclusion:**
    - LyC is detected at `10.3 sigma` in `F435W`, with measured flux `4.2 +- 0.5 nJy`, corresponding to `8.0 sigma` after the adopted flux treatment.
    - The inferred escape fraction is very high, `~50-100%`, after correcting for intrinsic production and IGM attenuation.
    - The system requires a recent burst plus a more evolved stellar population, so the LyC leakage is not set by morphology alone.
    - Lyalpha-based morphology provides cautious support for the halo fraction as a high-z LyC tracer, but only once burst-driven variation in LyC production is accounted for.
  - **Position:** one of the more interesting LyC papers here because it tests Lyalpha morphology as a tracer rather than stopping at a simple detection claim

- [2603.05907](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05907) Shengzhe Wang `Confirmation and Refutation of Lyman Continuum Leakers at z~3 with JWST NIRSpec/IFU`
  - **Question:** which previously claimed `z~3` LyC leakers survive JWST/NIRSpec IFU scrutiny once sub-kpc contamination is removed?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec medium-resolution IFU observations
    - HST/WFC3 `F336W` pre-selection plus ground-based spectroscopy
    - Two candidate leakers at `z=3.1`: LACES-94460 and LACES-104037
  - **Method:**
    - Use IFU spectroscopy to distinguish genuine LyC emission from projected low-z interlopers
    - Model the surviving system with a picket-fence escape framework
    - Connect the confirmed LyC region to galaxy structure and merger state
  - **Conclusion:**
    - LACES-94460 is rejected as a LyC leaker because its apparent `F336W` signal comes from a nearby interloper at `z=1.6`.
    - LACES-104037 is confirmed as a bona fide LyC source, with the leakage arising in a tidal-tail structure during an early-stage merger.
    - The confirmed LyC region is reproduced by a very young stellar population of age `~5 Myr` with `f_esc ~ 99%` in the picket-fence model.
    - The paper is strong evidence that mergers can create localized, extreme LyC escape and that IFU-scale spatial resolution is essential for secure high-z LyC identification.
  - **Position:** a high-value calibration paper for the LyC field because it shows both how false positives arise and what a merger-driven confirmed leaker looks like

## LAEs, protoclusters, and stacked spectroscopy

- [2603.03570](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.03570) Ronaldo Laishram `Discovery of a z~eq 4.9 Lyman-α Emitter Protocluster: Wavelength-Dependent Environmental Effects on Galaxy Structure`
  - **Question:** what does a `z~4.9` LAE protocluster imply for environmental effects on early galaxy structure, especially in rest-frame optical light?
  - **Data:**
    - SILVERRUSH narrow-band LAE survey data in COSMOS
    - JWST COSMOS-Web `F150W` and `F277W` imaging
    - Morphology sample of `16` protocluster and `23` field LAEs
  - **Method:**
    - Identify the protocluster and map its multi-peak overdensity structure
    - Fit Sersic profiles in rest-frame UV and rest-frame optical bands
    - Compare size and Sersic-index distributions between protocluster and field LAEs at fixed stellar mass
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The structure contains `4` overdensity peaks across `~65 x 36 cMpc^2`, with the primary peak showing a `4x` surface-density enhancement inside `1.5 pMpc`.
    - Protocluster LAEs are `~40%` larger in rest-frame optical, with median `R_e = 0.81^{+0.26}_{-0.04} kpc` versus `0.58^{+0.11}_{-0.04} kpc` in the field and `p = 0.041`.
    - At fixed stellar mass, the protocluster sample is offset by `+0.12 dex`, or `~31%`, from the field optical size-mass relation.
    - No significant environmental difference appears in the rest-frame UV sizes or Sersic indices, which suggests the environment is affecting the extended stellar component rather than the compact UV-bright core.
  - **Position:** a directly relevant protocluster paper because it isolates a wavelength-dependent environmental effect on LAE structure at `z~5`

- [2603.06409](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.06409) R. Tripodi `The Collective Voice of Lyα Emitters: Insights from JWST Stacked Spectroscopy`
  - **Question:** what does stacked JWST spectroscopy of typical `z>4` LAEs reveal about Lyalpha escape, enrichment, and internal structure?
  - **Data:**
    - Public JWST/NIRSpec prism spectra for `287` LAEs at `z>4`
    - Combined CAPERS, CEERS, JADES, and RUBIES surveys
    - Spatially resolved `2D` spectral stack on sub-kpc scales
  - **Method:**
    - Build a two-dimensional stacked spectrum
    - Compare radial trends in resonant and non-resonant line equivalent widths
    - Derive metallicity, ionization, dust, `N/O`, and `xi_ion` from the stack
    - Compare the trends with SPICE radiation-hydrodynamic simulations
  - **Conclusion:**
    - `EW(Hbeta)` and other optical lines decline with radius, while `EW(Lyalpha)` rises outward and the Lyalpha escape fraction increases from `~16%` in the center to `>=24%` at larger radii.
    - Typical stacked LAEs are low metallicity systems with `12 + log(O/H) = 7.7 +- 0.2`, high ionization parameters, and negligible dust attenuation.
    - The stack also shows elevated nitrogen abundance with `log(N/O) ~ -0.4` and ionizing-photon production efficiency `log(xi_ion / Hz erg^-1) ~ 25.2`.
    - SPICE bursty-supernova-feedback models reproduce the radial trends, tying Lyalpha redistribution to stochastic star formation and feedback-driven gas flows.
  - **Position:** one of the more useful stacked-LAE papers here because it links Lyalpha escape, chemistry, and feedback in a way directly relevant to faint reionization-era emitters

- [2603.04542](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04542) Omer Zvi Katz `Beyond21: A Global Framework for Cosmic Dawn and Reionization Within and Beyond the Standard Model`
  - **Question:** can cosmic-dawn and reionization observables be modeled in a single fast global framework that also allows beyond-standard-model extensions?
  - **Data:**
    - Open-source Python package coupling Pop II and Pop III star-formation prescriptions to IGM evolution
  - **Method:**
    - Predict the global `21 cm` signal, UV luminosity functions, ionization history, and cosmic X-ray background in one self-consistent pipeline
    - Keep the architecture modular so astrophysical prescriptions and new physics can be swapped in easily
    - Demonstrate one extension with millicharged-dark-matter baryon cooling
  - **Conclusion:**
    - A full global-evolution run takes only `~0.1 s` on a single CPU core, so the package is practical for broad high-resolution parameter searches.
    - The framework unifies several observables that are often modeled separately, which reduces inconsistency across `21 cm`, UVLF, ionization-history, and CXB inference.
    - The modular design makes it straightforward to compare standard astrophysical scenarios with beyond-standard-model ones.
  - **Position:** a useful infrastructure paper for global cosmic-dawn and reionization inference, especially when several probes must be modeled consistently

## Gas flows and morphology interpretation

- [2603.05584](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05584) Scott Lucchini `ENhanced Galactic Atmospheres With Arepo: Resolving the CGM at 200 pc with the ENGAWA Simulations`
  - **Question:** what changes in CGM structure and ion columns when Milky-Way-like halos are resolved to `200 pc` instead of standard mass-based refinement levels?
  - **Data:**
    - `4` cosmological zoom-in simulations of Milky-Way-like galaxies
    - Fixed-volume refinement throughout the CGM down to `200 pc`
    - Post-processed radiative transfer with `COLT`
  - **Method:**
    - Replace purely mass-based refinement with fixed-volume CGM refinement
    - Measure the response of `H I`, `Mg II`, `O VI`, and cold-cloud statistics
    - Recompute ion populations with stellar radiation included in post-processing
  - **Conclusion:**
    - `200 pc` refinement increases low-ion column densities in `H I` and `Mg II` and produces more cold clouds, helping relieve some long-standing simulation-observation tensions.
    - Adding radiative transfer reduces `H I` while changing `Mg II` and `O VI` only weakly, which tempers but does not erase the resolution gain.
    - Higher resolution shrinks the intermediate-temperature boundary layers around clouds, producing smoother transitions to the ambient CGM temperature.
  - **Position:** a useful CGM infrastructure paper for anyone who cares about how unresolved cloud structure biases simulated low-ion observables

- [2603.01882](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01882) J. A. Hernández-Guajardo `A z~1 galactic-scale outflow transversally mapped to ~50 kpc through gravitational-arc tomography`
  - **Question:** can a galactic outflow be mapped transversely across tens of kiloparsecs rather than inferred from a single down-the-barrel sightline?
  - **Data:**
    - Star-forming foreground galaxy at `z~1`
    - Background gravitational arc at `z~2.4` creating `7` transverse sightlines spaced by `~6 kpc`
    - `Mg II` and `Fe II` absorption measurements out to `~50 kpc`
  - **Method:**
    - Measure absorption along the minor axis in both down-the-barrel and transverse geometries
    - Deproject the line-of-sight velocities to infer the outflow geometry and boundness
    - Compare the mapped outflow with earlier major-axis arc-tomography results
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The mapped outflow shows blue-shifted `Mg II` absorption in both down-the-barrel and transverse sightlines, which confirms a large-scale collimated wind.
    - The measured line-of-sight velocities are `~62-239 km s^-1` with dispersions `~53-133 km s^-1`, so the structure is dominated by bulk motion.
    - The inferred opening angle is `~18-25 deg`, the mass outflow rate is `>=0.06 Msun yr^-1`, and the mass loading factor is `>=0.004` between `~10` and `50 kpc`.
    - The deprojected speeds only barely approach the escape velocity, so the outflow material is likely still gravitationally bound.
  - **Position:** a directly useful CGM-kinematics paper because it shows how gravitational-arc tomography can map wind geometry across multiple transverse sightlines

- [2603.01668](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01668) Yingjie Peng `A Unified Explanation for JWST Little Red Dots and High-Redshift Low-Mass Disk-like Galaxies: Prolate Galaxies Viewed End-on vs Side-on`
  - **Question:** are some high-z disk-like galaxies and LRDs the same intrinsically prolate population viewed from different angles?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST morphology statistics for flattened low-mass `z>3` galaxies
    - LRD number counts and spectral-selection phenomenology at `z~4-9`
  - **Method:**
    - Assume many low-mass high-z galaxies are intrinsically prolate rather than oblate disks
    - Map side-on and end-on projections to disk-like and LRD-like observed classes
    - Compare the expected end-on fraction with observed LRD demographics
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Side-on and intermediate-angle projections of prolate systems can mimic the unexpectedly abundant flattened low-mass galaxies at `z>3`.
    - Rare near end-on views become compact, high-surface-brightness, and heavily column-reddened, which naturally reproduces several LRD selection properties.
    - The expected near-end-on fraction is only `~1-3%` for `theta_max ~ 10-15 deg`, which matches the rarity of LRDs in wide JWST fields.
    - The model does not exclude AGN or starbursts, but it removes the need for a special new LRD-only population and makes direct geometric predictions that can be tested.
  - **Position:** a simple geometric alternative in the LRD debate, useful because it ties LRD rarity to the same axis-ratio evidence used for low-mass high-z morphology studies
