## Executive view

This week is stronger than usual in papers that connect gas physics to environment. The most coherent chain for your core interests is [2604.13159](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13159) M. Galbiati, [2604.13162](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13162) Paryag Sharma, and [2604.14273](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14273) Imran Sultan: one resolved quasar-CGM field, one large absorber-coincidence measurement, and one theory paper that explains when cold versus hot accretion should dominate.

The environment and quenching side is also unusually good. [2604.11942](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11942) Takumi Kakimoto extends small-scale environmental quenching out to `z=4.53`, while [2604.11892](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11892) Mingyu Li provides a rare direct stripping case at `z=3.06`. For dusty growth and structural interpretation, [2604.13408](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13408) Juno Li and [2604.11879](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11879) S. J. McKay are useful calibration papers.

On the high-z and reionization side, [2604.13267](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13267) Kanak Saha, [2604.11899](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11899) Stefano Carniani, and [2604.13000](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13000) Zhaoran Liu are the main papers to track: escaping ionizing photons from faint LAEs, strong outflow evidence in a `z=14.18` galaxy, and a clean time-domain constraint on LRD central engines.

## Quasars, CGM, and gas coherence

- [2604.13159](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13159) M. Galbiati et al. `Resolving circumgalactic gas flows around a z~3.6 quasar using MUSE and ALMA`
  - **Question:** is the gas around the `z~3.66` quasar MQN04 inflowing, stripped, or outflowing, and how does that gas connect to the surrounding overdensity?
  - **Data:**
    - VLT/MUSE WFM + NFM: Lyα, C IV, and non-resonant He II emission, with kinematic information down to `~1 kpc`
    - ALMA Band 3: CO(4-3) and 3 mm continuum for the quasar systemic redshift and cold-gas context
    - HST imaging: rest-frame UV/optical morphology
    - UVES quasar spectrum: H I absorption along the sightline
    - Spectroscopic/photometric galaxy census in the surrounding field
  - **Method:**
    - Use ALMA CO(4-3) to anchor the quasar systemic redshift
    - Compare Lyα, C IV, and He II morphology and velocity fields
    - Map He II/Lyα line ratios and assess radiative-transfer effects
    - Identify nearby galaxies and estimate the large-scale overdensity around the quasar
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The CGM is highly asymmetric, with clumpy plus diffuse emission extending `~100 kpc` and blueshifted by roughly `0-800 km s^-1` relative to the CO-based systemic redshift.
    - The surrounding medium is highly ionized, supported by the He II/Lyα ratio, the low-column H I absorber (`~10^14.6 cm^-2`), and broadly consistent velocity shifts across tracers outside the nucleus.
    - The extended He II structure is more consistent with merger-driven stripping or quasar-illuminated inflow than with a simple symmetric outflow.
    - MQN04 lies in an extreme environment, with overdensity `delta~41` and five star-forming galaxies within `|Delta v_QSO| <= 1000 km s^-1`.
  - **Position:** extends `Borisova+2016` and `Guo+2020` with ALMA systemic-redshift anchoring in a dense quasar environment already highlighted by `Pensabene+2024`

- [2604.13162](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13162) Paryag Sharma et al. `Investigating the Circumgalactic Medium through Mg II absorption coincidence`
  - **Question:** over what transverse scales does cool Mg II-bearing CGM remain coherent across neighboring quasar sightlines?
  - **Data:**
    - SDSS: `9204` absorber-centric quasar sightline pairs with projected separations from `~50 kpc` to `~1 Mpc`
    - Photometric galaxy counts around the absorber sample
  - **Method:**
    - Measure Mg II coincidence probability as a function of projected separation
    - Compare the small-scale signal with a single-halo geometric model
    - Test the large-scale signal against galaxy clustering expectations
    - Stack paired sightlines without individual detections to recover weak equivalent-width excess
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Coincidence probability rises steeply to `~5-8%` below `~100 kpc`, then falls to a `~1-2%` plateau out to `~1 Mpc`.
    - The inner signal is consistent with halo-scale coherence, while the megaparsec-scale plateau is consistent with surrounding galaxy clustering rather than the same halo.
    - Stacked non-detections still show excess Mg II absorption, implying a cool metal-enriched coherence scale of `~100-200 kpc`.
  - **Position:** extends the paired-sightline coherence work of `Rogerson+2012`, `Rubin+2015`, and `Afruni+2023` to a much larger statistical Mg II sample

- [2604.13178](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13178) T. Mkrtchyan et al. `4MOST ChANGES: Catalog of high-redshift quasar candidates (4.5 < z < 7) selected with SED fitting`
  - **Question:** can multiwavelength SED fitting build a large southern-sky catalog of `z>4.5` quasar candidates for spectroscopic follow-up?
  - **Data:**
    - DELVE DR2 + DECaLS DR10: optical photometry
    - VHS DR5 plus VIKING coverage: near-infrared photometry
    - AllWISE: mid-infrared photometry
    - Gaia DR3: optical astrometry
    - NTT/EFOSC2 and Palomar/NGPS: spectroscopic validation
  - **Method:**
    - Build a matched multi-band catalog
    - Apply morphology and color cuts to reduce contaminants
    - Fit quasar and brown-dwarf templates to the photometry
    - Rank candidates with `chi^2`, BIC, and F-test criteria
    - Validate a subset spectroscopically
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The final catalog contains `6104` candidates in `4.5 < z < 7` with detections in at least seven bands and SED-selection cuts of `BIC > 0` and `F-test > 10`.
    - Initial follow-up confirmed `3` high-redshift quasars at `z>5` from `6` observed candidates.
  - **Position:** extends the high-redshift quasar selection programs of `Bañados+2016`, `Bañados+2023`, and `Wang+2021` into a 4MOST-ready southern target catalog

- [2604.13154](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13154) Zirui Chen et al. `From Clumps to Sheets: Geometry Controls the Temperature PDF of Multi-Phase Gas`
  - **Question:** are temperature PDFs in multiphase gas set mainly by microphysics or by the geometry of the cold-hot interface?
  - **Data:**
    - 3D hydrodynamic simulations under matched ISM-like and CGM-like cooling conditions
    - Planar mixing-layer runs and turbulent-box runs with the same microphysics
  - **Method:**
    - Compare temperature PDFs from sheet-like interfaces and clumpy turbulent media
    - Decompose the PDF into isosurface area times layer thickness
    - Track how interface morphology changes from clumps to connected sheets
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The temperature PDF is not universal under fixed cooling physics; geometry changes it strongly.
    - Layer thickness is controlled mainly by microphysics, but isosurface area is set by morphology.
    - Clump-to-sheet transitions broaden the PDF and boost the intermediate-temperature gas fraction.
    - The same geometric effect offers a route to explain large O VI reservoirs in the CGM and X-ray-Halpha correlations in stripped tails.
  - **Position:** reframes the mixing-layer interpretation developed in `Tan+2021` and `Chen+2023`, arguing that interface geometry, not only microphysics, controls the temperature PDF

- [2604.14273](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.14273) Imran Sultan et al. `Cold vs. Hot Gas Accretion and Angular Momentum in FIRE Simulations: From Halo to Galaxy Scales`
  - **Question:** how do CGM virialization, inflow temperature, and angular momentum transport change the way gas reaches galaxies across the `~10^12 Msun` halo transition?
  - **Data:**
    - High-resolution FIRE cosmological simulations spanning halo masses around the `~10^12 Msun` scale
  - **Method:**
    - Classify inflows as cold (`T < 10^5 K`) or hot (`T > 10^5 K`)
    - Relate inflow temperature to inner-CGM virialization state
    - Track specific angular momentum, circularization, and time-to-star-formation after accretion
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Pre-virialized halos are fed almost entirely by cold inflows, while virialized halos are dominated by hot inflows even when cold streams still coexist at high redshift.
    - Cold inflows carry higher specific angular momentum than dark matter and hot gas, but in bursty low-mass galaxies they usually do not circularize before forming stars.
    - In steady massive galaxies, hot inflows circularize and cool near galaxy radii, then form stars with disk-like kinematics.
    - Gas turns into stars much faster in bursty systems (`<~5` free-fall times) than in steady systems (`up to ~25` free-fall times).
  - **Position:** revisits the cold-stream picture of `Dekel+2009` in FIRE-era halos and connects directly to the disk-formation and hot-CGM cooling-flow interpretations in `Yu+2023` and `Sultan+2025`

## Environment, quenching, and obscured growth

- [2604.11942](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11942) Takumi Kakimoto et al. `The role of small-scale environments in the quenching of massive galaxies at 1<z<5`
  - **Question:** how strongly is massive-galaxy quenching tied to nearby companions and small-scale overdensity at `1<z<5`?
  - **Data:**
    - Subaru/FOCAS: spectroscopy of a quiescent-galaxy environment at `z=4.53`
    - COSMOS2020-based sample: photometric environmental statistics for massive galaxies
  - **Method:**
    - Spectroscopically confirm the redshift of a massive quiescent galaxy from the Lyman break
    - Identify physically associated neighbors within the same small-scale environment
    - Select quiescent galaxies with SFR-based criteria in COSMOS
    - Compare quiescent fractions across field, group-like, and cluster-like environments and across different projected scales
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The `z=4.53` quiescent galaxy is associated with at least three star-forming galaxies within `150 pkpc`.
    - The quiescent fraction is higher in group-like or cluster-like environments across `1<z<5`.
    - The strongest excess of quiescent galaxies at `z>2` appears on `<=100-300 pkpc` scales, supporting mergers and close interactions as major quenching channels.
  - **Position:** extends the environmental-quenching sequence from `Darvish+2016`, `Ito+2023`, and `Tanaka+2024` to `<=100-300 pkpc` scales anchored by a spectroscopic `z=4.53` group

- [2604.11892](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11892) Mingyu Li et al. `A Post-starburst Galaxy Undergoing Ram-pressure Stripping at Redshift 3.06`
  - **Question:** can ram-pressure stripping shut down star formation in group environments already at `z>3`?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRCam: imaging of A2744-JF-z3 and its `z=3.06` group environment
    - JWST/NIRSpec prism: galaxy and tail spectroscopy
    - ALMA: cold-gas and dust constraints
    - Chandra: X-ray context for the group field
  - **Method:**
    - Use JWST imaging to identify the group geometry and stripped tail morphology
    - Use NIRSpec diagnostics to confirm extended ionized-gas emission
    - Fit photometry and spectroscopy with BAGPIPES to derive post-starburst properties
    - Perform spatially resolved SED fitting to map dust attenuation and stripping signatures
  - **Conclusion:**
    - A2744-JF-z3 is a post-starburst group member at `z=3.06` with an extended emission tail of `~5 kpc` and a projected separation of `~60 kpc` from the central dusty spiral group member.
    - Ongoing removal of cold gas and dust is directly linked to the abrupt shutdown of star formation.
    - Ram-pressure stripping operates at `z>3` in a clumpy, filamentary intragroup medium, earlier than many simulations predict.
  - **Position:** pushes the ram-pressure-stripping framework summarized by `Cortese+2021` and `Boselli+2022` into the early group regime where `Morishita+2025` only gave indirect environmental-quenching evidence

- [2604.11879](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11879) S. J. McKay et al. `A Deep ALMA Survey of the Redshift Distribution of Dusty Star-forming Galaxies`
  - **Question:** what is the true redshift distribution of bright `870 um`-selected DSFGs once the sample is pushed to high spectroscopic completeness?
  - **Data:**
    - ALMA linescans of `870 um`-selected DSFGs in GOODS-S
    - Literature spectroscopic redshifts
    - JWST/NIRSpec coverage for part of the sample
  - **Method:**
    - Identify or confirm `20` spectroscopic redshifts from the new ALMA follow-up
    - Combine with literature values to build the most complete sample
    - Model the full redshift distribution above the flux-complete threshold
    - Benchmark multiple photometric-redshift methods against the spectroscopic sample
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The sample reaches `69%` spectroscopic completeness overall (`52/75`) and `97%` completeness above `f_870 > 2.5 mJy`.
    - All tested photometric-redshift methods show outlier fractions above `20%` and tend to overestimate the high-redshift tail.
    - The true abundance of massive dusty galaxies falls quickly at early times, with `<=10%` of `f_870 >= 2 mJy` DSFGs at `z>4` and `<=2%` at `z>5`.
  - **Position:** sharpens the DSFG redshift-distribution work of `Simpson+2014` and `Chen+2022`, arguing that the `z>4` tail is much smaller than photometric estimates suggest

- [2604.13408](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13408) Juno Li et al. `ALESS--JWST: Dust-driven Morphologies and Hidden Stellar Mass in z~3 Sub-millimeter Galaxies`
  - **Question:** how much do dust geometry and wavelength-dependent structure bias stellar-mass and morphology measurements in `z~3` SMGs?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRCam + MIRI: imaging of `12` ALESS SMGs at `z~3`
    - ALMA `870 um`: `0.08"-0.16"` resolution dust-continuum maps
  - **Method:**
    - Perform spatially resolved SED fitting on `~kpc` scales
    - Compare resolved results with integrated SED fits
    - Measure morphology and size changes as a function of rest-frame wavelength
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The galaxies follow a resolved star-forming main sequence linking SFR surface density to stellar-mass surface density.
    - Integrated fits systematically miss stellar mass because of a dust-obscuration bias, not simply classical outshining.
    - Rest-frame `~1.5-3 um` MIRI imaging reveals compact stellar structures that align with the `870 um` dust continuum more closely than NIRCam-based morphologies do.
    - The intrinsic stellar and dust sizes are consistent within uncertainties (`R_e,870 / R_e,* = 1.0 +- 0.4`), supporting compact obscured core growth toward massive quiescent descendants.
  - **Position:** extends `Hodge+2025` and `Gillman+2024`, adding MIRI-based stellar-structure constraints to the hidden-mass problem already highlighted by `Le Bail+2024`

## Reionization, extreme JWST populations, and survey methods

- [2604.13267](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13267) Kanak Saha et al. `The Great Escape of ionizing photons during Cosmic Morning`
  - **Question:** what do stacked faint LAEs at `z~6` imply about ionizing-photon escape and the sources of reionization?
  - **Data:**
    - AstroSat + HST: deep UV imaging
    - VLT/MUSE + JWST/NIRSpec: spectroscopically confirmed LAE sample in the HUDF
  - **Method:**
    - Stack the UV imaging of faint LAEs at `5.9 < z < 6.0`
    - Measure ionizing flux at rest-frame `350`, `392`, and `485 Angstrom`
    - Infer UV slope, ionizing production efficiency, and escape fraction from the stacked signal
    - Use individual source measurements to constrain ages, metallicities, and stellar populations
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Ionizing photons are directly detected from stacked faint LAEs at rest-frame `350`, `392`, and `485 Angstrom`.
    - The stacked population is faint (`M_UV = -18.77 +- 0.05`) but efficient, with `beta = -2.3 +- 0.1`, `log10 xi_ion^true = 25.86 +- 0.02`, and escape fraction `f_esc ~ 0.8`.
    - Individual galaxies show very blue continua, very young ages, and low metallicities, consistent with hot stellar populations capable of driving reionization.
    - Photons above `24.6 eV` imply that He I reionization has already begun by this epoch.
  - **Position:** connects the low-redshift LyC-leaker work of `Izotov+2016` to the faint-galaxy reionization arguments of `Naidu+2020` and `Atek+2024`, but with a direct stacked detection at `z~6`

- [2604.11899](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.11899) Stefano Carniani et al. `Intense and extended CIII] emission suggests a strong outflow in JADES-GS-z14-0`
  - **Question:** does spatially offset C III] emission in JADES-GS-z14-0 reveal powerful feedback in one of the brightest known galaxies at `z>14`?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec MSA PRISM/CLEAR: JADES and OASIS spectroscopy of JADES-GS-z14-0
    - JWST/NIRCam medium-band imaging for spatial constraints on the C III] region
  - **Method:**
    - Compare C III] detections between the JADES and OASIS pointings
    - Use the shutter offset to infer the displacement between stellar continuum and carbon-line emission
    - Use the non-detection in medium-band imaging to constrain the extent of the emitting region
    - Convert the inferred gas properties into outflow rate, mass-loading factor, and star-formation-efficiency limits
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The OASIS spectrum detects C III] at `10 sigma`, with luminosity three times higher than in the JADES data because the line-emitting gas is spatially offset by `~400 pc`.
    - The C III] region is extended on scales `>=165 pc`, supporting a diffuse carbon-enriched outflow interpretation.
    - The inferred mass outflow rate is `~160 Msun yr^-1`, with mass-loading factor `eta = 4-15`.
    - If outflows regulate the system, the instantaneous star-formation efficiency is limited to `epsilon_* <= 0.08`.
  - **Position:** extends the early-galaxy feedback picture built in `Carniani+2017`, `Bunker+2023`, and `Carniani+2025` with resolved evidence for a `z=14.18` carbon-enriched outflow

- [2604.13000](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.13000) Zhaoran Liu et al. `How I Wonder What You Are -- JWST's Little Red Dots do not TWINKLE`
  - **Question:** do LRDs vary like ordinary AGN when monitored in both spectroscopy and photometry?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST TWINKLE slitless spectroscopy in the FRESCO GOODS-N field
    - Complete Halpha-flux-limited sample of `18` LRDs at `3.9 < z < 6.8`
    - Comparison sample of `9` non-LRD broad-Halpha emitters in the same field
  - **Method:**
    - Monitor photometry, Halpha line flux, and line shape over `~140-220` rest-frame days
    - Compare the observed non-variability with reverberation-mapping AGN expectations
    - Test alternative variability scenarios with simulated light curves
  - **Conclusion:**
    - No variability is detected in photometry, Halpha flux, or line shape across the `18` LRDs.
    - If the LRDs behaved like the AGN used in standard reverberation-mapping calibrations, more than `10` should have varied; observing none is a `5.9 sigma` deficit.
    - The same non-detections hold for the `9` non-LRD broad-Halpha emitters in the field.
    - A simple sub-Eddington low-z-like AGN interpretation is disfavored; the data are more consistent with super-Eddington accretion, variability suppression, or no AGN at all.
  - **Position:** adds the first strong time-domain test to the LRD and infant-AGN sequence of `Matthee+2023`, `Kokorev+2024`, and `Maiolino+2024`

- [2604.12414](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12414) Shiro Mukae et al. `Enhancing Lyα Emitter Identification in HETDEX with a Convolutional Neural Network`
  - **Question:** can low-S/N LAE identification in HETDEX be pushed deeper without losing control of contamination?
  - **Data:**
    - HETDEX COSMOS catalog: training sample
    - Two-dimensional spectral images of single-line detections
    - Ancillary validation data plus the `Dark Energy Explorers` participatory-science labels
    - DESI spectroscopy of HETDEX candidates for independent testing
  - **Method:**
    - Train a CNN on low-resolution 2D spectral-image cutouts
    - Focus on the difficult `4.8 <= S/N <= 5.5` regime
    - Evaluate balanced accuracy, precision, and recall at high and low S/N
    - Use visual-attribution maps to identify what image features drive the classification
    - Apply the model to the full HETDEX catalog and inspect the resulting redshift distribution
  - **Conclusion:**
    - In the high-S/N regime, the model reaches balanced accuracy `94.1%`, precision `97.5%`, and recall `97.5%`.
    - In the low-S/N regime, it still reaches balanced accuracy `85.1%`, precision `78.2%`, and recall `84.4%`.
    - DESI-identified LAEs are recovered at `99%` in the high-S/N sample and `93%` in the low-S/N sample.
    - Applying the CNN suppresses spurious spikes in the `z~1.9-2.5` distribution and makes an effective threshold of `S/N = 4.8` usable for cosmological LAE work.
  - **Position:** extends the HETDEX classification infrastructure of `Mentuch Cooper+2023` and `Davis+2023` with the machine-learning direction opened by `House+2023`
