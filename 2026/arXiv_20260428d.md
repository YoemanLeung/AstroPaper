## Executive view

The 2026-04-28 batch is strong for reionization triage. The highest-priority paper is [2604.23823](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23823) Hakim Atek, which uses deep lensed JWST/GLIMPSE imaging to push the `z=6-9` UV luminosity function to `M_UV=-12` and finds no turnover down to `M_UV=-12.3`. Its inferred ionizing emissivity implies that very faint galaxies can maintain reionization even for a highly clumped IGM.

The most important cautionary high-z paper is [2604.23668](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23668) Marusa Bradac. Two apparently exciting `z>15` dropout candidates in Bullet Cluster JWST imaging are instead ultra-cool Y dwarfs confirmed with NIRSpec spectra and proper motions. This is directly relevant to extreme-dropout reliability.

The LRD / early black-hole interpretation slot is filled by [2604.22924](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22924) Antti Rantala, which connects dense assembling star clusters, runaway formation of extremely massive or supermassive stars, embedded stellar black holes, and faint multiple Little Red Dot systems. It is theoretical, but it gives a concrete alternative channel to compare with direct-collapse and accreting-BH interpretations.

For IGM and feedback work, [2604.24144](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24144) Shouvik Sarkar gives improved MWA `z=8.2` 21-cm upper limits, [2604.22922](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22922) Laura Borrelli finds local ultra-fast AGN outflows with velocities above `0.3c`, [2604.24200](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24200) Sara Santoni tests cosmic-web filament recovery from gas and Compton-y maps, and [2604.24494](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24494) Zhijie Zhang highlights missing extended hot gas in TNG50 Milky Way analogues.

## Reionization frontier and early compact populations

- [2604.23823](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23823) Hakim Atek et al. `A GLIMPSE of the 99%: a census of the faintest galaxies during the epoch reionization and its implications for galaxy formation models`
  - **Question:** does the reionization-era UV luminosity function turn over at the faint magnitudes expected from radiative feedback or star-formation thresholds?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/GLIMPSE: deep lensed imaging of galaxies at `z=6-9`
    - Gravitational lensing: effective UVLF reach to `M_UV=-12`, about three magnitudes deeper than previous robust constraints
    - Modeling inputs: statistical and systematic lensing uncertainties in the UVLF determination
  - **Method:**
    - Construct the UV luminosity function with end-to-end uncertainty propagation
    - Test for faint-end turnover against galaxy-formation and cosmological-simulation predictions
    - Compare the faint population with models calibrated to the JWST UV-bright excess at `z>10`
    - Convert the UVLF into ionizing emissivity under reionization assumptions
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The `z~7` UVLF continues rising steeply with faint-end slope `alpha=-1.98^{+0.06}_{-0.05}`.
    - No clear turnover is found down to `M_UV=-12.3`.
    - A luminosity-function truncation at `M_UV >= -15` is effectively ruled out by the detected faint population.
    - The inferred `z=7` ionizing emissivity is `log(n_ion / s^-1 Mpc^-3)~50.85`, enough to maintain reionization even for `C_HII=5`.
    - Post-JWST models tuned to the bright `z>10` excess fail to reproduce the lower-redshift, faint-end evolution.
  - **Position:** the day's core reionization paper because it puts the faint galaxy population, not only UV-bright JWST sources, back at the center of the ionizing-budget problem

- [2604.23668](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23668) Marusa Bradac et al. `Two Exciting High-redshift Galaxy Candidates Turn Out to Be Two Exciting Ultra-cool Brown Dwarfs`
  - **Question:** can some apparent `z>15` JWST dropout candidates instead be cold Milky Way brown dwarfs?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRCam: Bullet Cluster candidates selected as F277W and F356W dropouts
    - JWST/NIRSpec: follow-up spectra of two candidates
    - JWST/NIRCam repeat imaging: proper-motion baseline of about `1 yr`
  - **Method:**
    - Fit the NIRSpec spectra with ultra-cool Y dwarf templates
    - Use repeat imaging to measure proper motions
    - Estimate distances and sky density for this contaminant population
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Both high-redshift candidates are well fit by Y dwarf templates rather than galaxy spectra.
    - The inferred temperatures are `350^{+110}_{-80} K` and `410^{+110}_{-50} K`.
    - Both objects lie at distances of about `500 pc`.
    - Proper motions of `49 +/- 8 mas/yr` and `24 +/- 3 mas/yr` support a Galactic stellar interpretation.
    - The inferred sky density is `0.14 Y dwarfs/arcmin^2`, with higher contamination risk at low Galactic latitude.
  - **Position:** a high-priority quality-control result for `z>15` JWST searches because it shows that extreme red dropout colors alone are not enough without spectra or astrometric checks

- [2604.22924](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22924) Antti Rantala `Supermassive stars with embedded stellar black hole cores: dense assembling star clusters as faint multiple Little Red Dot systems`
  - **Question:** can dense high-redshift star clusters form supermassive stars with embedded stellar black holes that appear as faint multiple Little Red Dot systems?
  - **Data:**
    - Direct simulations: assembling dense star clusters with post-Newtonian black-hole dynamics and stellar evolution
    - Physical regime: proto-globular-cluster-like densities comparable to high-z JWST compact systems
    - Target comparison: faint multiple LRD populations discovered with JWST
  - **Method:**
    - Simulate runaway stellar collisions that build extremely massive and supermassive stars
    - Track mass segregation and relaxation of stellar black holes inside the influence region of the massive star
    - Follow interactions between stellar black holes and the gaseous layers of the extremely massive or supermassive star
    - Compare resulting sizes and masses with observed faint multiple LRD systems
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Cluster surface densities `Sigma_h >= 10^6 Msun pc^-2` can build extremely massive stars with `m_star>1000 Msun` or supermassive stars with `m_star>10000 Msun`.
    - Stellar black holes with `m_bh <= 60 Msun` can become embedded within the gaseous layers of those stars.
    - The quasi-star-like embedded-BH phase can last much longer than the lifetime of the supermassive star itself.
    - Assembly-region sizes of about `100 pc` and quasi-star masses `>=10^4 Msun` are consistent with faint multiple LRD systems.
  - **Position:** a useful LRD interpretation paper because it adds a dense-cluster channel that differs from both normal stellar populations and direct accreting-SMBH explanations

- [2604.24144](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24144) Shouvik Sarkar et al. `The Tracking Tapered Gridded Estimator for the 21-cm power spectrum from the Murchison Widefield Array (MWA) drift scan observations -- III. Improved upper limits at z = 8.2 from multiple pointings`
  - **Question:** how much can multiple MWA drift-scan pointings improve 21-cm power-spectrum upper limits at `z=8.2`?
  - **Data:**
    - MWA: zenith-pointing drift scans at `nu_c=154.2 MHz`
    - Survey geometry: `163` pointing centers spaced by `0.5 deg` across `349.0 deg <= RA <= 70.0 deg`
    - Target signal: EoR 21-cm power spectrum at `z=8.2`
  - **Method:**
    - Apply the Tracking Tapered Gridded Estimator to measure angular brightness-temperature fluctuations
    - Identify foreground-contaminated pointings, especially Fornax A near `RA~50.5 deg`
    - Select relatively foreground-free pointings and incoherently combine `23` of them
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The best single pointing gives a `2 sigma` upper limit `Delta^2_UL(k)=(173.13)^2 mK^2` at `k=0.161 Mpc^-1`.
    - The combined `23` pointings give `Delta^2_UL(k)=(98.67)^2 mK^2` at `k=0.156 Mpc^-1`.
    - The combined limit is about `3x` lower than earlier MWA limits.
    - The limit remains about `2x` higher than LOFAR, `21x` higher than HERA, and about `3` orders of magnitude above theoretical predictions.
  - **Position:** important for reionization measurement context because it clarifies where MWA currently sits relative to LOFAR, HERA, and theory

## AGN feedback, black-hole masses, and mid-IR kinematics

- [2604.22922](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22922) Laura Borrelli et al. `Discovery of ultra-fast outflows with v_out>0.3 c in local bright active galactic nuclei`
  - **Question:** how common are very high-velocity X-ray ultra-fast outflows in bright local type 1 AGN when the search extends to `12 keV`?
  - **Data:**
    - XMM-Newton: `33` flux-limited observations of local `z<0.2` type 1 AGN
    - X-ray spectra: modeled over `2-12 keV`
    - UFO search band: `7-12 keV`
  - **Method:**
    - Fit direct-continuum and reflection components to the X-ray spectra
    - Search for blueshifted absorption features from highly ionized gas
    - Use Monte Carlo simulations to assess line significance
    - Compare UFO incidence and velocity with accretion properties
  - **Conclusion:**
    - UFOs are detected in six sources above the `95%` Monte Carlo confidence level.
    - The detected fraction is `18%` for this sample.
    - Some wind velocities exceed `40%` of the speed of light.
    - The velocity distribution is shifted to higher energies than previous searches limited to `10 keV`.
    - No correlation is found between SMBH accretion properties and the presence of winds.
  - **Position:** a useful AGN-feedback reference because it shows that local UFO demographics depend strongly on hard-band search depth and that high-velocity winds can be temporally variable

- [2604.24721](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24721) Shu Wang et al. `Dynamical Modeling of the Broad-Line Region with High-Mass Active Galactic Nuclei and Constraints on the Virial Factor`
  - **Question:** how should virial factors for black-hole mass estimates be calibrated in high-mass reverberation-mapped AGN?
  - **Data:**
    - Seoul National University AGN Monitoring Project: `8` high-mass AGN
    - Emission line: Hbeta broad-line region reverberation data
    - Combined dynamical-modeling sample: `38` AGN with consistently determined black-hole masses
  - **Method:**
    - Dynamically model BLR geometry, inclination, and kinematics
    - Compare modeled `M_BH` with reverberation virial products
    - Derive virial factors for line-dispersion and FWHM-based mass estimators
    - Compare with the local active and inactive `M_BH-sigma_*` relation
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The Hbeta-emitting BLRs are best described as thick disks at intermediate inclinations, with far-side-weighted emission.
    - Kinematics combine rotation with inflowing and outflowing components.
    - The calibrated virial factors are `log10(f)_pred=0.69 +/- 0.21` for `sigma_line,rms` and `-0.08 +/- 0.23` for `FWHM_mean`.
    - The updated virial factors have intrinsic dispersion of about `0.2 dex`.
    - The sample extends dynamical BLR modeling to `M_BH~10^8-10^8.5 Msun`.
  - **Position:** a calibration paper for AGN mass work; it is especially relevant when high-z black-hole demographics depend on virial mass systematics

- [2604.24711](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24711) Miriam Eleazer et al. `Halfway to the Peak: Kinematic Signatures of Stable Rotating Disks in Luminous Infrared Galaxies at z=0.5-0.6`
  - **Question:** are infrared-luminous galaxies at `z=0.5-0.6` dynamically disturbed systems or stable rotating disks, and does AGN activity disrupt their global kinematics?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/MIRI MRS: six infrared-luminous galaxies at `z=0.5-0.6`
    - Emission lines: `[Ar II] 6.99 um`, `[Ne II] 12.81 um`, and `H2 0-0 S(5) 6.91 um`
    - Spatial resolution: about `3.0 kpc` at `z~0.55`
  - **Method:**
    - Map mid-infrared atomic and molecular gas kinematics
    - Derive rotation curves from `[Ar II]` velocity fields
    - Compare `V/sigma`, AGN fraction, and central velocity dispersion
  - **Conclusion:**
    - All six galaxies show ordered rotation with `V/sigma >= 2`.
    - No strong evidence is found for recent major mergers or galaxy-wide ionized outflows.
    - `V/sigma` does not correlate with AGN fraction, so global disk disruption is not required for AGN activity in this sample.
    - Higher AGN fractions coincide with elevated central dispersions, consistent with localized turbulence or central structural effects.
  - **Position:** a useful JWST kinematic comparison sample for cosmic-noon disk assembly and AGN triggering, though it is lower priority than the high-z papers

## Cosmic web, CGM, and modeling tools

- [2604.24200](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24200) Sara Santoni et al. `The Three Hundred project: cosmic web identification from 2D gas and Compton-y maps of galaxy clusters outskirts`
  - **Question:** can projected gas and Compton-y maps recover the filamentary cosmic-web network around galaxy clusters?
  - **Data:**
    - The Three Hundred simulations: galaxy-cluster outskirts at `z=0`
    - Mock observations: 2D gas distributions and simulated Compton-y maps
    - Reference networks: projected 3D filament skeletons
  - **Method:**
    - Use DisPerSE to identify filamentary networks in 2D gas maps
    - Compare 2D skeletons with projected 3D networks in purity, completeness, connectivity, and spine distance
    - Extract networks from Compton-y maps and compare them with gas skeletons
  - **Conclusion:**
    - 2D gas skeletons provide good representations of projected 3D networks in both critical points and filaments.
    - The median spine distance between 2D and projected 3D networks is about `0.22 h^-1 Mpc`.
    - Connectivity is slightly underestimated in 2D.
    - Gas and SZ networks agree spatially with median distance about `0.24 h^-1 Mpc`.
    - Filaments in cluster outskirts contribute about `80%` of the integrated Compton-Y parameter.
  - **Position:** a practical LSS method paper because it tests whether SZ and gas maps can recover physically meaningful filamentary environments around clusters

- [2604.24494](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24494) Zhijie Zhang et al. `Probing the Hot Gaseous Halos of Milky Way-like Galaxies in the TNG50 simulation`
  - **Question:** do TNG50 Milky Way analogues reproduce observed hot gaseous halos in soft X-ray emission and oxygen absorption?
  - **Data:**
    - TNG50: cosmological simulation sample of Milky Way-mass analogues
    - Synthetic observables: soft X-ray emission, O VII absorption, and O VIII absorption
    - Observational comparisons: global soft X-ray luminosity, eROSITA stacking profiles, emission measure, and absorption constraints
  - **Method:**
    - Generate synthetic X-ray and absorption observables from simulated halos
    - Compare inner-halo and large-radius surface-brightness profiles against observations
    - Test whether the simulated temperature distribution produces enough O VIII-bearing gas
  - **Conclusion:**
    - TNG50 analogues reproduce the global soft X-ray luminosity and several inner-halo observables.
    - Simulated X-ray surface-brightness profiles fall too steeply at large radii compared with extended eROSITA stacking emission.
    - The simulated halos underproduce O VIII absorption, indicating too little gas at `T~1.6-3.2 x 10^6 K`.
    - The feedback model likely deposits energy too centrally and too vigorously to sustain an extended multiphase corona.
  - **Position:** a CGM model-calibration result that matters for interpreting missing-baryon and feedback predictions in larger-scale galaxy-formation simulations

- [2604.23147](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23147) Xuejie Li et al. `A graph-based Neural Network surrogate model for accelerating semi-analytical model of galaxy formation and evolution`
  - **Question:** can a graph neural network emulate semi-analytic galaxy-formation models across merger trees, parameters, and redshifts?
  - **Data:**
    - Uchuu simulation: dark-matter merger trees
    - Galacticus: semi-analytic model outputs
    - Predicted quantities: stellar mass, luminosity, angular momentum, gas metal mass, and specific SFR over `0<=z<=5`
  - **Method:**
    - Build a conditional graph neural network using merger-tree structure and SAM parameters
    - Train across multiple SAM realizations and redshifts
    - Validate catalog-level galaxy-property predictions against Galacticus outputs
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Stellar mass is predicted over `0<=z<=3` with scatter `0.19-0.28 dex`.
    - The coefficient of determination is `R^2=0.946-0.973` for those stellar-mass predictions.
    - A single graph-based model can reproduce several galaxy properties across multiple merger trees and redshifts.
    - Public inference code, trained models, and example data products are available.
  - **Position:** a useful methods paper for fast galaxy-population modeling, especially if future high-z survey interpretation needs large SAM parameter sweeps
