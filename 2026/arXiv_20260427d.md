## Executive view

The 2026-04-27 batch is compact but useful. The highest-priority paper is [2604.22460](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22460) Arjan Bik, which uses JWST/NIRSpec and MIRI spatially resolved spectroscopy of Gz9p3 at `z=9.3` to separate a metal-poor starbursting tail from a more evolved central clump. It is the strongest direct high-redshift ISM paper in the batch.

The best quasar-environment paper is [2604.22559](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22559) Muhammad Akmal Husain, an ALMA study of PSO J083+11 at `z=6.34`. The quasar and its companion are both compact, rotating, and not obviously disturbed, so the system is framed as a pre-outflow phase where black-hole growth precedes large-scale feedback.

For environments and baryons, [2604.22401](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22401) Guillaume Elias links quiescent fractions in `1.6<z<3.6` groups to the cold-to-hot gas-accretion transition, while [2604.22105](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22105) Kritti Sharma shows that CHIME/FRB dispersion measures correlate with many large-scale-structure and hot-gas tracers. Together they make the day more relevant to group-scale quenching and missing-baryon work than the small submission count suggests.

The remaining profile-relevant entries are methodological or context papers: [2604.22702](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22702) J. M. Palencia expands JWST caustic-crossing stellar-event statistics behind Abell 370, [2604.21977](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21977) S. H. Vincken introduces a Euclid vision-transformer lens finder, and [2604.22613](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22613) B. I. Ciocan measures redshift evolution in the radial acceleration relation with MUSE-HUDF galaxies.

## Reionization-era galaxies and quasar environments

- [2604.22460](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22460) Arjan Bik et al. `Spatially resolved metallicity and ionization in the merging system Gz9p3 at z=9.3`
  - **Question:** what does spatially resolved JWST spectroscopy reveal about the ionized ISM, metallicity, and burst structure of the `z=9.3` merger Gz9p3?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec and MIRI integral-field spectroscopy: rest-frame UV and optical line emission across Gz9p3
    - JWST/MIRI imaging: continuum and line-emission morphology across a `5 kpc` structure
    - Archival JWST/NIRCam and ALMA data: supporting continuum and far-infrared line context
  - **Method:**
    - Measure integrated and aperture-resolved spectra across the main clump and tail
    - Derive SFR, oxygen abundance, ionizing efficiency, and burstiness from emission-line diagnostics
    - Compare optical and far-infrared `[O III]` spatial peaks to identify changes in ISM conditions
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The integrated galaxy has `SFR=13.4 +/- 1.8 Msun/yr`, `12+log(O/H)=7.84 +/- 0.05`, `log(xi_ion)=25.4 +/- 0.1`, and burstiness parameter `0.9 +/- 0.1`.
    - The main galaxy and tail differ strongly in star formation, metallicity, physical conditions, and ionizing efficiency.
    - Optical `[O III]` peaks in the main galaxy while far-infrared `[O III]` peaks toward the tail, indicating distinct gas conditions in the two components.
    - The tail is consistent with a recent, metal-poor starburst, while the central clump is more enriched and shows extreme excitation.
  - **Position:** a high-priority EoR ISM paper because it turns Gz9p3 from a luminous high-z object into a spatially resolved merger with chemically distinct components

- [2604.22559](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22559) Muhammad Akmal Husain et al. `A Quasar--Companion System Without AGN Outflow at z ~ 6: The Case of PSO J083+11`
  - **Question:** is the `z=6.34` quasar PSO J083+11 already driving detectable AGN feedback into its nearby companion and circumgalactic environment?
  - **Data:**
    - ALMA: `[C II] 158 um` emission-line observations of the quasar host and companion
    - ALMA: far-infrared continuum tracing dust-reprocessed star formation and AGN heating
    - Geometry: companion projected separation `18.248 +/- 0.277 kpc` inside the quasar proximity zone
  - **Method:**
    - Use `[C II]` and FIR luminosities to infer star-formation rates
    - Compare `L_[C II]/L_FIR` with normal star-forming-galaxy values
    - Build `[C II]` moment maps to test morphology, velocity fields, and velocity dispersions
    - Search for disturbed kinematics or high-dispersion gas expected from AGN-driven outflows
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The quasar host has SFR `544-3764 Msun/yr` from `[C II]` and `1861-2932 Msun/yr` from FIR emission.
    - The companion has lower SFRs, `21-145 Msun/yr` from `[C II]` and `76-211 Msun/yr` from FIR emission.
    - Both galaxies have typical `L_[C II]/L_FIR` ratios and no clear `[C II]` deficit, so stellar heating can dominate the ISM energetics.
    - Both systems show compact, centrally peaked emission and ordered rotational kinematics rather than high-dispersion outflow signatures.
    - The system is best treated as a pre-outflow accretion phase, not as a resolved case of quasar feedback acting on the companion.
  - **Position:** a useful negative result for high-z quasar-environment studies because proximity to a luminous quasar does not automatically imply observable CGM-scale feedback

- [2604.22702](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22702) J. M. Palencia et al. `First Statistical Study of Over 100 Magnified Stellar Events at Redshift z approx 0.725 with JWST`
  - **Question:** can multi-epoch JWST imaging turn caustic-crossing stars into a statistical probe of stellar populations, lens models, and small-scale dark-matter structure?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST imaging: three observing cycles from `2022-2024`
    - Target field: the Dragon galaxy at `z~0.725` behind the massive cluster Abell 370
    - Event sample: more than `100` magnified stellar events
  - **Method:**
    - Identify flux variations associated with caustic-crossing events in multi-epoch imaging
    - Use the spatial distribution of events to constrain the high-luminosity end of the stellar luminosity function
    - Test parity asymmetry as a proposed wave-dark-matter diagnostic
    - Use the event map to trace the highest-magnification regions and critical curves
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The Dragon field now contains more than `100` detected magnified stellar events, far beyond earlier single-field samples.
    - The high-end luminosity-function slope is constrained to `beta=2.18^{+0.20}_{-0.30}`.
    - The previously discussed parity asymmetry remains present in the larger event sample.
    - The event distribution provides an alternative empirical map of the lensing critical curves.
  - **Position:** not a high-z galaxy result by redshift, but important for JWST strong-lensing methodology that underpins magnified high-z galaxy and stellar-population searches

## High-z environments and cosmic-web baryons

- [2604.22401](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22401) Guillaume Elias et al. `Quiescent fractions in high-redshift galaxy groups reflect their hot-or-cold state of gas accretion`
  - **Question:** do quiescent fractions in high-redshift groups trace whether the halo is still fed by cold accretion or has transitioned to hot accretion?
  - **Data:**
    - Group sample: `16` spectroscopically confirmed galaxy groups at `1.6<z<3.6`
    - Halo masses: `10^12.8-10^13.9 Msun`
    - COSMOS2020: SEDs and candidate member galaxies for membership-probability estimates
  - **Method:**
    - Fit SEDs to classify candidate members as star-forming or quiescent
    - Estimate group quiescent fractions with probabilistic membership
    - Compare each group with the expected cold- or hot-accretion state of its halo
    - Test radial trends in massive quiescent-galaxy locations and stellar-mass gaps
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Quiescent fractions reach about `50%` in hot-accretion groups and are consistent with zero in cold-accretion groups.
    - Mature hot-accreting groups show a `4.4 sigma` excess of massive quiescent galaxies at `R<0.5 R_vir`.
    - Most groups have small stellar-mass gaps, typically `M_*,1/M_*,2 < 3`, so they are still actively assembling.
    - The dominant galaxy's stellar-mass excess relative to the SHMR does not predict the group quiescent fraction.
  - **Position:** a direct environment-quenching result for cosmic-noon and post-cosmic-noon groups because it links quiescence to the gas-accretion state rather than only to galaxy internal mass

- [2604.22105](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22105) Kritti Sharma et al. `Backlighting the Cosmic Web with Fast Radio Bursts: An Anthology of Dispersion Measure Cross-Correlations with Large-Scale Structure and Baryon Tracers`
  - **Question:** can FRB dispersion measures be cross-correlated with large-scale-structure tracers to map cosmic-web baryons and feedback?
  - **Data:**
    - CHIME/FRB: `3455` unique FRB sources with about `15 arcmin` localizations
    - Redshift regime: LSS and baryon tracers at `z lesssim 1.5`
    - Cross-correlation tracers: galaxies, weak lensing, CIB, CMB lensing, tSZ, X-ray clusters, superclusters, SXRB, and radio continuum
  - **Method:**
    - Cross-correlate FRB dispersion measures with each external tracer map
    - Compare hot-gas-weighted tracers with feedback-dependent theoretical predictions
    - Use the sign and amplitude of the correlations to test whether overdense sightlines carry larger electron columns
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Statistically significant correlations are detected at `2.6-5 sigma` with ten probes.
    - FRB sightlines through overdense environments carry systematically larger DMs.
    - The `tSZ x DM` and `SXRB x DM` amplitudes are consistent with moderate-feedback baryon models at about the `0.5 sigma` level.
    - The `SXRB x DM` correlation rules out a weak-feedback scenario at about `3.5 sigma`.
  - **Position:** a strong LSS/IGM method paper because it turns FRBs into a quantitative missing-baryon and feedback probe that can scale with future localization surveys

- [2604.21977](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.21977) S. H. Vincken et al. `Euclid Quick Data Release (Q1). AstroVink: A vision transformer approach to find strong gravitational lens systems`
  - **Question:** can a vision-transformer classifier reduce the human inspection cost for finding strong lenses in Euclid imaging?
  - **Data:**
    - Euclid Q1: `1.08M` targets in the original selection
    - Training data: simulated strong lenses, labeled non-lenses, high-confidence Q1 lens candidates, and realistic rejected negatives
    - Validation set: `110` lens candidates used for top-ranked recovery tests
  - **Method:**
    - Fine-tune a DINOv2-based vision transformer for lens versus non-lens classification
    - Retrain after Q1 with real candidates and difficult negatives from visual inspection
    - Rank candidates, then use Space Warps citizen science and expert vetting for final classification
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The base model recovers `88/110` lens candidates in the top `500`, with one lens per `5.7` inspected objects.
    - The retrained model recovers all `110/110` in the same top-ranked set and improves inspection efficiency to one lens per `4.5` objects.
    - The Q1 search identifies `8` Grade A and `26` Grade B new lens candidates.
    - Realistic negative examples are critical for performance on real Euclid data.
  - **Position:** a survey-infrastructure result that matters for high-z science because Euclid-scale lens finding will define many future magnified-galaxy and quasar-environment samples

## Dynamics, radio AGN, and galaxy-formation theory

- [2604.22613](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22613) B. I. Ciocan et al. `MUSE-DARK III: The evolution of the radial acceleration relation at intermediate redshifts`
  - **Question:** does the radial acceleration relation measured locally persist and evolve at intermediate redshift?
  - **Data:**
    - MUSE Hubble Ultra Deep Field: high signal-to-noise galaxy kinematics
    - Sample: `79` star-forming galaxies complete above `M_* > 10^8.8 Msun`
    - Redshift range: `0.33<z<1.44`
  - **Method:**
    - Use 3D forward modeling to infer observed intrinsic acceleration
    - Decompose disks into stellar, gas, and dark-matter components with pressure-support corrections
    - Fit the radial acceleration relation in the full sample and redshift bins
    - Repeat the analysis under different halo profiles and a MOND framework
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The intermediate-redshift relation is offset from the local relation and has `a0(z~1)=2.38 +/- 0.1 x 10^-10 m/s^2`.
    - The intrinsic scatter is about `0.17 dex`.
    - The characteristic acceleration increases with redshift, with `a1=1.59 +/- 0.1 x 10^-10 m/s^2` in a linear redshift parameterization.
    - Similar evolution is recovered for several dark-matter halo profiles and in the MOND framework.
  - **Position:** useful background for galaxy-dynamics evolution, though less central than the high-z gas and environment entries in this batch

- [2604.22665](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22665) Mohamad Shalaby et al. `The Sound of the Universe: A Resonant Gravitational Instability Driven by Baryon-Dark Matter Relative Drift`
  - **Question:** can post-decoupling baryon-dark-matter relative drift drive a resonant instability that modifies structure growth across astrophysical scales?
  - **Data:**
    - Theory: coupled baryon and cold-dark-matter perturbation analysis with relative drift
    - Regimes considered: planets, protoplanetary disks, stars, molecular clouds, galaxies, galaxy clusters, and an expanding FLRW universe
  - **Method:**
    - Analyze the Doppler-shifted dark-matter mode and baryonic acoustic branch
    - Derive growth rates at resonance for subsonic projected drift
    - Track momentum exchange between species as a collisionless drag
    - Extend the instability calculation to expanding cosmological backgrounds
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Subsonic projected drift can make stable baryonic sound waves resonate with the dark-matter mode and grow faster than the intrinsic CDM rate.
    - The instability peaks below the baryon Jeans scale and can create a complete-stability window between the Jeans scale and resonance in baryon-dominated environments.
    - Growth times range from years to tens of millions of years across the environments considered.
    - In an expanding universe, growth is enhanced or suppressed depending on mode orientation relative to the dark-matter stream.
  - **Position:** speculative but relevant to LSS theory because it proposes a new baryon-dark-matter coupling channel that could imprint scale- and orientation-dependent structure

- [2604.22347](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22347) Soumen Kumar Bera et al. `A Morphological Identification and Study of Radio Galaxies from LoTSS DR2. I. The "Winged" Radio Galaxies`
  - **Question:** what winged radio galaxy population is revealed by high-resolution LoTSS DR2 imaging?
  - **Data:**
    - LoTSS DR2: low-frequency, high-resolution radio imaging
    - Catalog: `621` new winged radio galaxies and `403` additional candidates
    - Morphological split: `382` X-shaped and `239` Z-shaped confirmed winged sources
  - **Method:**
    - Identify winged radio morphologies in LoTSS DR2
    - Classify the sources into X-shaped and Z-shaped subclasses
    - Estimate radio powers, spectral indices, linear sizes, and Fanaroff-Riley morphology
  - **Conclusion:**
    - About `88%` of the confirmed winged sources are edge-brightened and consistent with FR-II morphology.
    - The average `144 MHz` radio power is `log10(P_144MHz / W Hz^-1)=26.25`.
    - The average spectral index between `144 MHz` and `1.4 GHz` is `-0.84`, steeper than older FIRST-selected winged samples.
    - The median linear size is `498 kpc`, and about `16%` exceed `0.7 Mpc`.
  - **Position:** a radio-AGN demographics paper that can support environmental interpretation of large-scale radio morphologies, but it is not a high-z priority by itself
