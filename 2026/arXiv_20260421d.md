## Executive view

The 2026-04-21 batch is strong for the profile. The most direct IGM paper is [2604.16751](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16751) Marie Wisz, which extends damped Lyalpha metallicity measurements to `z~5` with newly detected high-redshift DLAs and finds a sharp drop in neutral-gas metallicity. The strongest cosmic-dawn pair is [2604.16666](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16666) D. J. McLeod and [2604.17963](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17963) Kimi C. Kreilgaard: one uses `>0.6 deg^2` of JWST imaging to constrain the decline of star formation beyond `z>12`, while the other uses BEACON pure-parallel fields to measure UV luminosity functions and bright-galaxy clustering over `z~7-14`.

The best cosmic-noon gas papers are [2604.18522](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18522) Yang Sun, [2604.18503](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18503) Jean-Baptiste Jolly, and [2604.18504](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18504) Jianhang Chen. Together they cover neutral Na D outflows at `0.6<z<4`, molecular radial flows at `z~1.1-1.6`, and spatially resolved dust/CO/[C I] structure in massive main-sequence disks. These are not protocluster papers, but they are highly relevant for interpreting how gas transport and feedback set galaxy growth near cosmic noon.

Two cross-listed papers are worth keeping in the reading queue. [2604.17162](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17162) Kritti Sharma uses FRB dispersion measures to constrain baryon fluctuations and feedback suppression in the matter power spectrum. [2604.17981](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17981) Christopher C. Lovell proposes a cheap emulator for distribution functions in gigaparsec-scale survey volumes, directly relevant to Roman/Euclid/Rubin-scale modeling.

## IGM, DLAs, and baryons outside galaxies

- [2604.16751](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16751) Marie Wisz et al. `The Qz5 Survey (II): Metallicity Evolution of Damped Lyalpha Systems Out to z~5`
  - **Question:** does the neutral gas metallicity traced by DLAs decline smoothly to `z~5`, or is there a sharper transition near the end of reionization?
  - **Data:**
    - VLT/X-shooter: mid- to high-resolution spectroscopy for newly detected `z>4.7` DLAs
    - Keck/HIRES: high-resolution spectroscopy for the same high-redshift DLA sample
    - Literature DLA compilation: metallicity-unbiased systems over `0<z<5.5`
  - **Method:**
    - Add `5` newly detected high-redshift DLAs without metallicity pre-selection
    - Correct Fe abundances for dust depletion and alpha enhancement
    - Fit alpha-element metallicity evolution as a linear function of redshift
    - Compare the highest-redshift metallicity bin against the trend measured below `z<4.7`
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Neutral gas metallicity follows a slope of about `-0.22 +/- 0.05 dex` per unit redshift below the highest-redshift bin.
    - The highest-redshift bin has an `N_HI`-weighted mean metallicity of `<Z> = -2.00`, which is offset from the lower-redshift trend by `4.4 sigma`.
    - The high-z DLA metallicity distribution differs from the lower-redshift comparison at about `2.4 sigma` by the K-S test.
    - A sharp decline near `z~5` may trace delayed CGM enrichment or the end of reionization, but the current inference still depends on small-number statistics.
  - **Position:** a high-priority IGM paper for connecting quasar absorption metallicities, CGM enrichment timing, and reionization-era neutral-gas evolution

- [2604.17162](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17162) Kritti Sharma et al. `Signatures of Suppressed Matter Clustering revealed by Fast Radio Bursts`
  - **Question:** can FRB dispersion measures directly constrain feedback-driven baryon redistribution and matter-clustering suppression?
  - **Data:**
    - FRB sample: `109` bursts with redshifts and dispersion measures
    - Halo-model framework: gas fractions in group and cluster halos of `10^13-10^15 Msun`
    - Simulation comparison set: several hydrodynamical feedback scenarios
  - **Method:**
    - Use FRB DMs to infer spatial fluctuations in the baryon density field
    - Connect baryon fluctuations to the matter power spectrum over `k~0.1-3 h/Mpc`
    - Fit feedback-sensitive halo-model parameters
    - Compare the inferred suppression with hydrodynamical simulations
  - **Conclusion:**
    - FRBs reduce the posterior variance of the matter-power suppression near `k~1 h/Mpc` by a factor of `~8` relative to the prior.
    - The constraints probe a complementary low-redshift regime, roughly `z<=0.3`, without selecting gas by density or temperature.
    - Extreme large-scale feedback models are disfavored at about `~2 sigma`.
    - Larger FRB samples should become a competitive baryon-physics probe for precision cosmology.
  - **Position:** not a high-redshift galaxy paper, but highly relevant to IGM/CGM baryon accounting and to the feedback systematics that affect LSS interpretation

- [2604.18150](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18150) Murilo Macedo et al. `On the relative CNO underabundance in quasar absorption systems at z~3 arising from Population III enrichment and attenuation by intermediate-mass black holes and primordial baryon accretion`
  - **Question:** can Pop III enrichment plus baryon accretion and IMBH mass sequestration explain relative CNO underabundances in high-redshift quasar absorption systems?
  - **Data:**
    - Quasar absorption systems: C, N, and O abundance constraints at `z~3-6`
    - Semi-analytic chemical-enrichment model: adapted from an existing cosmic enrichment framework
    - Cosmic star-formation and baryon-accretion prescriptions: updated inputs for enrichment history
  - **Method:**
    - Model CNO production from Pop III yields
    - Update the cosmic star-formation-rate term
    - Add intermediate-mass black holes as long-lived matter sinks
    - Compare the resulting CNO enrichment with observed absorption-system abundances
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Pop III yields alone tend to overproduce the relevant CNO abundances in the model.
    - IMBH mass sequestration acts as a metallicity attenuator and helps reconcile the model with quasar-absorption data.
    - The combination of primordial baryon accretion, Pop III enrichment, and IMBH sinks can reduce the CNO excess.
    - The result makes IMBH accretion and retention efficiencies a key uncertainty for early chemical-evolution modeling.
  - **Position:** a speculative but relevant absorber-chemistry paper for linking Pop III yields, early black-hole growth, and high-redshift metal-line absorption

## Cosmic dawn, first galaxies, and high-z clustering

- [2604.16666](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16666) D. J. McLeod et al. `A search for the first galaxies across >0.6 deg^2 of JWST imaging: new evidence for a rapid decline in star-formation activity at z>12`
  - **Question:** how rapidly does the UV luminosity function and inferred cosmic star-formation-rate density decline over `12.5<z<18.5`?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRCam imaging: `>0.6 deg^2` total area
    - Survey geometry: more than `150` independent sightlines
    - Candidate search window: extreme-redshift galaxies over `12.5<z<18.5`
  - **Method:**
    - Select high-redshift galaxy candidates from wide-area NIRCam imaging
    - Derive the evolving UV luminosity function at `z>12`
    - Convert UV luminosity-function constraints into star-formation-rate-density evolution
    - Compare the inferred evolution with log-linear extrapolations from earlier JWST LF studies
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The UV LF and inferred SFRD decline rapidly between `z=11` and `z=13.5`, over only about `~100 Myr` of cosmic time.
    - The lack of candidates above `z>14.5` supports an even sharper fall toward earlier epochs.
    - The SFRD decline at `z>12` is about `~4x` steeper than below `z<12`.
    - The data still allow models with enhanced early star-formation efficiency or stochastic star-formation histories up to `z~12`, but they disfavor a simple continuation of the same high abundance into still earlier times.
  - **Position:** a key wide-area JWST constraint on whether the apparent abundance of early UV-bright galaxies persists into the first `~200-300 Myr`

- [2604.17963](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17963) Kimi C. Kreilgaard et al. `BEACON: JWST NIRCam Pure-parallel Imaging Survey. III. Constraints on the UV LF and the Clustering of z~7-14 Galaxies`
  - **Question:** what do many independent JWST pure-parallel sightlines imply for UV luminosity functions and bright-galaxy clustering over `z~7-14`?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRCam BEACON DR2: `~400 arcmin^2` across `36` independent sightlines
    - Dropout sample: `164` candidates over `7<z<12`
    - Spectroscopic overlap: `11` pointings with public JWST spectroscopy
  - **Method:**
    - Select F090W, F115W, and F150W dropout candidates
    - Build UV luminosity functions at `z~7.5`, `z~10`, and higher-redshift limits
    - Use spectroscopic-overlap fields to assess sample purity
    - Measure overdensities around bright galaxies and compare with semi-numerical simulations
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The candidate set contains `150` F090W dropouts, `14` F115W dropouts, and no robust F150W dropouts.
    - The public-spectroscopy overlap gives `100%` purity for the matched BEACON candidates.
    - At `z~10`, the BEACON LF is lower than many photometric JWST estimates but agrees better with spectroscopic constraints.
    - Bright galaxies with `M_UV<-20.5` at `7<z<10` are significantly clustered; fields containing such objects are about `~3x` more likely to be overdense than the survey average.
    - The observed clustering exceeds pre-JWST luminosity-halo-mass expectations, pointing to more massive halos, multiple halo occupation, or altered early star-formation efficiencies.
  - **Position:** one of the strongest daily matches for high-z LSS and reionization-era galaxy demographics because it combines UV LFs with an explicit clustering measurement

- [2604.18173](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18173) Nazanin Davari et al. `Gravitational Waves from the Cosmic Dawn: Tracing Cosmic Black Hole Binaries with ET, LGWA and LISA`
  - **Question:** how do early black-hole seeding and accretion prescriptions change the detectable gravitational-wave population from `z>=4` cosmic binary black holes?
  - **Data:**
    - Cosmic Archaeology Tool: semi-analytic evolution of light, medium-weight, and heavy BH seeds
    - GWFish forecasts: detectability for LISA, LGWA, and ET
    - Growth scenarios: Eddington-limited and super-Eddington accretion
  - **Method:**
    - Evolve BH binary populations through galaxy mergers and dynamical delays
    - Assign accretion prescriptions and seed types
    - Forecast detector-specific event rates and mass-ratio distributions
    - Compare how super-Eddington growth shifts detections between instrument bands
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Super-Eddington growth moves a significant fraction of detectable systems from the ET band to the LISA band.
    - The LISA detection rate rises from `~32 yr^-1` to `~64 yr^-1` when switching from Eddington-limited to super-Eddington growth.
    - The ET detection rate falls from `~64 yr^-1` to `~4 yr^-1` under the same change.
    - Mass ratios, redshift distributions, and detector-band event rates can diagnose early BH growth physics.
  - **Position:** indirect for galaxy reports, but useful for connecting high-z SMBH seed models, LRD/overmassive-BH discussions, and future gravitational-wave constraints

## Cosmic-noon gas transport, outflows, and AGN fueling

- [2604.18522](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18522) Yang Sun et al. `A Census of Na D-traced neutral ISM and outflows at 0.6<z<4`
  - **Question:** how common are neutral Na D absorption and Na D-traced outflows in galaxies across `0.6<z<4`, and what drives those outflows?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec medium-resolution grating spectroscopy: `309` galaxies over `0.6<z<4`
    - Surveys: SMILES, JADES, Blue Jay, and Aurora
    - Spectral tracer: Na D `5890, 5896 Angstrom` absorption after stellar-continuum subtraction
  - **Method:**
    - Subtract stellar continua from NIRSpec spectra
    - Fit the Na D doublet to identify neutral ISM absorption
    - Separate systemic neutral gas from outflowing Na D components
    - Compare outflow incidence with mass, star-formation state, spectral age indicators, and AGN fraction
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Neutral ISM absorption is detected in `76/309` galaxies.
    - Massive galaxies dominate the detections: `85%` of Na D ISM detections occur above `log(M_*/Msun)>10`.
    - Na D outflows are identified in `26` galaxies.
    - Star-forming galaxies show outflow properties linked to star formation, while quiescent galaxies often require an AGN energy source.
    - Five quiescent galaxies show possible fossil outflows, supporting long-lived AGN-driven neutral-gas signatures after the active accretion phase.
  - **Position:** a high-value cosmic-noon feedback paper because it gives a statistical JWST/NIRSpec baseline for neutral outflows in both star-forming and quiescent systems

- [2604.18503](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18503) Jean-Baptiste Jolly et al. `NOEMA3D: Resolving radial gas flows in disk galaxies at z~1.1-1.6 with high-resolution CO observations`
  - **Question:** do spiral arms and bars drive substantial radial molecular-gas flows in massive main-sequence disks near cosmic noon?
  - **Data:**
    - IRAM-NOEMA: deep CO observations, with `>=20 hr` on source per target
    - Galaxy sample: `10` massive main-sequence galaxies at `z~1.1-1.6`
    - JWST imaging: high-resolution stellar and morphological context
  - **Method:**
    - Resolve molecular-gas kinematics and morphology on kiloparsec scales
    - Model axisymmetric disk rotation with DysmalPy
    - Measure coherent residual velocity fields after subtracting circular rotation
    - Interpret residuals as in-plane radial flows linked to bars and spiral structure
  - **Conclusion:**
    - All galaxies show ordered rotation with median intrinsic turbulence `sigma_0~32 +/- 10 km/s`.
    - The median dynamical ratio is `V_c/sigma_0~8.6 +/- 2.9`, consistent with turbulent but rotation-dominated disks.
    - Non-circular motions reach `~50-100 km/s`, larger than typical local disk-galaxy values.
    - Molecular inflow rates are of order the star-formation rate, with typical net inflow around `~50 Msun/yr`.
    - Spiral arms and bars can efficiently funnel cold gas toward galaxy centers at `z~1-2`.
  - **Position:** directly useful for interpreting how cosmic-noon disks sustain central star formation, bulge growth, and possibly SMBH fueling without requiring major mergers

- [2604.18504](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18504) Jianhang Chen et al. `NOEMA3D: Spatially resolved dust, CO, and [C I] in massive star-forming main sequence galaxies at cosmic noon`
  - **Question:** how do dust, CO, and [C I] trace the spatial distribution of molecular gas in massive main-sequence galaxies at `z=1.1-1.6`?
  - **Data:**
    - NOEMA3D survey: CO(4-3), CO(3-2), [C I](1-0), and dust continuum
    - Galaxy sample: `10` massive main-sequence galaxies at cosmic noon
    - Spatial resolution: resolved measurements on `3-6 kpc` scales
  - **Method:**
    - Compare the spatial distributions of dust continuum, CO, and [C I]
    - Test resolved versus galaxy-integrated tracer correlations
    - Measure radial molecular-gas fraction and depletion-time behavior
    - Interpret gas and dust sizes relative to stellar disks and starburst comparisons
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Molecular gas and dust are spatially extended, with sizes comparable to stellar disks rather than compact starburst-like centers.
    - CO remains the most effective tracer for mapping molecular-gas distribution and kinematics.
    - Resolved tracer correlations have about `~2x` larger scatter than integrated correlations, implying inhomogeneous ISM conditions on `3-6 kpc` scales.
    - Molecular-gas fraction and depletion time stay nearly constant out to `2 R_e`, supporting an approximately linear global Kennicutt-Schmidt relation.
    - Extended disks plus ordered gas kinematics favor steady accretion and transport through spiral arms or bars.
  - **Position:** a companion to [2604.18503](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18503) Jean-Baptiste Jolly that turns gas-flow interpretation into a broader dust/CO/[C I] tracer calibration for cosmic-noon disks

- [2604.17955](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17955) Kotaro Kobayashi et al. `Vaulting the barrier: An intrinsic mechanism to fuel the gas beyond the nuclear ring into the central region of barred galaxies`
  - **Question:** how can bar-driven gas bypass a nuclear ring and reach the central tens of parsecs where nuclear starbursts and AGN fueling occur?
  - **Data:**
    - Three-dimensional Lagrangian hydrodynamic simulations in a fixed barred potential
    - Comparison with the usual two-dimensional expectation that gas stalls in a nuclear ring
  - **Method:**
    - Track gas trajectories approaching the nuclear ring
    - Identify particles that acquire vertical excursions and lose angular momentum
    - Compare the origin of central gas with material initially inside and outside the ring
    - Classify the geometry of successful center-reaching streams
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Some inflowing gas vaults over the nuclear-ring barrier and reaches the inner `~50 pc`.
    - Center-reaching material originates outside the ring, at radii `>=300 pc`, rather than from secondary leakage out of the ring.
    - Successful delivery requires vertical excursions of about `|z|~100 pc` plus substantial angular-momentum loss.
    - The pathway is intrinsically three-dimensional and is missed by two-dimensional calculations.
  - **Position:** a useful low-redshift/theory complement to NOEMA3D because it gives a concrete mechanism connecting bar-driven inflow, central gas supply, and AGN fueling

## SMBH growth, cosmic web methods, and survey-scale modeling

- [2604.16539](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16539) Hitaishi Chillara `Classifying Supermassive Black Hole Growth Regimes to Observables Across Cosmological Simulations with Forecasts for LSST`
  - **Question:** can LSST-like broadband photometry identify over-massive versus under-massive SMBH growth regimes when direct black-hole masses are unavailable?
  - **Data:**
    - Cosmological simulations: SIMBA, IllustrisTNG, and EAGLE
    - Forward-modeled LSST photometric bands
    - SMBH growth-regime labels from simulation populations
  - **Method:**
    - Forward-model simulated galaxies into LSST-like observables
    - Train an ensemble machine-learning classifier on photometric features
    - Test cross-simulation transfer using rank-normalized features
    - Decompose the classification signal into host-galaxy colors and accretion-state SED effects
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The classifier reaches `91-94%` accuracy within SIMBA and IllustrisTNG under LSST magnitude limits.
    - Cross-simulation transfer reaches `83-89%` accuracy, despite differing SMBH feedback prescriptions.
    - Host-galaxy colors drive much of the classification signal, with `82-87%` accuracy from colors alone.
    - The method provides an LSST-era baseline for identifying post-seeding growth regimes in simulations with heavy seeds of at least `>=10^4 Msun`.
  - **Position:** relevant to LRD and over-massive-BH interpretation because it asks which SMBH-growth signatures survive in survey-scale photometry

- [2604.17981](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17981) Christopher C. Lovell et al. `Efficiently emulating distribution functions in gigaparsec volumes for varying cosmological parameters`
  - **Question:** can small targeted regions emulate distribution functions from gigaparsec-scale simulations across changing cosmological parameters?
  - **Data:**
    - Quijote simulation suite: many `(1 h^-1 Gpc)^3` N-body volumes
    - Target regions: overdensity-selected subvolumes of about `(50 h^-1 Mpc)^3`
    - Emulator inputs: regional overdensity plus global cosmological parameters
  - **Method:**
    - Select small regions spanning a range of overdensities
    - Train a differentiable emulator for the halo mass function in those regions
    - Integrate over the overdensity distribution to recover the full-box halo mass function
    - Generalize the approach toward baryonic distribution functions and higher-order statistics
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The method recovers global halo-mass distributions while using only `~0.026%` of the original simulation volume.
    - Targeted zoom-like regions can emulate much larger boxes at a fraction of the computational cost.
    - The dynamic range can be pushed to lower halo masses than conventional periodic simulations of the same effective volume.
    - The framework is directly relevant for Euclid, Roman, and Rubin survey interpretation.
  - **Position:** a methods paper worth tracking for large-volume LSS and galaxy-formation inference, especially if paired with baryonic emulator extensions

- [2604.18209](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.18209) Benjamin Hertzsch et al. `Galaxy Populations in the IllustrisTNG Caustic Skeleton`
  - **Question:** how do galaxy colors and star-formation activities vary across a multiscale cosmic-web skeleton defined by dark-matter flow caustics?
  - **Data:**
    - IllustrisTNG simulations: dark-matter, galaxy, and intergalactic-gas fields
    - Caustic skeleton: parameter-free web formalism based on multistreaming dark-matter flow
    - Galaxy-environment assignment: voids, walls, filaments, and cluster nodes
  - **Method:**
    - Construct the multiscale caustic skeleton of the simulated dark-matter distribution
    - Measure baryonic gas properties near caustic structures
    - Associate simulated galaxies with web environments across multiple scales
    - Compare galaxy colors and star-formation activity as functions of web scale and environment
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Galaxy properties vary continuously across scale-space cosmic-web environments rather than only across a single void-wall-filament-node classification.
    - The formation time of web components is tied to galaxy-property trends.
    - The caustic-skeleton framework provides a way to connect hierarchical structure growth with the observed color-density relation.
  - **Position:** relevant to LSS/environment work because it treats cosmic-web embedding as multiscale and dynamically defined, not just density-threshold classified
