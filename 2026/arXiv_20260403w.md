## Executive view

This is a black-hole-growth and extreme-source week. The main thread is how to interpret JWST-era early AGN and LRDs once millimeter, FUV, and radio information are added. [2604.01123](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01123) Yihao Zhou argues that abundant heavy seeds plus early mergers can reproduce several JWST massive-BH candidates, while [2603.29652](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29652) Giovanni Mazzolari uses NOEMA to constrain the host galaxies of three `z>6` JWST AGN.

The observational side is strong enough to be useful. [2604.03370](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03370) Xihan Ji uses FUV spectroscopy of a `z=7.04` LRD to argue against a fully closed `BH^star` geometry, [2603.28863](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28863) Mingyu Li finds a radio source that is dark in every JWST band, and [2603.28044](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28044) Youwen Kong shows that most radio-selected high-z quasar candidates would be missed by classic ultra-steep-spectrum cuts.

The week also has one clean first-stars inference paper: [2604.02814](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02814) Sho Ukai demonstrates that future global-signal experiments like REACH should be able to constrain typical Pop III masses and star-formation efficiencies.

## Early black holes and LRD interpretations

- [2604.01123](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01123) Yihao Zhou `First results of AMBRA: Abundant Seeds and Early Mergers as a Pathway to the First Massive Black Holes`
  - **Question:** can abundant heavy seeds plus early black-hole mergers explain the first massive black holes seen by JWST without requiring long-lived super-Eddington growth?
  - **Data:**
    - AMBRA simulation suite combining the large-volume ASTRID framework with gas-based BRAHMA seeding models
    - Heavy-seed prescription producing `4 x 10^4-10^5 Msun` seeds in metal-poor star-forming halos
  - **Method:**
    - Compare AMBRA with the older ASTRID treatment
    - Track early BH seeding, mergers, and accretion histories in dense peaks
    - Measure the resulting BH demographics and merger-event rates
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The improved seeding model raises the `z=8` BH number density by more than an order of magnitude over the `10^5-10^7 Msun` range relative to ASTRID.
    - BHs that reach masses comparable to GN-z11 and CEERS-1019 typically form in very compact density peaks and undergo repeated early mergers.
    - For these systems, roughly `~50%` of the BH mass by `z=11` comes from BH mergers before gas accretion becomes dominant.
    - Without merger-driven early assembly, ASTRID cannot reproduce the most massive JWST BH candidates.
    - AMBRA predicts about `~4` LISA-detectable BH mergers per year at `z>=8`, roughly `10^3` times the ASTRID rate.
  - **Position:** a strong early-BH growth paper because it makes the merger contribution explicit and turns the scenario into a direct future LISA test

- [2603.29652](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.29652) Giovanni Mazzolari `Constraints on the host galaxy and AGN properties of three z > 6 JWST AGN from NOEMA observations`
  - **Question:** what do deep NOEMA `[C II]` and continuum observations imply about the host galaxies of three `z>6` JWST AGN, including two LRD-like sources?
  - **Data:**
    - Deep NOEMA `[C II] 158 um` and continuum observations
    - Three JWST-discovered AGN at `z>6`, two with LRD-like properties and one blue extended Type I AGN
    - JWST/MIRI constraints used in the SED decomposition
  - **Method:**
    - Use the `[C II]` and dust-continuum non-detections to limit gas and dust masses
    - Compare the limits with the expected `[C II]-SFR` relation
    - Refit the SEDs with AGN plus stellar components under the NOEMA constraints
  - **Conclusion:**
    - None of the three sources is significantly detected in `[C II]` or dust continuum, even after stacking.
    - The resulting limits are `log L_[CII]/Lsun < 7.77-8.1`, roughly `~2 sigma` below the expected `[C II]-SFR` relation.
    - Gas-mass limits are `log(M_gas/Msun) < 9.26-9.59`, with dust-mass limits `log(M_dust/Msun) < 5.68-6.55`.
    - The revised SED decomposition lowers the stellar masses by up to `~2 dex`, giving `0.03 <= M_BH / M_* <= 0.7`.
    - For the two LRDs, the UV is consistent with stellar light while the optical-to-NIR rise is reproduced by a fairly standard Type I AGN partially obscured along the polar direction with `E(B-V)_polar ~ 1`.
  - **Position:** a useful millimeter anchor for the LRD debate because it shows that fairly standard AGN plus patchy dust can explain two high-z LRD-like systems without resorting to exotic alternatives

- [2604.03370](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.03370) Xihan Ji `Holes in the BH^star? AGN signatures in the FUV spectrum of a black-hole dominated Little Red Dot at z=7.04`
  - **Question:** does the far-UV spectrum of the `z=7.04` LRD Abell2744-QSO1 support a fully covered `BH^star` geometry, or are there optically thin escape channels?
  - **Data:**
    - New JWST far-UV spectroscopy of Abell2744-QSO1
    - NIRCam imaging and NIRSpec slit data for spatial information
  - **Method:**
    - Search for broad `Lyalpha` and metal-line AGN signatures in the rest-frame FUV
    - Separate the low- and high-velocity `Lyalpha` components
    - Compare the line fluxes and kinematics with expectations from a nearly closed `BH^star` geometry
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The spectrum shows broad `Lyalpha` with `FWHM ~ 1000 km s^-1` plus `O I`, `C IV`, and/or `Fe II` emission.
    - The low-velocity `Lyalpha` component with `v <= 200 km s^-1` is likely spatially extended, but the high-velocity component stays unresolved.
    - The broad component is unlikely to be created only by resonant scattering in the ISM, so the paper argues that it originates in the BLR.
    - The presence of broad-line `Lyalpha` and larger-scale fluorescence means at least one relatively optically thin path exists from the BLR, so the absorber cannot be fully closed; it must have holes or be clumpy.
  - **Position:** a strong LRD test case because it uses FUV line physics to argue directly against the simplest fully covered `BH^star` picture

## Obscured sources and high-z quasar searches

- [2603.28863](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28863) Mingyu Li `Something Bright at the Edge of Everything: A Uniquely JWST-Dark Radio Source in COSMOS`
  - **Question:** what is a bright radio source in COSMOS if it is detected from LOFAR to the VLA but remains invisible in every JWST band?
  - **Data:**
    - Radio detections from LOFAR `144 MHz` through VLA `3 GHz`
    - Deep non-detections in HST, JWST, Chandra, Herschel, and ALMA imaging
  - **Method:**
    - Cross-match radio and JWST source catalogs in COSMOS
    - Inspect whether the source is compact, steep-spectrum, and truly unmatched across the other bands
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The source is the only object in the COSMOS cross-match that is undetected in every JWST band while being clearly radio-detected across the full frequency range.
    - It is only marginally resolved and has a steep unbroken radio spectrum.
    - The object could be an extremely dust-obscured radio-loud source at cosmic dawn or a detached radio lobe whose host lies elsewhere.
    - In either case, it highlights a population that deep JWST imaging alone can miss completely.
  - **Position:** a useful discovery-space paper for the reionization and early-AGN community because it shows why radio-selected searches are still needed even in the JWST era

- [2603.28044](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28044) Youwen Kong `A Wide and Deep Exploration of Radio-detected Active Galactic Nuclei with Subaru HSC (WERGS). XIII. High-Redshift Radio Quasar candidates beyond Ultra-Steep Spectrum Selection: Dropout selection from HSC--VLASS over ~1200 deg^2`
  - **Question:** can high-z radio quasars be selected efficiently without relying on the classical ultra-steep-spectrum criterion?
  - **Data:**
    - HSC-SSP Wide optical imaging and VLASS radio data over `~1200 deg^2`
    - Cross-matches with FIRST, LoTSS, and TGSS for spectral-index information
  - **Method:**
    - Apply `g`, `r`, and `i` dropout selection to radio-identified sources
    - Fit the SEDs of the selected objects and examine their radio spectra and number densities
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The search finds `~400` high-z radio-AGN candidates at `z >= 4` and potentially up to `z~7`.
    - Their optical magnitudes cluster around `i_AB ~ 24-26`, so they are mostly inaccessible to shallower surveys like SDSS.
    - Most candidates have flat to moderately steep radio spectra with `-1 <= alpha <= 0`, and some show spectral turnovers, meaning standard ultra-steep-spectrum selection would miss most of them.
    - The comoving number density declines sharply around the `i`-dropout regime at `z~6`, which hints at a disappearance of luminous radio AGN toward the EoR.
  - **Position:** a practical high-z radio-quasar selection paper because it demonstrates that dropout-plus-radio selection is broader and more complete than ultra-steep-spectrum filtering

- [2604.06059](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06059) Shenli Tang `Spectroscopic confirmation of dual and offset quasars from the Subaru HSC-SSP program`
  - **Question:** how many close dual or offset quasar candidates from HSC-SSP survive spectroscopic confirmation?
  - **Data:**
    - `99` spectra from NTT/EFOSC2, Gemini/GMOS-N, Keck/NIRES, and Subaru/FOCAS
    - HSC-selected close companions at `0.6-4 arcsec`, or `<30 kpc`, around SDSS quasars
  - **Method:**
    - Use `2D` image decomposition to identify PSF-like companions
    - Confirm broad-line dual quasars and quasar-galaxy offset pairs spectroscopically
    - Measure BH masses, luminosities, and dual fractions by redshift
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The program confirms `6` broad-line dual quasars and `11` offset quasars.
    - The duals lie at `1.5 < z < 3.3`, with BH masses `10^8.5-10^10 Msun`, luminosities `10^45.5-10^47.5 erg s^-1`, and moderate Eddington ratios `0.01-0.4`.
    - The inferred dual fractions are only `0.2-1.2%` at `z<0.8`, `0.08-0.24%` at `0.8<z<1.5`, and `0.06%` at `1.5<z<3.3`.
    - Those values are lower than theoretical expectations for a rising dual fraction at cosmic noon, though the selection function still matters.
  - **Position:** a useful merger-side quasar paper because it expands the confirmed dual sample and quantifies how rare such systems remain in practice

## Pop III and exotic growth channels

- [2604.02814](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.02814) Sho Ukai `Inferring population III star properties from the 21-cm global signal`
  - **Question:** can the global `21 cm` signal constrain the typical mass and star-formation efficiency of Population III stars?
  - **Data:**
    - Semi-numerical global-signal simulations
    - Escape-fraction prescriptions that depend on stellar and halo mass
    - Radiation-hydrodynamic heating structure around first-star host halos
  - **Method:**
    - Propagate the Pop III parameters into the thermal and ionization evolution of the IGM
    - Run a Fisher analysis including realistic foreground contamination
    - Test whether future global-signal experiments can recover the Pop III parameters
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The global `21 cm` signal is sensitive enough to both the typical Pop III stellar mass and the Pop III star-formation efficiency once realistic halo-dependent escape fractions and heating structure are included.
    - After foregrounds are accounted for, future experiments such as REACH should still deliver meaningful constraints on those parameters.
  - **Position:** a clean first-stars inference paper because it turns the global signal into a quantitative Pop III parameter-estimation problem rather than only a qualitative sensitivity claim

- [2603.28682](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.28682) Muhammad A. Latif `How Overmassive Black Holes Formed at Cosmic Dawn`
  - **Question:** how can overmassive black-hole galaxies at `z~10` form so early while reaching extreme `M_BH/M_*` ratios?
  - **Data:**
    - Cosmological simulation of a direct-collapse BH and its host galaxy from birth to `z~10`
  - **Method:**
    - Seed a `70000 Msun` direct-collapse BH at `z=25.7`
    - Follow its accretion, host-galaxy growth, and metal-feedback history for several hundred Myr
    - Compare the resulting spectra and mass ratios with JWST OBG candidates
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The BH grows at about half the Eddington rate to `6.0 x 10^6 Msun` by `z=10.1`.
    - Its host reaches `M_* ~ 4 x 10^8 Msun`, metallicity `~0.1 Zsun`, SFR `~2 Msun yr^-1`, and `M_BH/M_* ~ 0.01`, close to observed overmassive BH galaxies such as GN-z11, UHZ1, and GHZ9.
    - The extreme mass ratio follows naturally from early star-formation suppression by the BH and later blowout of metals by Pop III supernovae.
    - The simulated spectra match UHZ1 and GHZ9 well.
  - **Position:** a useful overmassive-BH formation paper because it follows the co-evolution of a DCBH and its host for several hundred Myr instead of only modeling the seed phase
