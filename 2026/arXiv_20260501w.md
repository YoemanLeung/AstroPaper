## Executive view

This week is strong and broad. The single best high-redshift galaxy result is [2604.25632](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25632) Qiong Li, which uses JWST CEERS and JADES data to show that `z=4-10` galaxies in overdense regions are already more metal-rich and have higher SFR density. It directly connects environment, chemical enrichment, and galaxy growth during reionization.

The strongest reionization-frontier cluster is [2604.23823](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23823) Hakim Atek, [2604.26331](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26331) Hyunbae Park, [2604.25991](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25991) Erini Lambrides, and [2604.26750](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26750) Koki Otaki. Atek pushes the lensed JWST UV luminosity function to `M_UV=-12`; Park shows that double-peaked Lya during reionization needs nearby voids and favorable ionizing background; Lambrides reports spectroscopic variability in a `z~7` LRD; Otaki models how supernova reverse shocks reshape early dust extinction curves.

The best CGM and quasar-environment papers are [2604.25127](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25127) Shiwu Zhang and [2604.26027](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26027) Jelena Ritter. Zhang uses KCWI Lya nebulae around `85` quasars at `z=2-3` to favor an evolutionary AGN obscuration scenario. Ritter combines VLT/UVES and JWST/NIRSpec for `z>3` radio-galaxy halos and links cool Lya absorbers with ionized outflows.

The week also has a coherent protocluster and cosmic-web thread. [2604.27071](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27071) Jaclyn B. Champagne measures chaotic molecular gas in Spiderweb protocluster dusty star-forming galaxies at `z=2.16`. [2604.24852](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24852) Jade Paste and [2604.24200](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24200) Sara Santoni study gas and SZ-traced filaments around clusters. [2604.22105](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22105) Kritti Sharma and [2604.25828](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25828) Shion Andrew support FRB dispersion as a cosmic-web baryon probe.

For AGN feedback, the most useful set is [2604.27159](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27159) Ming-Yi Lin, [2604.27332](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27332) Minhang Guo, [2604.24899](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24899) R. Middei, and [2604.24892](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24892) Oscar Veenema. ## Reionization-era galaxies, LRDs, and early dust

- [2604.25632](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25632) Qiong Li et al. `Environmental dependence of the Mass-Metallicity Star Formation Relations at z=4-10 with JWST`
  - **Question:** does environment already affect chemical enrichment and the mass-metallicity-SFR relation during the epoch of reionization?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/CEERS and JWST/JADES: deep imaging and spectroscopy
    - Galaxy sample: `225` galaxies with stellar masses, SFRs, and gas-phase metallicities
    - Environment metric: projected fifth-nearest-neighbor surface density `Sigma_5` within `Delta z=+/-0.25`
  - **Method:**
    - Compare dense and field galaxies in the MZR and MZR-SFR space
    - Benchmark against the local `T_e`-based FMR
    - Test secondary trends with NIRCam size and stellar age
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Dense-region galaxies at `4.5<z<7` are more metal-rich at fixed stellar mass by `0.1-0.2 dex`.
    - The full sample lies `0.2-0.3 dex` below the local `T_e`-based FMR, with a smaller deficit in overdense regions.
    - The SFR density in overdense regions at `z~6-9` is higher by a factor of `2-3`.
  - **Position:** the week's most important high-z environment paper because it makes early overdensity measurable through metallicity and SFR, not just counts

- [2604.23823](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.23823) Hakim Atek et al. `A GLIMPSE of the 99%: a census of the faintest galaxies during the epoch reionization and its implications for galaxy formation models`
  - **Question:** does the reionization-era UV luminosity function turn over at very faint magnitudes?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/GLIMPSE: deep lensed imaging of galaxies at `z=6-9`
    - Gravitational lensing: UVLF reach to `M_UV=-12`
    - Modeling: statistical and systematic lensing uncertainties
  - **Method:**
    - Construct a lensed UVLF with end-to-end uncertainty propagation
    - Compare with simulations and bright-end JWST-calibrated models
    - Convert the UVLF into ionizing emissivity
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The `z~7` UVLF keeps rising with slope `alpha=-1.98^{+0.06}_{-0.05}`.
    - No clear turnover is found down to `M_UV=-12.3`.
    - The inferred ionizing emissivity is `log(n_ion / s^-1 Mpc^-3)~50.85`, enough to maintain reionization for `C_HII=5`.
  - **Position:** a core reionization result because it puts the faint majority of galaxies back into the ionizing-budget calculation

- [2604.25991](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25991) Erini Lambrides et al. `The GlimmIr: Spectroscopic Variability in a z~7 LRD Indicates Rapid Changes in Both the Narrow and Broad Line Regions`
  - **Question:** do Little Red Dots show spectroscopic variability on rest-frame timescales short enough to reveal the scale of their ionizing source?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec: `10.2 hr` C3PO F290LP/G395M spectrum
    - Comparison data: `8.4 hr` THRILS F290LP/G395M spectrum taken `99` observed days earlier, about `13` rest-frame days
    - Additional checks: RUBIES prism/clear and CAPERS observations
    - Target: `z~7` LRD
  - **Method:**
    - Compare continuum, broad-line, and `[O III] 5008` fluxes across epochs
    - Test whether differences can be explained by MSA slit placement or flux calibration
    - Interpret variability in the context of accreting-black-hole and dense-gas models
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Continuum and broad-line flux differ by about `30%`.
    - `[O III] 5008` flux differs by `42%` between the two main epochs.
    - The variability is not explained by slit placement or simple flux calibration offsets.
    - If LRDs are accreting black-hole systems, direct sightlines from the accretion disk to nebular gas must exist beyond the BLR, requiring covering fractions `<100%`.
  - **Position:** a high-priority LRD paper because variability gives a size and ionization constraint that static SED interpretations cannot provide

- [2604.26331](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26331) Hyunbae Park et al. `Double-Peaked Lya Emission during Reionization Requires Nearby Voids and a Favorable Local Ionizing Background`
  - **Question:** how can blue-side Lya photons escape during reionization to produce double-peaked profiles?
  - **Data:**
    - Cosmic Dawn III simulation: sightlines from reionization-era galaxies
    - Transmission modeling: Lya photons through local density and ionization fields
  - **Method:**
    - Identify simulated sightlines matching observed double-peaked Lya profiles
    - Link transmission to nearby voids and local ionizing background intensity
    - Compare probabilities at different global ionization fractions
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Successful sightlines intersect voids a few comoving Mpc from the source.
    - Regions with `<=30%` of the cosmic mean density become highly transmissive under ionization equilibrium.
    - The global probability of observing double-peaked emission is about `3 x 10^-3` at `80%` ionization and zero at `60%` ionization.
    - A tenfold increase in local ionizing background raises the probability by about `10^4`.
  - **Position:** an important IGM/reionization paper because double-peaked Lya becomes a probe of void geometry and local ionizing background, not only galaxy outflows

- [2604.26750](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26750) Koki Otaki et al. `Dust enrichment from core-collapse supernovae and extinction curves in the high-redshift universe`
  - **Question:** can core-collapse supernova dust, after reverse-shock processing, explain flat high-redshift UV attenuation curves and weak UV bumps?
  - **Data:**
    - Dust-yield models: varying progenitor mass, rotation, metallicity, and ISM density
    - High-z comparison: JWST-inferred attenuation curves for `z>7` galaxies
  - **Method:**
    - Model a single starburst population with an IMF
    - Evolve grain size distributions with reverse-shock destruction
    - Compute extinction curves for individual dust species and mixtures
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Before reverse shocks, `M_dust/M_star~10^-3` at `30 Myr`.
    - Reverse shocks reduce the dust-to-stellar mass ratio to at most `~10^-5`.
    - Grains smaller than `~10 nm` are destroyed, leaving amorphous carbon dominant.
    - The processed dust produces flatter extinction curves with a broad bump near `2500 Angstrom`, consistent with high-z observations.
  - **Position:** a useful early-dust paper because it connects JWST attenuation-curve shapes to supernova grain processing rather than only to dust mass

## CGM, quasars, and protoclusters

- [2604.26027](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.26027) Jelena Ritter et al. `Exploring the gaseous halos of z>3 radio galaxies with UVES and JWST/NIRSpec`
  - **Question:** what do high-resolution Lya absorption and JWST rest-optical spectra reveal about cool halo gas around `z>3` radio galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - VLT/UVES: high-resolution Lya halo spectra for four high-redshift radio galaxies
    - Redshift range: `3.1<z<4.5`
    - Spectral resolution: `Delta v~12 km/s`
    - JWST/NIRSpec: two targets, 4C+03.24 and TNJ0205
  - **Method:**
    - Identify H I absorbers in Lya emission profiles
    - Compare UVES absorber decomposition with MUSE measurements
    - Use NIRSpec rest-optical lines to test ionized-gas outflows
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Each target has `6-14` absorbers with `N_HI=10^12-10^17 cm^-2`.
    - About `70%` of absorbers are spatially resolved along the radio jet axis over extents `>30 kpc`.
    - UVES resolves deep absorbers into multiple components missed at lower spectral resolution.
    - Some absorbers may be physically associated with the host systems.
    - NIRSpec reveals potential ionized ISM outflows, suggesting a kinematic link between `[O III]` gas and cool Lya halo gas.
  - **Position:** one of the week's strongest CGM papers because it combines high-resolution absorption with JWST outflow diagnostics in massive `z>3` AGN hosts

- [2604.25127](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25127) Shiwu Zhang et al. `Constraining the nature of active galactic nuclei through circumgalactic Lya emission at z=2-3`
  - **Question:** do Lya nebulae around obscured and unobscured quasars favor orientation or evolution as the origin of AGN obscuration?
  - **Data:**
    - Keck/KCWI: Lya nebulae around `59` unobscured and `26` obscured quasars
    - Redshift range: `z=2-3`
  - **Method:**
    - Compare nebular symmetry, scale length, and velocity-dispersion radial profiles
    - Interpret trends against orientation-only and evolutionary AGN scenarios
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Unobscured-quasar nebulae are less symmetric, `a_w=0.2-0.6`, and more extended, `r_h=10.7 +/- 0.5 kpc`.
    - Obscured-quasar nebulae have `a_w=0.6-0.8` and `r_h=6.6-7.7 kpc`.
    - Unobscured quasars show steep velocity-dispersion gradients of `-4.3 +/- 0.4 km/s/kpc`, while obscured quasars are nearly flat.
    - The trends favor an evolutionary scenario where feedback redistributes gas during the obscured-to-unobscured transition.
  - **Position:** a high-priority quasar-CGM result because extended Lya morphology and kinematics challenge pure orientation unification

- [2604.27071](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27071) Jaclyn B. Champagne et al. `Chaotic Molecular Gas in Five Dusty Star-forming Galaxies in the Spiderweb Protocluster at z = 2.16`
  - **Question:** how do gas supply and kinematics vary among dusty star-forming galaxies in a mature `z=2.16` protocluster?
  - **Data:**
    - ALMA: CO(3-2) observations of five Spiderweb protocluster DSFGs
    - Spatial range: about `0.5-4 cMpc` from the core
  - **Method:**
    - Measure molecular-gas mass and CO kinematics
    - Fit disk models and inspect disturbed residuals
    - Compare gas fractions with distance from the Spiderweb Galaxy
  - **Conclusion:**
    - All five galaxies show disturbed molecular-gas kinematics.
    - The most massive galaxies and all X-ray AGN have gas fractions `<30%`, below the sample average of `49%`.
    - Gas fractions and sSFR decline with distance from the Spiderweb Galaxy.
  - **Position:** the week's most direct high-z protocluster gas paper because it moves beyond member counts to cold-gas availability and dynamics

- [2604.22559](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22559) Muhammad Akmal Husain et al. `A Quasar--Companion System Without AGN Outflow at z ~ 6: The Case of PSO J083+11`
  - **Question:** is the `z=6.34` quasar PSO J083+11 already affecting its companion and CGM through AGN-driven outflows?
  - **Data:**
    - ALMA: `[C II] 158 um` and FIR continuum for the quasar host and companion
    - Geometry: companion projected separation `18.248 +/- 0.277 kpc`
  - **Method:**
    - Infer SFRs from `[C II]` and FIR emission
    - Map `[C II]` morphology and velocity fields
    - Search for outflow-like dispersions or disturbed kinematics
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The quasar host has SFR `544-3764 Msun/yr` from `[C II]` and `1861-2932 Msun/yr` from FIR.
    - Both galaxies show compact, centrally peaked emission and ordered rotational kinematics.
    - No significant AGN-driven outflow signature is found.
  - **Position:** a useful negative high-z quasar-environment result because proximity and extreme SFR do not guarantee observable feedback

## Cosmic web, baryons, and environment structure

- [2604.22105](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.22105) Kritti Sharma et al. `Backlighting the Cosmic Web with Fast Radio Bursts: An Anthology of Dispersion Measure Cross-Correlations with Large-Scale Structure and Baryon Tracers`
  - **Question:** can FRB dispersion measures map cosmic-web baryons through cross-correlations with LSS tracers?
  - **Data:**
    - CHIME/FRB: `3455` unique FRB sources with about `15 arcmin` localizations
    - Tracers: galaxies, weak lensing, CIB, CMB lensing, tSZ, X-ray clusters, superclusters, SXRB, and radio continuum
  - **Method:**
    - Cross-correlate FRB DMs with each tracer map
    - Compare hot-gas-weighted correlations with feedback models
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Significant correlations appear at `2.6-5 sigma` with ten probes.
    - FRB sightlines through overdense environments carry larger DMs.
    - `tSZ x DM` and `SXRB x DM` favor moderate feedback, while weak feedback is ruled out at about `3.5 sigma` by `SXRB x DM`.
  - **Position:** the week's strongest observational missing-baryon method paper

- [2604.24852](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24852) Jade Paste et al. `Cosmic gas accretion from filaments onto galaxy clusters using the IllustrisTNG simulation`
  - **Question:** how do filaments shape gas accretion and thermalization around galaxy clusters?
  - **Data:**
    - IllustrisTNG-300: `415` galaxy clusters at `z=0`
    - Profiles: temperature, entropy, density, pressure, and radial velocity
  - **Method:**
    - Compare isotropic profiles with gas selected in filaments
    - Separate cluster outskirts and larger cluster-environment regimes
  - **Conclusion:**
    - At `2-4 R_200`, fast infalling warm gas enters the circumcluster medium along filaments.
    - At `1-2 R_200`, filament gas enters the hot intracluster medium with strong tangential velocity gradients.
    - Strong accretion shocks around filaments are not found, suggesting slow thermalization.
  - **Position:** a useful simulation counterpart to FRB and SZ cosmic-web probes because it predicts how filament gas should appear near clusters

- [2604.27845](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27845) Zhuoming Zhang et al. `Anisotropy of Satellite Galaxies-I: Contrasting Correlations with Central Galaxy, Host Halo, and Large-Scale Filament Structures`
  - **Question:** how does satellite anisotropy transition from central-galaxy to halo to cosmic-filament control?
  - **Data:**
    - Simulations: SIMBA, EAGLE, and IllustrisTNG-100
    - Redshift reach: alignments persist up to `z=1.5`
  - **Method:**
    - Measure satellite alignments relative to central galaxies, halos, and filaments
    - Trace satellite orbits in SIMBA
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Satellite anisotropy aligns with halo and central-galaxy major axes.
    - A `3 sigma` scale transition appears: `<0.3 R_200c` follows central morphology, `0.3-2 R_200c` follows halo triaxiality, and `>2 R_200c` follows filaments.
    - Satellite orbits erase primordial filament signals after accretion inside `<2 R_200c`.
  - **Position:** a useful environmental-structure paper because it links observed satellite alignment to physical scale and orbital processing

## AGN feedback and black-hole growth

- [2604.27159](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27159) Ming-Yi Lin et al. `Molecular Outflows in the Nucleus of the Nearby Compton-thick AGN NGC 3079`
  - **Question:** what powers the compact molecular outflow in NGC 3079?
  - **Data:**
    - NOEMA: CO(2-1) at `0.5 arcsec`, about `40 pc`
    - Disk modeling: 3D-Barolo and DysmalPy
  - **Method:**
    - Model the rotating disk and isolate high-velocity nuclear residuals
    - Estimate molecular outflow rate, kinetic power, and momentum rate
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The blueshifted component is offset by `0.17 arcsec`, about `14 pc`, and has `v-v_sys=-350` to `-450 km/s`.
    - The molecular outflow rate is `8.82 Msun/yr`.
    - The momentum rate is about `15x` the AGN radiation momentum rate, favoring an energy-driven and likely jet-powered outflow.
  - **Position:** a strong low-z AGN-feedback benchmark for molecular outflow energetics

- [2604.27332](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.27332) Minhang Guo et al. `Active Galactic Nucleus Feedback in an Elliptical Galaxy. IV. The Importance of the Jet Wind Coupling`
  - **Question:** why does combined jet and wind feedback suppress star formation more efficiently than either channel alone?
  - **Data:**
    - MACER simulations: `FullFeedback`, `JetOnly`, and `WindOnly`
    - Jet parameters: GRMHD-informed small-scale accretion models
  - **Method:**
    - Compare SFR suppression and kinetic-energy dissipation across feedback modes
    - Identify turbulence generation at wind-jet shear layers
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Time-averaged SFRs are `10^-1`, `10^-2`, and `10^-3 Msun/yr` in `JetOnly`, `WindOnly`, and `FullFeedback`.
    - Energy dissipation efficiency reaches `0.64` in the combined model.
    - Kelvin-Helmholtz instability from wind-jet shear drives stronger turbulent heating.
  - **Position:** an important theory caution because feedback channels cannot always be added linearly in galaxy-formation models

- [2604.24899](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24899) R. Middei et al. `Ultra-thick warm absorbers: Enlarging the parameter space of AGN ionised outflows`
  - **Question:** are ultra-thick warm absorbers a distinct high-column AGN outflow phase?
  - **Data:**
    - Sample: `12` AGN with candidate ultra-thick warm absorbers
    - X-ray facilities: XMM-Newton, NuSTAR, and Swift
  - **Method:**
    - Fit ionized absorption and track variability from days to years
    - Search for Fe K-band UFO connections
  - **Conclusion:**
    - UTWAs have `log(N_H/cm^-2) >= 22.5` and `0.5 <= log(xi/erg cm s^-1) <= 2.5`.
    - All hosts show extreme soft X-ray variability driven by absorber changes.
    - Four of `12` sources also show UFO signatures.
  - **Position:** a useful bridge between classical warm absorbers and nuclear ultra-fast outflows
