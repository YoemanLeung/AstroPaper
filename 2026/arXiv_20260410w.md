## Executive view

This week is unusually good for the overlap between environment, morphology, and early black-hole growth. [2604.07440](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07440) Francesca Rizzo is the cleanest match to your structure-growth interests: a giant `z=3.09` protocluster disc with resolved `[C II]`, a flat `~530 km s^-1` rotation curve, and a direct argument that sustained hot-CGM accretion is more plausible than cold streams.

[2604.08687](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08687) Damien Korber and [2604.06504](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06504) Minghao Yue are the strongest early-BH papers: one finds a fast AGN-driven outflow in a `z=6.64` LRD host, the other confirms a close quasar pair in a massive merger at `z=5.7`.

The rest of the week is still useful for interpretation. [2604.07076](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07076) Maria Koller shows that metallicity gradients at `z~7-10` are diverse rather than uniform, [2604.09347](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09347) C. ## Environment, morphology, and structure growth

- [2604.07440](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07440) Francesca Rizzo `The assembly and fate of a giant disc galaxy in a protocluster at z = 3`
  - **Question:** how can a giant disc already exist in a protocluster at `z=3.09`, and what is its most plausible formation pathway and descendant?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST plus high-resolution ALMA observations of ADF22.1 at `z=3.09`
    - Extended `[C II]` emission over `~30 kpc`
  - **Method:**
    - Measure the rotation curve and decompose it into baryonic and dark-matter components
    - Infer the halo mass, baryon fraction, and angular-momentum partition
    - Compare the system with local giant discs and plausible `z=0` descendants
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The galaxy has a flat outer rotation curve reaching `~530 km s^-1`.
    - The inferred halo mass is `log(M_200/Msun) = 12.9^{+0.4}_{-0.3}`, with baryon-to-halo mass ratio `0.4^{+0.6}_{-0.3}` in units of the cosmic baryon fraction and specific-angular-momentum ratio `j_baryon / j_halo = 1.0^{+0.7}_{-0.5}`.
    - On these dynamical measures, ADF22.1 is essentially indistinguishable from local giant discs, despite living in a dense protocluster and hosting an AGN.
    - Cold-stream accretion cannot explain its size, dynamics, angular momentum, and baryon fraction simultaneously; sustained accretion from a hot CGM via spontaneous or fountain-driven condensation is a better fit.
    - The likely descendants are extreme early-type galaxies with unusually high mass or angular momentum.
  - **Position:** a strong protocluster-dynamics paper because it ties mature disc structure at `z=3` to hot-CGM fueling rather than to the default cold-stream picture

- [2604.06504](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.06504) Minghao Yue `A Close Quasar Pair in a Massive Galaxy Merger at z=5.7`
  - **Question:** what does a close quasar pair inside a massive galaxy merger look like at `z=5.7`, and how common might such systems be at very high redshift?
  - **Data:**
    - High-resolution ALMA observations of J2037-4537
    - Far-infrared continuum and `[C II]` line emission from the two quasar hosts
  - **Method:**
    - Test whether the system is a true quasar pair or a lensed quasar
    - Use host-galaxy kinematics and morphology to diagnose the merger state
    - Infer quasar-pair fraction constraints at `5.5<z<6`
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The far-IR and `[C II]` morphologies show tidal disturbance, which rules out the doubly imaged lens interpretation.
    - Both hosts are massive, `M_dyn >= 10^10 Msun`, and strongly star-forming with `SFR >= 500 Msun yr^-1`.
    - The confirmed system implies a lower limit on the quasar-pair fraction of `F_pair > 1.2%` at `5.5<z<6`, much higher than the quasar-pair fraction at `z<=4`.
    - The system should form a gravitationally bound SMBH binary within `<=2 Gyr`, and the elevated high-z pair fraction may contribute to the gravitational-wave background seen by PTA experiments.
  - **Position:** a high-value merger-side quasar paper because it provides a rare direct high-z dual-accretion case with implications for both SMBH growth and the GW background

- [2604.05024](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.05024) Yunchong Zhang `Winding Back the Clock: Recent Star Formation Histories of Massive Quiescent Galaxies Are Consistent With Their Rapid Number Density Evolution Since z~7`
  - **Question:** are the recent star-formation histories of massive quiescent galaxies consistent with their surprisingly rapid number-density evolution since `z~7`?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRCam photometry from CEERS and PRIMER
    - JWST/NIRSpec/PRISM spectra of massive quiescent galaxies in RUBIES
    - Independent number-density comparison sample from PANORAMIC
  - **Method:**
    - Infer recent star-formation histories with `Prospector` using spectro-photometric fits
    - Compute the time window over which each source would be classified as quiescent
    - Reconstruct the past number density of the quiescent population and compare it with direct observational measurements
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The reconstructed number densities agree well with direct measurements up to `z~7`, which supports the internal self-consistency of the stellar-population modeling.
    - That consistency strengthens the observational case for the rapid build-up of the massive quiescent population.
    - The result also reinforces the known tension between observations and theoretical predictions at `3<z<7`, where the observed quiescent population remains too abundant.
  - **Position:** a useful quenching paper because it cross-checks quiescent-galaxy SFHs against the observed evolution of their number density rather than treating the two separately

## Feedback, metallicity, and reionization analogs

- [2604.08687](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.08687) Damien Korber `GLIMPSED: Direct evidence for a fast AGN-driven outflow from a z=6.64 Little Red Dot host galaxy`
  - **Question:** is there direct evidence for a fast AGN-driven outflow in a `z=6.64` LRD host galaxy?
  - **Data:**
    - Deep JWST/NIRSpec medium-grating spectroscopy of GLIMPSED-329380 behind Abell S1063
    - Spatially resolved host-galaxy and AGN components
  - **Method:**
    - Separate the outflowing host-gas component from the AGN component
    - Measure line widths, abundances, Balmer decrement, and energetics from `[O III]` and `Halpha`
    - Compare the AGN spectral component with LRD phenomenology
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The ionized outflow reaches `FWHM ~ 5500 km s^-1`, a speed seen only in AGN-dominated systems.
    - The narrow lines show little dust attenuation, but the outflowing gas is dusty according to the Balmer decrement.
    - The host has `12 + log(O/H) ~ 7.95`, or `~18%` solar, and is mildly nitrogen enriched with `log(N/O) ~ -0.75`.
    - Despite the extreme velocity, the mass-loading factor is `<0.1` and the kinetic power is only `~10^43 erg s^-1`, so the immediate impact on star formation is probably limited.
    - The AGN component shows several LRD-like traits, including the V-shaped continuum, exponential hydrogen-line wings, forbidden `[Fe II]` lines, a Balmer break, and a broad absorption feature at `~4550 Angstrom`.
  - **Position:** one of the clearest direct-feedback papers for an LRD-like system, because it ties the AGN interpretation to a resolved fast outflow rather than only to continuum shape

- [2604.07076](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07076) Maria Koller `Metal Mayhem at z ~ 7-10: Diversity and Evolution of Gas-Phase Metallicity Gradients`
  - **Question:** how diverse are metallicity gradients in low-metallicity galaxies at `z~7-10`, and what drives that diversity?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec IFU observations of `7` low-metallicity systems at `z=7.2-9.5`
    - `3` low-metallicity satellites associated with two of the sources
  - **Method:**
    - Map radial gas-phase abundance profiles
    - Compare the gradients with interaction state, satellite presence, and AGN indications
    - Measure deviations from the local fundamental metallicity relation
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The galaxies span stellar masses `log(M_*/Msun) ~ 7.8-9.5`, SFRs `log(SFR/Msun yr^-1) ~ 0.5-2.5`, and metallicities `4-15%` solar.
    - The satellites are even more primordial, at `3-4%` solar metallicity and `log(SFR/Msun yr^-1) ~ -0.5` to `-0.9`.
    - The systems lie well below the local FMR, with deviations reaching `Delta FMR ~ -0.9 dex`, which is consistent with strong pristine-gas accretion.
    - The gradients show large scatter, averaging `-0.02 +- 0.04 dex kpc^-1`, with both positive and negative cases.
    - Flat gradients are common in interacting systems with satellites, while one positive gradient suggests central funneling of metal-poor gas; tentative AGN feedback may also flatten some systems.
  - **Position:** a useful early resolved-chemistry paper because it argues against a single canonical metallicity-gradient trend in the first billion years

- [2604.09516](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09516) L. Bonatto `The k-MENDEL sample of local analogs to reionization galaxies. Spectral identification of EELGs and properties of green peas in DESI`
  - **Question:** can DESI isolate a large robust sample of local analogs for reionization-era extreme emission-line galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - DESI spectroscopy of `~16000` EELGs at `0.01 < z < 0.96`
    - SED fits plus `T_e`-based metallicities
  - **Method:**
    - Select EELGs automatically with `k`-means classification
    - Characterize stellar masses, SFRs, metallicities, ionization, and AGN contamination across the sample
    - Compare their locus with local mass-metallicity and main-sequence relations
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The sample spans stellar masses `10^6-10^10 Msun` and SFRs `0.1-100 Msun yr^-1`, extending SDSS-based analog samples to lower metallicities and higher redshifts.
    - The metallicities cover `12 + log(O/H) ~ 7.0-8.5`, and the galaxies lie `0.3-0.5 dex` below local mass-metallicity relations.
    - They sit systematically above the star-forming main sequence, with `sSFR` up to `~100 Gyr^-1`.
    - The most extreme systems reach `O32 ~ 5-60`, similar to confirmed LyC emitters, while only `~6%` show AGN-like signatures.
    - The large metallicity scatter points to massive metal-poor inflows plus strong feedback rather than to simple equilibrium bathtub evolution.
  - **Position:** a useful local-analog paper because it provides a statistically large nearby comparison set for reionization-era low-metallicity starbursts and LyC-leaker physics

## LRDs and quenched systems

- [2604.09177](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09177) Lucy R. Ivey `The Cliff: A Metal-Poor Little Red Dot Hosting an Overmassive Black Hole at z = 3.55`
  - **Question:** what does a metal-poor LRD hosting an overmassive black hole at `z=3.55` imply for seed masses and BH growth pathways?
  - **Data:**
    - High-resolution JWST/NIRSpec IFU observations of The Cliff from RUBIES
    - Rest-frame optical emission-line coverage
  - **Method:**
    - Use narrow-line ratios and line non-detections to infer the gas metallicity
    - Compare the source with BH-growth simulations tuned to early overmassive systems
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The system is metal poor, with `Z = 0.017 +- 0.004 Zsun`, supported by the low narrow-line `[O III]/Hbeta` ratio and the non-detection of `[O II]` and `[N II]`.
    - Simulations can reproduce the overmassive BH only with high seed masses of `10^4-10^5 Msun`.
    - Those successful systems are as rare in the simulation volume as The Cliff is in the RUBIES volume, which emphasizes how unusual the source is.
  - **Position:** a useful LRD-growth paper because it links the unusually low gas metallicity directly to the kind of high-seed-mass scenarios needed to reproduce the source

- [2604.09347](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09347) C. D'Eugenio `A first [CII] view of high-z quiescent galaxies`
  - **Question:** what does `[C II]` emission look like in high-z quiescent galaxies, and what does it reveal about their residual ISM?
  - **Data:**
    - ALMA `[C II] 158 um` detections or upper limits for `5` massive quenched galaxies at `2<z<4.7`
    - Dust-continuum measurements plus JWST/MIRI and NIRCam imaging
  - **Method:**
    - Convert `[C II]` and dust continuum into gas fractions and dust temperatures
    - Compare the results with a first empirical calibration of `alpha_[CII]`
    - Inspect the stellar and dust morphologies for interaction signatures
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The inferred molecular-gas fractions span an extreme range of `0.1%-25%` if standard `alpha_[CII]` holds.
    - Dust temperatures reach `~40-50 K` in two galaxies, higher than expected from lower-redshift quiescent systems.
    - Some galaxies show strong `[C II]` deficits down to `[C II]/L_IR ~ 2 x 10^-4`, typical of ULIRGs, which implies extra dust-heating mechanisms beyond ordinary stellar heating.
    - JWST plus ALMA reveal disturbed stellar morphologies and dust or gas offsets, supporting an interaction- or merger-driven origin akin to local post-starburst systems.
  - **Position:** a high-value quiescent-galaxy paper because it shows that residual gas in high-z quenched systems is diverse, warm, and often dynamically disturbed

- [2604.07138](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07138) Pierluigi Rinaldi `The Way We Tally Becomes the Tale: the Impact of Selection Strategies on the Inferred Evolution of Little Red Dots Across Cosmic Time`
  - **Question:** how much of the inferred cosmic evolution of LRDs is driven by photometric selection choices rather than by the intrinsic population itself?
  - **Data:**
    - JADES GOODS-N and GOODS-S imaging over `~349.6 arcmin^2`
    - Photometric census of `412` LRDs across `z~2-11`
  - **Method:**
    - Explore a broad range of optical redness instead of only extreme-red cuts
    - Enforce compactness and visual inspection for robustness
    - Recompute luminosity functions and number-density evolution under different selections
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The classic extreme-red cuts isolate only a minority of the LRD population, `<=25%`, while most systems occupy a broader and less explored color space.
    - Current UV and optical luminosity functions and number-density trends are therefore strongly selection-biased.
    - Spectroscopically confirmed LRDs span a continuous range of spectral shapes, consistent with varying AGN and host-galaxy contributions rather than a single template.
  - **Position:** a strong demographic sanity-check paper for the LRD literature because it shows that current evolution claims depend heavily on how the class is defined
