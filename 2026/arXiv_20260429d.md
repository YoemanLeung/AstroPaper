## Executive view

The 2026-04-29 batch is strongest in environment and baryon-cycle work. The best profile match is [2604.25632](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25632) Qiong Li, which uses JWST CEERS and JADES galaxies at `z=4-10` to show that overdense regions are already more metal-rich and have higher star-formation-rate density during the epoch of reionization. It is the day's most direct bridge between high-z environment and early chemical enrichment.

The best quasar-CGM paper is [2604.25127](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25127) Shiwu Zhang. It uses KCWI observations of `85` quasars at `z=2-3` to compare Lya nebulae around unobscured and obscured quasars. The result favors an evolutionary AGN picture over a purely orientation-based unified model because unobscured quasars have more extended, asymmetric, and kinematically declining nebulae.

The cosmic-web and missing-baryon side is also coherent. [2604.24852](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24852) Jade Paste studies gas accretion from filaments onto clusters in TNG300, [2604.24863](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24863) R. Seppi forward-models X-GAP group observations against FLAMINGO feedback models, and [2604.25828](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25828) Shion Andrew tests whether FRB dispersion is an unbiased large-scale matter tracer. Together they connect cluster outskirts, group hot gas, and line-of-sight electron columns.

For AGN feedback and black-hole demographics, [2604.24899](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24899) R. Middei defines ultra-thick warm absorbers as a rare high-column phase of ionized AGN outflows, while [2604.24892](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24892) Oscar Veenema uses JWST/NIRSpec and MIRI/MRS to separate AGN outflow and star-forming disk kinematics in NGC 7582. The BLR papers [2604.24901](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24901) Lizvette Villafana and [2604.25163](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25163) Jiancheng Wu are lower priority but useful for mass-systematics context.

## High-z enrichment and quasar CGM

- [2604.25632](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25632) Qiong Li et al. `Environmental dependence of the Mass-Metallicity Star Formation Relations at z=4-10 with JWST`
  - **Question:** does large-scale environment already affect chemical enrichment and the mass-metallicity-SFR relation during the epoch of reionization?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/CEERS and JWST/JADES: deep imaging and spectroscopy
    - Galaxy sample: `225` galaxies with stellar masses, SFRs, and gas-phase metallicities
    - Redshift range: `z=4-10`
    - Environment metric: projected fifth-nearest-neighbor surface density `Sigma_5` within `Delta z=+/-0.25`
  - **Method:**
    - Measure the mass-metallicity relation in dense and field environments
    - Add SFR to test environmental offsets in the mass-metallicity-SFR relation
    - Compare high-z galaxies with the local `T_e`-based fundamental metallicity relation
    - Test residual trends with NIRCam effective radius and mass-weighted stellar age
  - **Conclusion:**
    - At `4.5<z<7`, dense-region galaxies are more metal-rich at fixed stellar mass by about `0.1-0.2 dex`.
    - Including SFR increases the environmental separation, consistent with more efficient enrichment in overdense regions.
    - The full high-z sample lies about `0.2-0.3 dex` below the local `T_e`-based FMR, with a smaller deficit in overdense regions.
    - The SFR density in overdense regions at `z~6-9` is higher by a factor of `2-3`.
    - The age-metallicity relation is positive in both environments and steeper in the field.
  - **Position:** the day's core high-z galaxy result because it links environment, early metal enrichment, and accelerated star formation in the same JWST sample

- [2604.25127](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25127) Shiwu Zhang et al. `Constraining the nature of active galactic nuclei through circumgalactic Lya emission at z=2-3`
  - **Question:** do Lya nebulae around obscured and unobscured quasars support an orientation-only AGN unified model or an evolutionary scenario?
  - **Data:**
    - Keck/KCWI: Lya nebulae around `59` unobscured and `26` obscured quasars
    - Redshift range: `z=2-3`
    - Observables: nebular symmetry, scale length, and velocity-dispersion radial profiles
  - **Method:**
    - Compare Lya surface-brightness morphology between obscured and unobscured quasars
    - Measure asymmetry with the `a_w` symmetry parameter
    - Fit nebular scale lengths and velocity-dispersion profile slopes
    - Interpret the trends against orientation-based and evolutionary AGN pictures
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Unobscured-quasar nebulae are less symmetric, with `a_w=0.2-0.6`, than obscured-quasar nebulae with `a_w=0.6-0.8`.
    - Unobscured-quasar nebulae are more extended, with scale length `r_h=10.7 +/- 0.5 kpc`, compared with `6.6-7.7 kpc` for obscured quasars.
    - Unobscured quasars show steeply declining velocity-dispersion profiles with slope `-4.3 +/- 0.4 km/s/kpc`.
    - Obscured quasars show much flatter slopes, `-0.2 +/- 0.7` and `-0.6 +/- 0.4 km/s/kpc`.
    - The nebular trends favor an evolutionary scenario in which feedback redistributes gas during a transition from obscured to unobscured phases.
  - **Position:** a high-priority quasar-CGM result because it uses extended Lya emission to test the physical nature of AGN obscuration at cosmic noon

- [2604.24836](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24836) Daniel DeFelippis et al. `The Thermodynamic and Kinematic Evolution of Circumgalactic Gas around z=1 in the IllustrisTNG model`
  - **Question:** how quickly does multiphase CGM gas around `z=1` galaxies change its thermodynamic and kinematic state?
  - **Data:**
    - IllustrisTNG/TNG100: high-cadence subregion output
    - Sample: six galaxies in `10^11.5-10^12 Msun` halos at `z=1`
    - Time sampling: about `2 Myr`
    - Tracers: Monte Carlo particles following CGM gas in a Lagrangian frame
  - **Method:**
    - Track CGM gas forward in time by tracer particles
    - Measure changes in temperature, density, radius, and kinematic quantities
    - Compare gas in ion-like temperature and density regimes for Mg II, C IV, and O VI analogues
  - **Conclusion:**
    - CGM gas mixes rapidly between temperature and density phases.
    - Within about `500 Myr`, gas evolves into cold `T~10^4 K` and warm-hot `T~10^5.5 K` phases at small and large radii.
    - Kinematic autocorrelations require about `400 Myr` to decorrelate from initial values, giving a feedback reprocessing timescale.
    - O VI-like gas remains in its phase for hundreds of Myr longer than Mg II-like or C IV-like gas.
  - **Position:** useful for interpreting CGM absorption because different ions can trace gas in different evolutionary states even when they are spatially close

## Cosmic web and hot baryons

- [2604.24852](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24852) Jade Paste et al. `Cosmic gas accretion from filaments onto galaxy clusters using the IllustrisTNG simulation`
  - **Question:** how do cosmic-web filaments control gas accretion, thermalization, and velocity structure around galaxy clusters?
  - **Data:**
    - IllustrisTNG-300: `415` galaxy clusters at `z=0`
    - Gas profiles: temperature, entropy, density, pressure, and radial velocity
    - Geometry: isotropic cluster profiles compared with gas selected in filaments only
  - **Method:**
    - Identify filament-associated gas around clusters
    - Compare isotropic and anisotropic radial gas-property profiles
    - Measure radial velocity distributions in cluster outskirts and environments
    - Separate regimes at `1-2 R_200` and `2-4 R_200`
  - **Conclusion:**
    - At `2-4 R_200`, fast infalling warm gas enters the warm-hot circumcluster medium along filaments.
    - Filaments remain colder than their surroundings because they thermalize slowly and generate transverse temperature gradients.
    - At `1-2 R_200`, filament gas enters the hot intracluster medium with a strong tangential velocity gradient.
    - Warm gas preferentially penetrates clusters from filaments, while hot gas is preferentially ejected beyond them.
    - Strong accretion shocks around filaments are not found, supporting slow thermalization of filament gas.
  - **Position:** a strong cosmic-web baryon paper because it gives a geometric and thermodynamic picture of how filament gas enters cluster environments

- [2604.24863](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24863) R. Seppi et al. `Bound or blown: the fate of hot gas in galaxy groups`
  - **Question:** which AGN feedback strengths reproduce the observed hot-gas content and thermodynamic scaling relations of galaxy groups?
  - **Data:**
    - XMM-Newton Group AGN Project: X-GAP group sample
    - FLAMINGO simulations: hydrodynamical models spanning multiple AGN feedback prescriptions
    - Observables: `L-T`, `M_gas-T`, group counts, mean temperature, and velocity dispersion
  - **Method:**
    - Forward-model the X-GAP selection function, detection effects, and observational systematics
    - Generate end-to-end XMM-Newton mock observations
    - Analyze simulations and data in the same observable space
    - Use covariance among multiple observables to discriminate feedback prescriptions
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Scaling-relation normalization is the strongest discriminator between feedback models.
    - Cosmic variance causes `>20%` fluctuations in detected group counts, making counts alone weak.
    - Intermediate feedback gives the best agreement with X-GAP.
    - The `fgas-2sigma` model has the lowest tension, `0.8 sigma`.
    - The most extreme ejective feedback model, `fgas-8sigma`, is ruled out at `>4 sigma`.
  - **Position:** a hot-baryon calibration result because it narrows viable AGN feedback strengths in low-mass halos where baryon retention is most uncertain

- [2604.25828](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25828) Shion Andrew et al. `Fast radio burst dispersion is an unbiased tracer of matter on large scales`
  - **Question:** can FRB dispersion measures trace large-scale matter without strong bias from foreground structure or astrophysical selection?
  - **Data:**
    - FRB dispersion-measure measurements used as line-of-sight electron-column probes
    - Large-scale matter tracers and simulations for bias tests
    - Focus: matter correlations on large scales
  - **Method:**
    - Test whether FRB dispersion follows matter fluctuations on large scales
    - Quantify possible biases from source environments, propagation, and foreground structure
    - Compare measured or simulated dispersion-matter correlations with unbiased-tracer expectations
  - **Conclusion:**
    - FRB dispersion is consistent with an unbiased matter tracer on large scales.
    - The result supports using FRB samples for cosmic-web baryon mapping.
    - The paper complements direct multi-tracer FRB cross-correlation work by testing the underlying bias assumption.
  - **Position:** important for IGM/LSS methodology because it strengthens the physical basis for using FRB dispersion in missing-baryon and feedback measurements

## AGN outflows and black-hole systematics

- [2604.24899](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24899) R. Middei et al. `Ultra-thick warm absorbers: Enlarging the parameter space of AGN ionised outflows`
  - **Question:** do some local AGN host a rare, high-column, moderately ionized absorber phase distinct from standard warm absorbers and disk winds?
  - **Data:**
    - AGN sample: `12` sources with candidate ultra-thick warm absorbers
    - X-ray facilities: XMM-Newton, NuSTAR, and Swift
    - Detailed analysis subset: four of the twelve sources
  - **Method:**
    - Define ultra-thick warm absorbers by high column density and ionization state
    - Fit archival and unpublished X-ray spectra
    - Track absorber variability from days to years
    - Search for connections with Fe K-band ultra-fast outflow signatures
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Ultra-thick warm absorbers have `log(N_H/cm^-2) >= 22.5` and `0.5 <= log(xi/erg cm s^-1) <= 2.5`.
    - All hosts show extreme soft X-ray variability, in some cases up to an order of magnitude.
    - Variability is primarily driven by changes in the absorbing gas.
    - Four of the `12` sources also show ultra-fast outflow signatures in the Fe K band.
  - **Position:** a useful AGN-feedback paper because it identifies a high-column ionized absorber phase that may connect classical warm absorbers with nuclear ultra-fast outflows

- [2604.24892](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24892) Oscar Veenema et al. `Decoupling the AGN outflow and star-forming disk kinematics in the nuclear region of NGC 7582 with JWST NIRSpec and MIRI/MRS`
  - **Question:** can JWST integral-field spectroscopy cleanly separate AGN-driven outflow kinematics from star-forming disk rotation in a nearby Seyfert nucleus?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec and JWST/MIRI/MRS: integral-field spectroscopy of NGC 7582
    - Target: nearby Seyfert 2 galaxy with circumnuclear star-forming disk and biconical ionized outflow
    - Emission lines: ionization potentials from about `8-126 eV`
  - **Method:**
    - Fit Gaussian line profiles across ionic species
    - Use ionization potential to separate disk-dominated and outflow-dominated tracers
    - Fit a thin inclined disk plus one-dimensional outflow model for intermediate-ionization lines
    - Test whether outflowing gas can exceed the local escape velocity
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Low-ionization lines with ionization potential `<=20 eV` trace ordered disk rotation at position angle `-12 +/- 3 deg`.
    - High-ionization lines with ionization potential `>=35 eV` follow the outflow at position angle `54 +/- 10 deg`.
    - Outflowing gas has velocity dispersion `119 +/- 13 km/s`, above the disk value `78 +/- 11 km/s`.
    - The outflow is consistent with a hollow bicone that can accelerate gas beyond the local escape velocity.
    - The ionization-cone opening angle does not depend on ionization potential, implying largely unobscured polar torus regions.
  - **Position:** a local benchmark for resolved AGN-feedback diagnostics because it shows how JWST can decouple overlapping disk and outflow kinematics

- [2604.25543](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25543) Muhammad Luqman Hakeem Musa et al. `Compton-thick AGN Characterisation in a Multi-wavelength Context: Insights from the 70-Month SWIFT/BAT Catalogue`
  - **Question:** which multiwavelength observables distinguish Compton-thick AGN from less obscured AGN when X-ray selection is incomplete?
  - **Data:**
    - Swift/BAT 70-month catalogue: `243` AGN, including `26` Compton-thick and `217` non-Compton-thick sources
    - Multiwavelength data: radio, infrared, optical, and X-ray measurements
    - Radio survey: VLASS `2-3 GHz`
  - **Method:**
    - Compare radio luminosities, WISE colors, BPT classifications, and Eddington ratios
    - Use principal component analysis to identify dominant variance drivers
    - Test machine-learning classification using multiwavelength observables
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Compton-thick AGN show slightly higher mean `2-3 GHz` luminosities and redder `W3-W4` colors.
    - Most Compton-thick AGN fall outside standard WISE selection wedges, limiting mid-IR-only selection.
    - Compton-thick AGN favor significantly higher Eddington ratios.
    - A machine-learning classifier reaches recall `0.80` using intrinsic X-ray luminosity, `[O I] 6300`, Halpha luminosity, and `W2-W3` color.
    - The results favor high obscuration and accretion rates over a simple orientation-only explanation.
  - **Position:** useful for obscured-AGN census work because future deep surveys will need non-X-ray checks for heavily buried growth phases
