## Executive view

The 2026-05-04 batch has one clear read-first paper for the profile: [2605.00763](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00763) Romain A. Meyer. It uses JWST/NIRSpec IFU spectroscopy to identify `~2 x 10^8 Msun` overmassive black holes in two ultra-luminous Lyman-alpha emitters at `z~6.6`. The combination of broad Balmer lines, high black-hole-to-stellar-mass ratios, double-peaked Lyman-alpha, and inferred remnant ionized bubbles makes it directly relevant to high-z AGN, reionization topology, and Lyman-alpha visibility.

The second strongest profile match is [2605.00659](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00659) C. R. Carvajal-Bohorquez, which releases the final NIKA2 1.2 mm and 2 mm COSMOS catalogs. The catalog contains `323` millimeter sources at `>=80%` purity, has multiwavelength identifications for `~89%`, peaks at `z=2.8+/-0.1`, and includes `66` galaxies at `z>4`. It is a useful DSFG and obscured-SFR anchor for COSMOS-based high-z work.

The AGN and gas-physics support papers are also worth tracking. [2605.00092](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00092) David Izquierdo-Villalba compares LISA massive-black-hole-binary predictions across about `20` models and shows that seeding, resolution, and dynamical delays dominate event-rate uncertainty. [2605.00822](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00822) Gibson B. Bowling uses JWST/HST imaging to separate point-source and host light in `36` NIRCam-selected AGN candidates. [2605.00563](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00563) M. Fournier uses AthenaPK tracer simulations to show how magnetic fields change cold-gas condensation in a Perseus-like cool-core cluster.

## High-z AGN, reionization, and dusty galaxies

- [2605.00763](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00763) Romain A. Meyer et al. `Life After the Quasar: Overmassive Black Holes and Remnant Ionised Bubbles in and Around Two z~6.6 Galaxies`
  - **Question:** can rare double-peaked Lyman-alpha emitters at `z~6.6` be recently faded quasars with remnant ionized bubbles?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRSpec IFU: G235M/F170LP and G395M/F290LP spectra covering rest-frame `2000-7000 Angstrom` for COLA1 and NEPLA4
    - Line diagnostics: Hbeta+[O III] and Halpha+[N II] complexes extracted from the central unresolved region
    - Lyman-alpha context: ultra-luminous Lyman-alpha emitters with rare double-peaked profiles from prior narrow-band and spectroscopic work
    - Ancillary photometry: HEROES/Subaru-HSC and JWST constraints used in stellar-population fits
  - **Method:**
    - Fit narrow, outflow, and broad Balmer components in the NIRSpec spectra
    - Estimate black-hole masses from broad Halpha and Hbeta virial relations
    - Fit stellar populations and star-formation histories after masking AGN-sensitive spectral regions
    - Model ionized-bubble requirements and residual neutral fractions needed for blue-peak Lyman-alpha transmission
    - Compare quasar and double-peaked-LAE number densities to infer duty-cycle limits
  - **Conclusion:**
    - COLA1 and NEPLA4 host broad Balmer components with FWHM `3947+/-263 km/s` and `4114+/-305 km/s`, supporting AGN broad-line regions.
    - The inferred Halpha black-hole masses are `log10(M_BH/Msun)=8.28+/-0.05` and `8.24+/-0.06`, about `2 x 10^8 Msun`.
    - The black-hole-to-stellar-mass ratios are `0.22` and `0.10`, exceeding the local relation by factors of about `400-800`.
    - Current Eddington ratios are low, `lambda_Edd~0.05` and `0.04`, while stellar ages are `<50 Myr`, inconsistent with steady Eddington growth over the full history.
    - The galaxy ionizing output alone leaves residual neutral fractions `14-36x` too high for the observed blue Lyman-alpha peaks; a recent quasar phase can supply the required photoionization.
    - The remnant-bubble interpretation requires the last quasar episode to have ended within `<1 Myr`.
    - The inferred quasar duty cycle is `0.11 x 10^-3 < f_duty < 2.4 x 10^-3`, corresponding to `0.09-1.84 Myr` average quasar lifetimes at `z=6.6`.
  - **Position:** the day's highest-priority paper because it links JWST high-z broad-line AGN, Lyman-alpha transmission, ionized bubbles, and episodic black-hole growth during reionization

- [2605.00659](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00659) C. R. Carvajal-Bohorquez et al. `The NIKA2 Cosmological Legacy Survey in COSMOS: Final 1.2mm and 2mm source catalogs and redshift distribution of dusty star-forming galaxies`
  - **Question:** what final 1.2 mm and 2 mm source catalog and redshift distribution does N2CLS provide for COSMOS dusty star-forming galaxies?
  - **Data:**
    - NIKA2/IRAM 30 m: COSMOS observations from February 2018 to January 2023, `877` scans, and `192.81 hr` observing time
    - Survey area and depth: `~1070 arcmin^2`, median noise `315 uJy` at `1.2 mm` and `91 uJy` at `2 mm`
    - Beam and map properties: FWHM `11.6 arcsec` at `1.2 mm` and `18 arcsec` at `2 mm`
    - Counterpart data: NOEMA, ALMA, VLA, MeerKAT, AS2COSMOS, A3COSMOS, COSMOSWeb, COSMOS2020, and other COSMOS catalogs
  - **Method:**
    - Build matched-filtered NIKA2 maps and blind source catalogs at both wavelengths
    - Estimate catalog purity using `117` SIDES-based simulated observations plus null catalogs
    - Set a detection threshold of `S/N=3.9`, corresponding to `>=80%` purity in both bands
    - Correct fluxes for filtering and flux boosting using simulations
    - Cross-match 1.2 mm and 2 mm detections into a master catalog and identify multiwavelength counterparts
    - Compare observed redshift distributions with SIDES, SHARK, FLAMINGO, and other galaxy-evolution models
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The master catalog contains `323` sources at `>=80%` purity: `104` in both bands, `197` only at `1.2 mm`, and `22` only at `2 mm`.
    - Multiwavelength identifications are secured for `~89%` of the sample.
    - The `1.2 mm` redshift distribution peaks at `z=2.8+/-0.1`, matching the epoch of peak cosmic star formation.
    - The catalog contains `66` galaxies at `z>4`, including `19` with spectroscopic redshifts.
    - SIDES matches the observed redshift distribution well, while four other galaxy-evolution models are statistically inconsistent with the data.
    - `25%` of N2CLS sources lack a SCUBA2 `850 um` counterpart, so homogeneous NIKA2 coverage recovers DSFGs missed by the non-uniform SCUBA2 map.
  - **Position:** a high-value COSMOS legacy dataset for obscured high-z galaxy evolution and for calibrating DSFG redshift distributions against simulations

## Black-hole growth and AGN-host coevolution

- [2605.00092](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00092) David Izquierdo-Villalba et al. `The LISA Astrophysics MBHcatalogues Project: A comparison of predictions of simulated massive black hole binaries`
  - **Question:** how uncertain are LISA massive-black-hole-binary merger predictions across current galaxy-formation models?
  - **Data:**
    - Model suite: about `20` semi-analytical models and cosmological simulations
    - Techniques: semi-analytical catalogs plus hydrodynamical simulations including FLARES, Horizon-AGN, Illustris, TNG, Eagle, Simba, Romulus25, NewHorizon, Obelisk, Renaissance, and others
    - LISA calculation: four-year mission duration with SNRs computed using `lisabeta`, PhenomXHM waveforms, and the SciRDv1 LISA noise curve
  - **Method:**
    - Compare massive-black-hole mass functions, black-hole-to-stellar-mass relations, AGN luminosity functions, merger rates, and binary properties
    - Apply dynamical-friction delay prescriptions to connect model merger outputs to LISA-relevant coalescences
    - Bin the redshift and mass parameter space and draw merger populations for a four-year LISA observing window
    - Compute averaged SNRs over sky position, inclination, phase, and polarization realizations
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Model predictions agree better at `z<=1`, where models are observationally calibrated, but diverge strongly at `z>=4-5` and for `M_BH<=10^7 Msun` or `M_star<=10^10 Msun`.
    - Predicted LISA detections over a four-year mission range from `<=1` to thousands, dominated by seeding prescriptions, resolution, and dynamical treatment.
    - Large-volume lower-resolution simulations often miss dwarf-galaxy massive-black-hole mergers, reducing the predicted LISA event rate.
    - Semi-analytical models with light and heavy seeds forming at `z~10` tend to predict higher merger rates because they include many low-mass binaries in LISA's sensitive mass range.
    - Dynamical-friction delays reduce merger rates most strongly at high redshift and for low-mass massive black holes.
    - Several models still predict a few detectable massive-black-hole-binary mergers per year.
    - Waveforms for intermediate mass ratios `10^-3<q<10^-1` are needed because model binaries span several orders of magnitude in mass ratio.
  - **Position:** a strategic AGN/black-hole-growth theory paper because LISA event rates will constrain early massive-black-hole seeding and low-mass host assembly

- [2605.00822](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00822) Gibson B. Bowling et al. `PEARLS: Two Distinct Populations of AGN Hosts Moving Between Star Formation and Quiescence`
  - **Question:** what host-galaxy star-formation states are selected by JWST/NIRCam point-source AGN morphology?
  - **Data:**
    - JWST/NIRCam: North Ecliptic Pole Time Domain Field imaging in multiple filters, including the NIRCam-selected compact point-source sample
    - HST: ACS and WFC3 imaging, including F275W, F435W, and F606W support
    - Sample: `36` NIRCam-selected AGN candidates from an initial compact-point-source catalog
    - Ancillary AGN data: Chandra X-ray, VLA radio, MMT/Binospec redshifts, and comparison X-ray/radio AGN catalogs
  - **Method:**
    - Use GALFIT to model one point source plus two Sersic components in each object
    - Subtract point-source light to isolate host-galaxy fluxes
    - Fit host SEDs with CIGALE using HST+JWST photometry and spectroscopic or photometric redshifts
    - Estimate SFR, stellar mass, sSFR, and AGN fraction
    - Compare offsets from the Popesso et al. star-forming main sequence with X-ray- and radio-selected AGN samples
  - **Conclusion:**
    - `13/36` objects have X-ray counterparts, `11/36` have radio counterparts, and `6/36` have both.
    - The host sample separates into a `bridge` population and a `branch` population in star-formation space.
    - The bridge objects occupy the region between radio-selected and X-ray-selected AGN host SFRs and often have early-type morphologies with compact bright cores.
    - The branch contains `22/36` galaxies above `Delta SFMS=-1`; these have stronger point sources and often X-ray or radio detections.
    - Branch galaxies show a positive relation between `Delta SFMS` and AGN fraction, consistent with current or recent interaction between AGN activity and host SFR.
    - Star-forming behavior shows no clear preference with stellar mass or redshift, so AGN activity is the more likely separator than host mass alone.
  - **Position:** a practical JWST-era AGN-host paper because point-source subtraction and host SED fitting are required before interpreting AGN feedback or coevolution trends

## Gas physics, clusters, and galaxy dynamics

- [2605.00563](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00563) M. Fournier et al. `XMAGNET -- Stir before serving: a Lagrangian perspective on mixing-driven condensation in the intracluster medium`
  - **Question:** how do magnetic fields alter the thermodynamic path from hot ICM to cold gas in a self-regulated cool-core cluster?
  - **Data:**
    - Simulation framework: AthenaPK hydrodynamical and MHD simulations of an idealized Perseus-like cool-core cluster
    - Feedback model: self-regulated AGN feedback with thermal, kinetic, and magnetic energy injection
    - Tracers: Lagrangian tracer particles following hot gas over a `300 Myr` lookback window before condensation
    - Time sampling: about `600` snapshots separated by `1 Myr`
  - **Method:**
    - Identify tracers transitioning from hot gas to cold phase
    - Reconstruct entropy, vorticity, compressive fraction, turbulent Mach number, and magnetic-energy histories
    - Compare hydro and MHD cooling pathways
    - Follow individual MHD cold structures, including a massive filament and two ballistic clouds
    - Decompose acceleration terms during post-condensation infall
  - **Conclusion:**
    - In both runs, most hot-to-cold transition tracers grow by mixing onto low-entropy seeds rather than by direct radiative cooling.
    - In the hydro run, low-entropy seeds form mainly at rising AGN-cavity edges.
    - In the MHD run, cold fragments are extracted from infalling filaments by AGN jets, uplifted into the ICM, and then seed further condensation.
    - Hydro tracers diverge from the background only `~30 Myr` before cooling, while MHD tracers diverge `~150 Myr` before condensation.
    - The MHD cooling transition has lower turbulent Mach number, `M_turb~0.28`, than the hydro transition, `M_turb~0.4`.
    - Magnetic tension can counteract up to `~100%` of gravitational acceleration during the final `20-30 kpc` of infall for ballistic clouds.
  - **Position:** a useful low-z cluster-gas analogue for CGM/ICM multiphase physics because it makes magnetic fields central to cold-gas assembly and AGN-regulated precipitation

- [2605.00349](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00349) Yuan Wang et al. `A Universal Dance of Galactic Disks: Ubiquitous Precession and Its Implications`
  - **Question:** can cosmological tidal torques produce ubiquitous disk precession, warps, and satellite alignments?
  - **Data:**
    - Simulations: IllustrisTNG Milky Way-like galaxy sample
    - Tracers: stellar disk spin orientation, cold-gas warps, satellite distributions, central-satellite alignment, and cold gas streams
    - Redshift range: cosmic evolution of disk spin orientation, with strong effects at `z>1` and persistent effects at `z~0`
  - **Method:**
    - Measure disk spin precession rates over disk-evolution intervals
    - Compare precession direction with tidal torque from asymmetric matter within and around the halo
    - Quantify cold-gas warp angle and its orientation relative to precession
    - Stack satellites around warped disks and measure satellite spin-position alignment
    - Use analytic torque estimates to interpret the simulation trends
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Disk precession is driven by external tidal torque from anisotropic matter within about `30 kpc`.
    - Precession is strongest at `z>1`, when accretion and surrounding asymmetry are high, but remains significant at `z~0`.
    - The Milky Way is predicted to precess at `~3-10 deg/Gyr` from its observed warp.
    - Disk precession correlates with cold-gas warp strength and can explain observed warp-satellite alignments.
    - Central-galaxy tidal torque can precess satellite disks and orient them toward the central galaxy.
    - Torque-regulated precession of accreted cold gas streams affects disk growth and morphology.
  - **Position:** a useful LSS/galaxy-dynamics paper because it links satellite alignments, warps, and cold accretion to the same local tidal-torque framework

## Lower-priority but useful references

- [2605.00122](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00122) Ryan J. Cooke et al. `Towards a measurement of the primordial helium isotope ratio`
  - **Question:** can metastable helium absorption provide a path to a primordial `3He/4He` measurement?
  - **Data:**
    - VLT/CRIRES+: high-resolution near-infrared spectroscopy of metastable helium absorbers toward Theta2A Ori, HD 319718, and Her 36
    - VLT/UVES: optical support spectra for He I* absorber characterization
    - Chemical-evolution models: VICE Milky Way calculations with varied primordial `3He/4He` and stellar-yield scale
  - **Method:**
    - Fit metastable helium isotope absorption with Voigt-profile components
    - Test time variability in the Orion absorber using two epochs separated by `~8 yr`
    - Combine absorber measurements with Milky Way chemical-evolution models
    - Use MCMC to infer the primordial isotope ratio and stellar-yield scale
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The Orion He I* absorber is stable at the level `d log10 N(He I*)/dt <= 7.2 x 10^-4 dex/yr`, or `<0.17%/yr`.
    - New He I* absorbers are identified toward HD 319718 and Her 36.
    - The inferred primordial isotope ratio is `(3He/4He)_P = 1.15(+0.24,-0.21) x 10^-4`, consistent with Standard Model BBN plus CMB baryon density.
    - With a Standard Model prior on the primordial ratio, the stellar-yield scale is `y/Zsun = 2.12(+0.31,-0.29)`.
    - More metal-poor environments and extremely large telescopes could turn this into a model-independent primordial probe.
  - **Position:** lower priority for the core high-z galaxy profile, but useful for future absorption-line cosmochemistry and metal-poor-gas applications

- [2605.00223](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00223) A. Valenzuela Navarro et al. `Extinction law and stellar mass in the Nuclear Bulge from kinematically-selected red clump stars`
  - **Question:** what extinction law and stellar mass are implied by kinematically selected red-clump stars in the Milky Way nuclear bulge?
  - **Data:**
    - Milky Way nuclear-bulge red-clump stars selected by kinematics
    - Near-infrared photometry and reddening information toward the nuclear bulge
    - Extinction structure around the Brick, G0.253+0.016
  - **Method:**
    - Select red-clump stars belonging to the nuclear bulge
    - Build a high-spatial-resolution reddening map
    - Derive extinction ratios and count red-clump stars
    - Scale completeness-corrected red-clump counts to stellar mass
  - **Conclusion:**
    - The total-to-selective extinction ratio is `A_K/E(H-K)=1.259+/-0.074`.
    - The near-infrared extinction ratio is `A_H/A_K=1.794+/-0.046`.
    - The reddening map shows filamentary structure and an extinction gradient over the Brick.
    - The nuclear-bulge stellar mass is `(12.2+/-2.6) x 10^8 Msun`.
  - **Position:** mainly a local Milky Way calibration paper, but relevant as a nearby dense-bulge analogue and extinction-systematics reference

- [2605.00262](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00262) Deidre A. Hunter et al. `Ultra-deep imaging of nearby dwarf irregular galaxies: stellar haloes and disk structure`
  - **Question:** do nearby low-mass dwarf irregular galaxies show stellar halos or extended disk structures in ultra-deep imaging?
  - **Data:**
    - Sample: `10` nearby dwarf irregular galaxies with stellar masses `10^6-6 x 10^7 Msun`
    - Imaging: ultra-deep V-band images and far-UV images as star-formation tracers
    - Structural measurements: ellipse fits as a function of surface brightness
  - **Method:**
    - Fit optical isophotes with ellipses at low surface brightness
    - Compare optical structure with far-UV star-forming regions
    - Stack seven galaxies to test for a faint halo component
    - Measure UBVI colors in outer annuli
  - **Conclusion:**
    - Patchy star formation skews broadband optical isophotes even at low disk surface brightness.
    - Individual galaxies show possible halo hints through ellipse irregularities.
    - The stack of seven galaxies is consistent with a pure exponential profile to `V=32.3 mag/arcsec^2`.
    - The corresponding stellar surface density is `0.0013+/-0.0011 Msun/pc^2`.
    - Outer annulus UBVI colors are consistent with ages of `1-6 Gyr`.
  - **Position:** lower priority for high-z work, but useful for interpreting faint stellar outskirts and internal versus external growth in low-mass galaxies
